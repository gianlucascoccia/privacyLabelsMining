Description

PicTapGo+ is the simple, fun and fast photo editor for iPhone and iPad. Users can crop and rotate photos, apply photo filters, retouch selfies, improve lighting and share to Facebook, Instagram and their IG stories. Create and save your own filter recipes and use our Batch Edit option to quickly edit a group of photos for Poshmark, Ebay or a digital scrapbook. PicTapGo+ lets you stack and combine filters and adjust filter strength to create your own custom style.

Over 50+ free filters included, or upgrade to PicTapGO+ Unlimited and unlock additional filters and vintage film looks inspired by Fuji, Kodak and Agfa. Unlimited subscribers also get access to pro-level features like curves, levels, shadow and highlight tools and vignettes.

From Totally Rad!, the creators of Photoshop tools such as RadLab and PixelSugar and Replichrome for Lightroom, PicTapGo+ is the camera filter app that professional photographers and graphic designers use to touch up their photos and share online. Share your PicTapGo+ photo edits and get featured on our Instagram with #pictapgo_app.

PicTapGo+ Unlimited is an auto-renewing subscription. A payment will be charged to your account at the end of your trial period (if applicable). Your subscription will automatically renew unless auto-renew is turned off in your Settings menu. Your account will be charged for the renewal within 24 hours prior to the end of the current period. You can manage and cancel your subscriptions by going to your account settings after purchase. For more information please see our Terms Of Service and Privacy Statement - http://www.gettotallyrad.com/legal.

What’s New

Oct 28, 2021

Version 2.4.1

Smacking down some bugs from the last couple releases...

[ FIXED ]
- Slider controls would change their value *very* slightly when you lifted your finger, making precise adjustments quite frustrating.
- AutoMask, when used with batch processing, would reuse the mask from the first image for the entire batch, leading to incorrect results for the rest of the batch.
- For some users (on iPads, in particular), the app would crash when scrolling through the filter grid.
- A few random little crashes, caused by esoteric little edge cases, that 99% of you never noticed.

[ IMPROVED ]
- PicTapGo+ no longer requires full access to your camera roll, and is perfectly happy with limited photo library access. Keep all those feet pics safely hidden away while you use your favorite editor. Or whatever. It's none of our business.
- Filter thumbnail scrolling and drawing performance should be greatly improved.
- Touch Tool performance should likewise be improved, and more stable.

[ MISC ]
- PicTapGo+ now requires iOS 14 or later. Apple's developer tools don't provide a way for us to test the app's rendering on iOS 13 or later without a physical device, and there ain't enough of y'all still on iOS 13 for me to justify keeping an old phone around just to support it. So... go update iOS :)
- PicTapGo no longer uses the obviously very misleading verbiage "Subscribe" on the subscribe button, but instead, per Apple's enlightened legal guidance, ALSO tells you that your subscription will automatically renew at the end of the billing period, in case this is your first time ever subscribing to a thing. We are working on fitting our entire Terms Of Use onto that button as well, but have been thus far frustrated by the limits of human vision, and the finite number of pixels available to cram disclaimers and legalese into a single screen of UI, all on a device you can hold in one hand. Please consider very carefully the weighty implications of pressing the big red button that pays our bills before you do so. Perhaps consult your financial advisor, ask for the advice of a few friends. Talk to your priest. Write a list of pros and cons. If you regret your decision, you'll be out like twenty bucks, which is money you could have otherwise used to purchase a genuine OEM cleaning cloth for your phone, so, ya know, it's a big decision.

Update I didn’t think I needed

PicTapGo has been my all time favorite photo editing app for years; I loved it so much that I was skeptical of any need for improvement.

First off, I was appreciative of the fact that I was able to import my existing recipes. Next, my only gripe with the old app was that it could be slow to navigate between albums when selecting a photo to edit, but there were no performance issues here! I also really love the ability to adjust the levels on a photo; it really makes a difference. Then, I discovered the feature I have been waiting for; when you apply a recipe you can see all the filters that were applied and tweak them individually. I was officially sold on the new version 😊.

Obviously the new version steers you towards a subscription. If almost any other free app I use went from free to freemium I would be cranky about it. However, I honestly feel like $20 annually is completely fair for this app so I’m willing to support a company I’ve been a fan of for so many years.
