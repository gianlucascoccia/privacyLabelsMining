Description

Join over three million members getting paid for health actions. More than $7,000,000 has already been paid out. Achievement is one of the top health apps in the app store. Yep, you earn cash by tracking steps - what are you waiting for?!

Backed by some of the top investors in Silicon Valley and driven by a social mission to help drive scientific research forward and make America healthier, we literally pay you cash (via direct deposit, PayPal, gift card, or charity donation) to do healthy things like walking, eating well, and even sleeping! We also provide any consumer with the opportunity to contribute to cutting-edge health research.

“I enjoy having Achievement here to motivate me. I have had back problems for many years. Walking is one of the only ways I keep my back problems under control since the more you move the looser your back becomes and helps blood flow to heal your back. When I have the benefit of making money from keeping myself healthy, I go just a little longer each day." --Kelli C

We pay you to:
• Track steps
• Log food
• Log meditation sessions
• Log sleep
• Log miles biked

Answer surveys or opt into programmatic health programs

Link any of your apps to track healthy activities: Fitbit, Strava, Stop, Breathe, Think. Achievement can also integrate with Apple Health to make it easy to track your progress.

Redeem your points for actual cash.

So far, we’ve paid out over $7,000,000 to our members, and there’s no limit to how much you can earn.

Earn $10 for every 10,000 points. Rewards are paid out within 3-7 business days, and Achievers can redeem cash directly into their PayPal account or bank account of choice.

We also partner with leading health companies to offer you extra opportunities to earn money by contributing to cutting-edge health research (totally optional and must meet study qualifications).

What else can we say? It's literally that easy - we pay you cash to track steps, no strings attached! Over 3 million members are already earning money with us, just install and get started!

Great app, but be sure to learn specifics on how it works.

I’m not deducting a star because it was due to user error, but to get the ONE thing I dislike out of the way first - I’ve been using this app since September 2017 and have FINALLY earned my first award. It took such a long time because I thought this app was using the info from my Fitbit app but it turns out that I have to sync my Fitbit daily or I miss out on the steps earned that day - I usually update it 1-2x a week while I charge my Fitbit. I have Apple Health connected as a backup (which is where a majority of my points have come from), but it’s not accurate (I assume because I don’t own an Apple Watch) so it only registers between 1,000-3,000 steps a day when, at least according to Fitbit, I walk over 10K steps daily. 😔 It’s very frustrating to realize just how many Achievement Points I’ve missed out on these past 9 months 😔

That being said, this app encourages me to exercise more often and to push myself further. Achievement sends weekly points updates via email, and every 10K points can be redeemed for $10 cash through PayPal or RocketMoney. I really love that they do this instead of offering gift cards - I hate being restricted to one store. They also offer additional ways to earn points through surveys and studies. Overall a fantastic app, just make sure you understand all the details to gain the most benefit from it. ♥️

Janahned07@gmail.comJana , 08/06/2020

WCAG accessible, still needs work with accuracy

I really wanted to give this app more stars. I’ve had this app for over a year. Pros:
For the most part, it is screen reader accessible. That means, the app is WCAG compliant. It’s not perfect but it’s much better than most of the pay for walking types of apps. It can sync with many apps and that is a good thing.
Here is a big huge con, though. And this is why I gave it three stars. Even though it syncs with the iPhone your smart phone or Fitbit, it always shows a different step counter. For example: one day I walked 8352 steps according to Fitbit. But somehow the achievement app only recorded 7000. Yesterday I walked to thousand 365 steps. Achievement only says I walked 21 I think the closer I get to the $10 mark, the less points the app is giving me for my exercises and I don’t understand that. It does give you six points for logging water and six points for eating food and apparently only four points for sleeping. That helps. Still, it is definitely misleading. End it doesn’t record these all of the time. I logged into my Fitbit and even though it is always thinking with my Fitbit, it doesn’t record them. And if I’m out of range or something and I login to my Fitbit and log my water intake or something, if achievement doesn’t sync with it on time within a certain timeframe, I don’t get the points either like I said, it is better than any of the other apps that I have tried. But it definitely needs improvement
