Description

Discover & Plan Adventures

Find the perfect campsite, discover secluded swimming holes, or identify good spots before your next hunting trip – with every adventurer in mind, we’re the only tool you need to get outside. 100% free.

◆ Go off the grid
See your GPS location on the map even without Wi-Fi or cell service. While offline, use customized maps, layers, and points to get better insights as you navigate.

◆ Find the perfect campsite with info on:
• Federal campsites nationwide
• Roads and trails
• Cell coverage
• Wildlife maps
• & More

◆ Discover off-roading trails with info on:
• Trail/road types based on your vehicle (ATV, 2WD, 4WD, Dirt Bike)
• Color-coded National Forest and BLM segments with allowed use and open dates
• Terrain slope shading and USGS topo contours
• & More

◆ Scout the best water spots with info on:
• Swimming holes
• Stream flows
• Waterfalls
• & More

◆ Plan your hunt with info on:
• Detailed hunt unit boundaries for all species
• Color-coded public land boundaries
• Wildlife activity - range, concentration, and migration areas for big game animals
• Terrain steepness
• & More

◆ Share and collaborate on your plan with friends.
Don’t let yourself be the planner for the whole group! Share your custom points with other Outly users so that everyone can collaborate on the details of your trip.

◆ Download maps right to your phone.
Save a map area before you head out and then use all the detailed layers once you're off-grid on your next adventure.

Fantastic Offline Navigation

I recently went on a camping trip in Colorado’s Collegiate Peaks and did a lot of hiking and trail running.

I used the web app to pick out some potential dispersed campsites and created points that synced with the mobile app. I downloaded offline maps of the areas I planned to be and they were super helpful to locate campsites and find new trails. From the web app I knew the cell coverage was pretty spotty so I didn’t have to worry about checking for a signal to load a map which was a huge stress relief.

This app has a lot of potential. I’m hoping for more trail coverage. Can’t wait to see what’s next!
