Description

PLAY SMARTER GOLF
TaylorMade’s myRoundPro provides an unprecedented statistical analysis experience, designed to help every golfer better understand, adapt, and improve their game.

With the myRoundPro app, you can track your rounds on the go, enabling you to record your scores, check detailed yardages with built-in GPS, analyze every shot on a map of the course, and begin to identify areas for improvement.

GET IN-DEPTH STATS
Based on the record of your shots and scores, myRoundPro analyzes golfers’ statistics to include new data such as Strokes Gained and classic statistics like fairways hit and greens in regulation—giving the golfer all the data they need to adapt and improve the way they play.

While you play a round of golf, this app uses your location even when the screen is turned off to improve user experience, which can decrease device battery life.

reusgov , 10/05/2021

Absolutely brilliant!!

I used the app for the first time yesterday. I have an apple watch with built-in gps so i was able to just use my watch to record shots and clubs used - the phone was in my bag during the whole round.

Using the watch in-game is pretty straight forward. I'm able to get yardage (thanks to the gps feature which is pretty accurate for the most part), select my club, and record the shot. The app also uses that data to make club suggestions for future holes and rounds - which is very helpful at this stage of my game.

Post-game, I pulled up my phone and the stats were all there. I was able to review the shot distances and where they landed - i found this very useful, especIally the tee shots. I could see whether i missed the fairway left or right for each hole (mostly right for this round - something I'd have to work on :) ).

There’s something to be said about not having to worry about wrestling a piece of paper and pencil just to keep score. With the app being 100% free (at least at the time of this comment), I probably would have just been happy with the stellar score keeping and tracking feature. Everything else is just a bonus. This app is a real contender against all the other expensive paid ones in my opinion. I hope they keep up with the development and update it on a regular basis.

therealseanb , 11/05/2019

The Golf App I’ve Been Looking For

I’ve literally tried them all, but for a stats nerd like me MyGolfPro finally seems to be the app I’ve been looking for. Simple, accurate shot tracking with an abundance of actually USEFUL stats. The visuals of the charts are very clean and easy to read. Inputting data during a round is a breeze.

BIG BONUS: Apple Watch app has FULL functionality without ever needing to open the phone app during a round.

3 suggestions for future releases:
1. When using the Apple Watch during a round it would be nice to be able to open the phone and have it synced with the current data to that point so that I can dial in putting specifics immediately after each hole instead of after the round.

2. A corresponding website would be HUGE. Being able to review stats on my laptop instead of always on the phone would be the icing on the cake.

3. Score integration with GHIN would put this over the top.

Really nice work TaylorMade. Thank you for the incredible, free app!

Cblack3 , 01/21/2019

Bad GPS tool, bad interface, great stats

I love the way they report stats and use the strokes lost metric. It is super helpful in informing where you need to improve your game...

Unfortunately the interface is super lacking. The app is pretty much useless as a general GPS app. It does not passively give yds to points of interest and getting yardage from a spot is clunky. I want yards to layup points and to bunkers, front back of green, etc. When I use the app I use Golfshot gps for my general yardage and score keeping. The only reason I even open this app is for the metrics that can be taken off of your shot placement and performance.

Another glaring issue with the interface is how they handle puts and the green. It often doesn’t let me move the flag after asking me to move the flag. Why not allow you to quickly mark current location as the flag location? It is very difficult to correctly position put points and the flag because of how they position the touch point under your finger a little too much.

All of the interface issues are frustrating. Not sure if I’ll keep up with the app or not. If a competitor provides strokes gained metrics and a better interface I’d probably move. If TaylorMade can work out it’s glaring interface problems they may be able to pull me from golfshot. The lack of updates don’t show promise.
