Description

The battle for world domination begins on your device!

Everybody wants to rule the world! Now you can play the classic game of Hasbro's RISK online.

A big gap to be filled in online

Game is well designed and rating would be five stars except for one fatal flaw! In online, when a player quits and the computer takes over, the computer needs to be more logical and sophisticated. The human opponent had at one point, four continents under full control, I had one (Australia) and the computer had its troops spread over Asia with out control of the full continent. The computer proceeded to consistently attack territories in Asia and corner me in Australia by stacking surrounding territories with high troop counts, while my human opponent was absolutely running away with the game! To make matters worse, the human opponent left some continents with little protection and the computer still didn’t attack one single territory to reduce the bonus troops for the human opponent. Needless to say I lost the match. My issue is I didn’t lose because the human player out strategized me, but rather because the computer player was in la la land with its head up its a-s. Please make the computer in online much more sophisticated so that the games remain competitive and fair when some one quits mid game.

Jorge E.O. , 01/12/2018

Great, but dice is not random

I really enjoyed this game. It looks great and works beautifully, but the dice is not random. The game places an advantage to one player from the beginning, at times sympathy to losing players, and at times purposely preventing players to gain territory on a given turn. I have been playing this game every day for two weeks and it’s a continuous trend. The advantaged player has an easier time taking over territories while not losing many points, if any at all, and this is felt throughout the game. Once in a while justice is served, but the advantage continues. On the other hand a player that is losing, including myself, is given sympathy by winning unbeatable odds. Worst case scenario, one game I attacked the same territory with a 6-2 odd from multiple territories for 4 full turns and lost every battle never obtaining it. Seems like bad luck, but I defended a similar situation, for not as long, and even still questioned the “randomness” of the game. After playing the game for some time you can predict some battles especially if one player is losing or you lose a battle that is clearly in you favor, so you learn to not attack because you will continue to lose for that turn. It seems like the idea is to drag on the game or make it more fun, but it just becomes frustrating. If this algorithmic sympathy/advantage is fixed, the I will re-download and play, but for now I’m done. I’ll stick with the board game.

Developer Response ,

Hi Jorge, apologies for the super late reply - it’s been a while! We'd like to check in on if you’ve tried since 2018?

We haven’t ever had advantages for other players (human and AI alike use the same odds)
We’ve also implemented an alternative to True Random, Balanced Blitz! What this does is increase the probability of high success attacks, and decrease the probability of low success attacks - it really cuts down on the luck factor, so a lot of our community prefer this over True Random.

Some more information can be found here: https://bit.ly/smgdiceodds Our dice simulator can be found at: https://riskodds.com Lastly, if you’d like to review our dice algorithm: https://github.com/smgstudio/risk-dice

If you have any questions/concerns can reach out to us via support ticket, discord, or pretty much any other social channel we've got setup!
