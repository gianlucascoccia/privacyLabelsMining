Description

Discover covered pictures by sloving logic number puzzles based on numbers on grids!

Nonogram is a game to solve logic number puzzles by filling or left blank cells according to numbers at the side of the grid to reveal the hidden pixel picture, as known as Hanjie, Picross, Griddlers, Japanese Crosswords, Paint by Numbers, Pic-a-Pix. A pleasant game to train and exercise your brain, also keep your mind active with the basic rules and logic behind the picture cross puzzle.

You can get a piece of Jigsaw shard in each passed puzzle. You can discover a world of over 3,000 amazing beautiful images with different themes. This is the first nonogram game where you can solve the puzzle and play jigsaw at the same time!

●Massive Themed puzzle packs in the game
●Relax in a special jigsaw puzzles by filling jigsaw pieces to gain beautiful photos
●There are intuitive and effective novice tutorials in the game, easy to learn and quite addictive once you start playing
●Choose the difficulty level from Very Easy, Easy, Medium, Hard or Very hard that best for you and become the expert!
●There are many auxiliary functions in the game, such as return to the previous step, get hints, and reset the game
●Autosave every puzzle, if you stuck you can try another puzzle and come back later
●Challenge different brand-new missions every week and get generous rewards for game items
●Special nonogram pro section will offer puzzles with large size 20x20, 25x25, 30x30, 35x35 to bring unique experience.

Take the challenge and enjoy the endless fun of puzzles!

Worth Keeping

I have downloaded and played most of the Nonogram apps. Some I have kept, some I deleted, some I paid for no ads, some I didn’t. This one is definitely a keeper. durge02 , 09/24/2021

A Favorite, but I have a suggestion

It’s hard for a game to keep my attention for long. Most games i’ll only keep for a week before it falls to the background and gets deleted. This game is one of only two that have managed to keep me interested for many months. I love nonograms and pretty much all of the competing apps that i’ve tried can’t compare. Only, I wish I could play it more. Basically, the reward ratio needs improvement. You pay with a lot of diamonds to play, and only earn a couple for completing. You also earn diamonds on a time based schedule, but the amount it caps out to is only enough to play one or two puzzles towards the end of a chapter when only “Very Hard” pricier puzzles remain. What you will earn a ton of is coins, which are only useful if you have no idea how to complete a nonogram puzzle. I only use my coins for the occasional unnecessary hint to earn diamonds from weekly challenges like “use 2 hints” So really I only use a couple hints a week because I don’t need them. I have amassed over 10,000 coins that have no use for me. I watch as many ads as they will allow to earn diamonds, so they get plenty of ad revenue from me. I’m one of the many people who will never spend real money on in game currency, so they would be better off if a feature was added to purchase diamonds with coins. That way I can play more, and they can make more money off the adds i watch after puzzles rather than making nothing because I cant play anymore.

Kerplunct , 01/19/2021

Fun but has a catch

It’s a fun game, but the catch is you have to pay to keep playing. You spend lots of diamonds to play, you make a couple diamonds when you win so you run out. Ads can give you diamonds but only a few. The more you play, the more diamonds it costs you and the less you make. So you gotta spend real money to keep playing. No thanks.

Edit: developer responded with options to continue playing. Thanks. One of the options, however, is to spend real money. I have never understood spending real money on digital fake monopoly type money. It runs out quickly and then you what? Pay again? I am sure some people spend a lot of money on games like this, but it seems unethical to even charge for that. The best and most quality games I know of either have a one time charge for all the features, or are free and have ads from time to time to get things. With this game, you are either paying real money for Monopoly money (which I won’t do on principle), or spending more time watching ads than actually playing the game. It could be done so much better. All of this on top of the fact that the puzzles are low quality and some have multiple possible solutions.
