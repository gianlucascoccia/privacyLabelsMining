Description

Tinybeans is where parents go! We are your go-to resource for all things parenting, providing users with connection and reassurance through the journey of parenthood and a safe space to store and share memories—all in one app.

We are a secure way to share photos of your family with your loved ones, but also provide you with the daily inspiration, recommendations and connections everyone needs to raise tiny, happy humans.

Unlimited invitees, unlimited storage. Perfect for parents, new moms, grandparents and moms-to-be. No wonder over 4 million users love Tinybeans!!

**Featured on the App Store, NYTimes, MSN, Parents, Fatherly, US Weekly and Forbes***

How to use Tinybeans:
Family photo album. Easily organize all of you your photos and videos.

Stay connected. Loved ones receive daily email updates - no extra effort required!

Photo Editor. Add stickers, text, filters and other cool effects.

Baby milestone tracker. Track your baby’s growth, milestones, and development.

Calendar view. View every moment, baby milestone, and photo by date.

Chart view. See your child’s week-by-week changes.

Flashbacks. See photos in your timeline to bring back fond memories.

Ideas. Get personalized content, daily inspiration, and recommendations based on your child's age, interest and location.

Jimram , 02/17/2018

So perfect, but a couple small caveats.

It’s all truly excellent. My main recommendations so far would be to make it easier to correct errors entered into height and weight measures (as I learned from a very prompt and clear customer service person named Toni, this can be done by swiping left on a given measurement, but this is not intuitive, so some kind of indication on that page would be helpful). Secondly, I recommend adding a similar measurement system for head circumference, since most doctor’s visits include that anyway, and is of higher importance in the age of Zika. Another, more challenging, issue is this: the statistics should be calibrated to take account of where children are relative to due date, not actual date of birth, so as to take into account preemies (and ‘posties’). There is a publicly available set of alternate statistics for preterm babies that can be used, as well as variant benchmarks used by CDC and WHO (the app Growth+ includes two variants for each organization, plus the preterm variant). By using the actual date of birth rather than the estimated due date, the measurement percentiles used by the Tinybeans app end up being highly skewed toward babies born exactly at their due dates. This would seem a major area of opportunity in an otherwise highly evolved, sophisticated, and delightful app!

Developer Response ,

This is great feedback!!! Thanks so much for sharing this with us. We are working on our overall product and this is something we will definitely keep in mind as we go forward. If you have anymore suggestions, we would love to hear. You can always email us at info@tinybeans.com.
