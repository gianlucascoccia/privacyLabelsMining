[Feature]

[Background]
In a time when demons and humans coexisted… Evil spirits from the underworld have begun a quest for power, strength, and dominance. The balance between the two worlds is now in jeopardy.
Fortunately, there's a group of gifted humans who can read the stars and draw amulets. They have the power to connect the two worlds, and even tame the spirits. They are willing to lay everything on the line to maintain peace and order between the two worlds. They are known as Onmyoji.
The gate to this magical world of spirits and beauty will now open for you…

W4LTR0N , 06/23/2019

Top quality

I played other popular games of this type and in comparison, this game has better character models, more characters, they are all unique, have their own animations and voices. The anime is top quality. Even though you see a lot of players building the same teams there is still more opportunity in versatility than other games. You can still use common characters for end game content because they each can serve an important role on your team.

Yeah there is still repetitive gameplay, modes that do not offer much different and many people still using the same teams and for that I am tempted to give the game 4 stars instead of 5 but then when I compare it to other games, this game does it better so it is as good as this kind of thing is going to get. The flaws are in the nature of this type of game, and they all have them.

I would say maybe with a game that seems to have a big budget like this, it could try some story mode that is more interesting than this that feels more like an rpg.

Thekrabking , 08/05/2021

Was fun

Like PVE? Better have the nisch team to be a top contender in PVE ranking. Otherwise any ol team will do to just clear.
Like PVP? Better have the same team of units but faster.
Other than a nice story, lovely graphics and beautiful character design, the gameplay is just a reck. PVE is easy to clear but boring to be good at. You can clear almost anything with any random team but to actually rank high you need to have a very nisch team with as close to the nearly impossible perfect soul set equipped. PVP is even worse anymore. Same 3 units on every team with overloaded kits and the only way to beat it is to use the same team but hope you have faster souls on. How else would you play when you have unblockable crowdcontrol that even the units that “cant be crowd controlled” are susceptible to? Ive walked away from PVP battles for 10+min and came back still in the same duel, still haven’t had a turn. You dont build damage you build speed on the same units as everyone else. In otherwords the only way you can use your favorite unit is if an event comes along and gives a buff to that unit for the event. Otherwise be prepared to run the same team as everybody else and show off your luck in soul stats. The game use to be very fun and offer a bunch of versatility but thats all gone.

Bai_Qian , 10/08/2020

Long time player review

I started this game a few months after it came to global. This game has expanded so much (I’m nearing my 1000th login next month) and I’ve met friends around the world now. Story and game mechanics are well fleshed out. No two teams are the same with the various layers of synergy and composition that makes a single team. The community is lively and helpful.
A little grindy? Maybe. But when you have a partner or mentor run with you it’s a lot easier and faster. Pvp is difficult when first start off but get involved with guilds and members that frequently duel and you can slowly learn the ropes.
Customer service could be much better but hopefully you won’t need it. It’s completely possible to never spend a dime in the game but know you’ll probably have to grind a little extra to get better drops.
If you decide to play talk to other players to help you get started. The beginning is a little overwhelming but easier to get through with the in game mentor system.
