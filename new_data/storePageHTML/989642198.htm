What’s New

Oct 26, 2021

Version 7.1

We are back with a big new update for MoneyCoach.
Here’s what’s new:

=== Monterey Support ===
We’ve updated the app to make the most out of the new things included with the macOS Monterey release. New designs, new icons, new navigation options, new materials and so much more. MoneyCoach on the Mac has never felt any better. Note that this new update is only available for devices running macOS Monterey 12.0 or later.

=== Locations ===
Yes! When adding a new transaction, you can search for a location and attach it to your transaction. This is just the beginning of this feature as we have plans to expand it even more in the future with additional insights and reports based on your spending locations.

=== Categories 3.0 ===

We’ve completely redesigned the Categories screen. You can sort them to your liking or use any of the built in sorting options. We also added additional insights to the categories too as now you can check how many transactions you’ve added in that category and see how much you’ve spent or earned per category.

=== Merge Categories ===

You can now merge categories to your liking. If you have multiple categories for basically the same thing, you can now merge them into one. Any subcategories linked to those categories will also get merged into that new category. You can also merge subcategories within a category. Have fun!

=== Edit Payees ===

You can now edit all your Payees. Change the name, sort them to your liking or with the new built in sort options, this will grant more control over your financial data.

=== Quick Note ===

Yep, on iPad you can now add Quick Notes to any of your Transactions, Budgets and Goals. Add more details about your spending, reflect on the progress with your budget and remember why you created that goal. Quick Notes gives you a lot of flexibility and it’s just a great addition to the MoneyCoach experience.

=== Tags ===
You can now add new tags, edit existing ones, delete and sort them however you want in the Settings.

=== New Custom App Icons ===
We added a couple of new custom app icons requested by the community and a special one featuring our lovely robot mascot.

=== Keyboard Navigation ===

iPad users this is for you. You can now navigate through the app without lifting your fingers from the keyboard. Combined with the new keyboard shortcuts we added, this takes your MoneyCoach iPad experience to the next level. Mac users also benefit from this new great addition.

=== Fixes & Improvements ===

- We improved the sorting when creating a new payee
- We improved the placement of the buttons in New Transaction screen
- We improved the CSV importing and exporting
- We fixed a bug that prevented you from using a newly created payee
- We fixed a bug that displayed the wrong localization for categories in the Settings
- We fixed a rare bug with the budgets
- We fixed a UI bug in category insights
- We fixed a UI bug in Personalized Goals

We put a lot of love and care into this massive new update and we hope you like what you see. App reviews are like gold to a small app developer. If you like using the app take a minute and leave one, it helps a ton!

If you have feedback or suggestions, please submit a support ticket from within the app.

Best,
MoneyCoach team

neblotr , 04/29/2020

Needs bank sync

If ever this app added bank sync it would be a top tier standard. As it is now it feels more like debit & credit, an app I used in college & about a year after graduating. It does train you to a certain mindset about expenses, but it’s easy to get behind in keeping track when all the entry is by hand. For an app that charges a $30/year sub I was surprised by the lack of web access bank connection. It’s effectively charging the cost of an app with actual AI built in (eg ability to set filters and predictive category assignment etc) but expecting the user to do all the data logging footwork.

I suppose it appeals to those without 3+ years of records to import, but given all entries seemingly need to be individually edited it’s not ideal for those not starting from square one.

Definitely love the concept, really really wanted to love it. Was ready to drop the $75/lifetime until I spent about 6 hours trying to fumble through all the data entry. Then realized I was working too hard to make an app work the way I wanted it to, which never works out in the long run.

Will keep my eye on it, definitely a promising developer team and concept.
