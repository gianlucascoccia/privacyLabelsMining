Description

We take care of your newsletters while you focus on managing your inbox.

Newsletters and e-mail are both very personal, but they don't have to be combined. When it's the time for your personalized news and favorite readings, Newsletterss is the perfect companion for newsletter consumption.

Your inbox should be just for e-mails.

- For the avid reader: Newsletterss puts all your personalized information in a single place, at the tap of a button.

- No more missing newsletters: Stop adding non-contacts to your address book to fight inaccurate spam algorithms.

- Convenient and flexible: Add it to your favorite RSS reader. Also ideal for subscribing and managing all your Substacks. The perfect Substack reader

What’s New

Oct 29, 2021

Version 3.7.0

Thank you for using The Newsletterss App! This new version comes with navigation improvements allowing you to sort through newsletters faster:

Our newly introduced bottom navigation for posts includes the following options:

- READ LATER: Advances to the next item in the currently selected inbox flow and marks the item as unread
- NEXT: Advances to the next item in the currently selected inbox flow
- DELETE: Sends the currently viewed item to the Deleted inbox flow and advances to the next one. When this options is performed from the Deleted flow, the item is permanently deleted and cannot be recovered
- MORE: Contains additional options such as Reply, Forward or Unsubscribe from the currently viewed newsletter

We hope you continue to enjoy our product, all feedback is always welcome at contact@newsletterss.com

Hey, this looks like exactly what I need to organize my newsletter subscriptions.

One issue I had is that I tried deleting the “confirmation emails” from my inbox, and when I did the newsletters were removed from my subscriptions tab. Not sure if they will reappear when a new email is sent or if the app unsubscribed me by accident...

Also, I changed my sub stack email to the one given by your app. Will this work for the goal of having those emails hit the apps inbox? Thanks

Hello, thanks for using our Newsletterss App and taking the time to provide feedback.

You are correct about the subscriptions re-appearing once you get a new e-mail, the app simply hides all subscriptions with zero items in it. Same goes for your statement about changing your Substack email, after you do that, all your Substack emails will be available in the app.

hodgesmr , 05/12/2021

What I’ve been looking for

This is the exact app I’ve been looking for to manage newsletter subscriptions. The app is clean and does what it says: keeps newsletters out of your email inbox. I appreciate that it has a web viewer for desktop. I have it 4 stars because I think the app needs two things: 1) the option to turn on a badge for unread count, and 2) some bug fixes on feed refresh - I seem to have to manually pull to refresh the inbox even after opening from a notification about a new item. I think if these two items were implemented I’d become a paid user.

UPDATE: Wow - the developer quickly implemented the unread badge, and I happily subscribed to the annual Premium tier! I think the app could still auto-refresh the Inbox when it enters the foreground, but otherwise this app is great!

Developer Response ,

Thanks for your feedback and using our app! Version 1.5 was just released with support for notification badges and some feed improvements. Our feed fully supports the pull down to refresh gesture, which comes in very handy if you want to get your most recent newsletters without leaving the app.
