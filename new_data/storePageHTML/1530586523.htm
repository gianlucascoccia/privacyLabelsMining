"The AudioKit 909 is PERFECT, fantastic job!" - Jakob Haq, iOS YouTuber

+ Hybrid Synth/sample based drum creation
+ AUv3 (Use as plugin in GarageBand, AUM, Cubasis 3, BM3, etc)
+ Easy to use controls. Create your own sound. Live tweaks.
+ Exclusive special sauce. Vibe it out.
+ Ableton Link if you want to rock it standalone

Tweak the knobs. This is a drum machine that is meant to be PLAYED.

Switch Patterns on screen (or with MIDI) to create new creations on the fly for live performance or songwriting.

It's like getting an expensive piece of vintage hardware on your iPhone and iPad.

Small & mighty: Entire app less than 20mb (on most devices)

WEBSITE:
audiokitpro.com/analog909

REQUIREMENTS:
iPhone 6s and above screen size. Requires iOS 11 and above. Will not work with 1st generation SE phones.

MIDI Guide:
+ MIDI for drum triggers (Bass Drum, Snare, etc) start on "C" Note 36 (C1 in GarageBand)
+ MIDI for pattern triggers start on "C" Note 12 (C-1 in GarageBand)

COMING:
+ AUv3 automation coming to AUM
+ MIDI CC and MIDI Learn coming
+ Better AUv3 timing coming. For now, try lowering buffer for quicker latency
+ MIDI Out

pixeltarian , 11/13/2020

Hopefully it will get better

Based on the positive reviews I purchased this drum app - It’s not as good as the hype. Some folks even said this was the new best drum apps. I cannot fathom how anyone could come to that conclusion. It’s no where near the top of the list for good drum apps. I like the “vibe it out” and crusher options and that’s about it. This app crashes when I press the save button. It glitches out and kills all audio when I try to solo a track. There is only one drum sample to choose from. There is no ride bell, shaker, or kalimba. There’s no probability options. Limited fx options (give us a reverb and delay bus). Speaking of bussing, there’s no fx busing. No midi mapping as of yet. No randomizer. The decay setting doesn’t really matter when the samples have no sustain information unless you set the tune to 0. Even at zero it has no impact of you want a long snare note (IE: turning the sustain knob to the right is useless) Having a synth engine for bass/snare is worthless when the perimeters only expire a small fraction of the potential range. They may as well be samples for how limited the synth engine is The GUI is very rough for a paid app. Did I mention it crashes when I press save? I have immediate buyers remorse. There’s probably worse drum apps but this does not compete with any of the well known drum app options as if bows

Developer Response ,

Hello, that does sound frustrating. We have submitted a quickfix to the app store for the save error that is affecting some users. It should hopefully be there for you soon. For those reading, the reviewer is right. This app is inspired by a very specific drum machine - the synth engine and controls are very similar to a piece of hardware and $99 desktop cloud app, all for $3. For those expecting an "everything" drum machine, this is not it. It's inspired by a very specific device and sound. And the controls and sounds match that. Thank you.
