silver83p , 05/03/2021

Generous, but opaque, promos

The “risk-free bets” claim is deceptive, because the free bets awarded are not worth the full value, and expire in 1 month or 2 weeks, depending on kind (a $15 free bet at -200 odds is equivalent to a $5 cash bet; at +200, to a $10 one). But still, I believed the generosity outweighed the issues, as long as you understood how it worked.

I thought customer support was great, until I was excluded from promos without warning. (The claim that they are meant for “recreational” players is belied by a March promo offering up to $1999 in free bets.) My bets were also getting slowed down by then, sometimes for around a minute (without any communication to explain the degraded experience). I also observed fairly regular errors in the prices and saw no simple way to alert their “traders”. Deposits and withdrawals have still worked fine so far.

They have good prices on many standard markets, best deal being -103 on NBA spread on both sides, at the moment of writing. Points betting is also a unique feature, although some would-be fun markets are too high-roller for recreational players due to the minimum 50 cents bet, and could benefit from normalization.
