Best Bettor’s App!

Since Apple phone apps came on the scene 10 years ago I have diligently searched for a bettors app that can track betting action and trends to assist my handicapping hobby. While many apps have come and gone that did half the job right, Sports Action is the first app to get it right ALL THE WAY! It provides up to the minute info, line changes, trends, information and all the bettor needs to get quick info. It’s best function though is the simple and easy way to put your action in to track results. The fact that it also keeps your betting history and percentages to see how you are doing long term is great. Though I can’t budget to upgrade just yet to the robust additions you get as a subscriber, the basic App is hands down the best for the recreational or even professional sports gaming enthusiast.

With legal sports betting coming soon to the US, I hope the company behind this App keeps their vigorish in the game!!!

Mattl121212 , 11/06/2019

App freezing, links broken under “my action” screen

In general, this is a great app. But It has been horrendous for about the last week or so. I find myself constantly having to close it out and reopen it just to get things to load. Clicking on games under the ‘My Action’ page doesn’t always enter into the game details like it should. I am surprised these issues have not yet been addressed yet but hope they will soon.

One other point... can we PLEASE get the ability to sort games based on a few options such as game time, # of bets placed, etc? I think this is necessary for NCAA football and basketball. Recognize that you have a degenerate group of users who just want to sort by game time so they can see when the next opportunity to put money anywhere but their own pocket will come about. Then go filter NCAAF by “All FBS” and see how challenging this is to navigate. In the words of Frank Costanza, “there has to be a better way”!

Now if you want a real people pleaser, consider a “Games starting in the next 15 minutes” (maybe 30 min?) screen alongside the ‘Top Games’, ‘My Games’, etc. I once used a book that had this category of games and must admit that it was revolutionary.
