Description

Pocket Drums is the most realistic sounding and looking drum app out there! Perfect for both beginners and pros alike, Pocket Drums utilizes the latest technology and decades of real drum experience to create the ideal drumming app for the iPhone or iPad.

All of the features of the app combine to make you feel as if you are sitting down and playing a real drum set. The drums respond to your touch, as the sound varies according to how hard you hit them. The pitch also changes slightly depending on where you hit the toms, just like a real kit. Playing realistic rolls is as easy as swirling your finger around, simply adjusting the roll speed by how fast or slow you swirl your finger.

There is also 15 different professionally recorded high fidelity drum sets to choose from, and an amazing drum wrap, with more to come. Pocket Drums also features 16 PlayAlong Beats, 10 unique Drum Beats and 10 Music Beats to rock out with, as well as 10 different metronome sounds.

A unique feature is the addition of 10 basic rudiment videos, so you can see firsthand how to play the drum rudiments.

With the ability to adjust the pitch, sensitivity, individual drum volume, reverb, and delay, recording, ability to share and export your recordings (optional in-app purchase), and so much more, Pocket Drums is by far the most comprehensive and realistic drumming app available today!

My thoughts

Ok, the app does what it says on the tin...however if you're an experienced drummer this app most likely won't be too helpful and/or fun. Having to learn basic drills such as bass high hat snare etc. is annoying due to you having to learn how to do the bass, snare and high hat just as you would normally drumming. The only way I feel I could play this app is if I use my toe to control the base and my fingers to do everything else. If you aren't a drummer this could inspire you to do the real thing so I guess it's good for that. Another thing: it works like a normal drum kit (kinda) so don't expect yourself to be amazing just keep at it (or even better get real drum lessons and try save up for an acoustic or electric kit.
