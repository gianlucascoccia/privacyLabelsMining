Description

Welcome to [Baby Shark Car Town]!
Meet all your favorite cars, including school bus, police car, helicopter, ambulance, firetruck, and many more!
The app includes videos on cars, racing games, coloring and other educational activities.
Ready to join the fun?

Key Features:

1. Watch 18 different Car Videos!
- Discover Pinkfong's best animated videos featuring different types of cars.
- Sing-along to familiar nursery rhymes and learn the features of different cars in a natural way.
- All content are available in English, Spanish, Portuguese, Korean, Chinese, Japanese and Russian.

2. Play Fun Car Games
- What's your favorite car? Police car? Fire truck? Pick one and play with it!
- Learn the numbers from 1 to 10 with fun games!

3. Drive Your Own Car
- Drive & race with your favorite car!
- Drive up to10 different vehicles. Options include police cars, helicopters, bulldozers, and more.
- Turn, honk, accelerate, and pick up your friends while you drive.
- Develop your spatial ability by steering the wheel and racing down the road.

4. Wash your Cars
- Wash the dirty cars with bubbles!
- Learn the names of the different cars.

5. Match & Learn about Cars
- Develop your cognitive skills by matching the different cars.

6. Paint and Color Vehicles
- Color your favorite car, using12 different coloring tools, including paint brushes, spray paints, and more.
- Develop your fine motor skills and creativity with fun coloring activities.

Are you ready to meet our cool car friends?
Join the action-packed adventure now!

Great app but...

My child loves this app. It’s his hands down favorite app. As soon as the iPad is turned on it’s the one that he goes to.

You are limited to Wheels on the bus unless you pay to unlock the other vehicles. He’s obsessed so we paid. I’m just annoyed that the o my way he can use the other vehicles is if he’s connected to wifi. If he isn’t they are all locked. The minute you connect.. unlocked.

What’s the point of me paying 13 bucks to unlock them for them to only be able to be used with wifi? It’s frustrating. A one year doesn’t comprehend why they can only be used at certain times. The majority of the time he o my gets his iPad when we’re out and about and doesn’t have wifi.

Is this an iPad feature? What am I missing?

If you’ve got an iPad that always has connectivity than go for it but if yours doesn’t and your kid will be using it out and about skip it.
