Description

Rule your kingdom, rule your romance.

King’s Throne is an empire simulation MMORPG where you rule a kingdom and experience the medieval royal life: exciting adventures, epic wars, irresistible romance, kingdom management and more than you could imagine! Play for free now, and immerse yourself in playing as a medieval king.

Key Features:
- Rule the Kingdom – Make good use of your resources to strengthen your kingdom. Always ready for expansion.
- Recruit Knights – Assemble legendary heroes along the journey and empower them to fight for you.
- Court Desirable Maidens – Encounter gorgeous ladies in town and forge your own love story.
- Raise a Dynasty – Nurture your children, arrange alliances through marriage, and eventually expand your influence.
- Undertake the Expedition – Crush the enemies on your way and foil their plots.
- Round Table – Unite other lords as an alliance and fight against other competitors together.
- Conquer the Server – Join PVP tournaments, unleash your strategy and prove your power.

Zombiez69 , 10/24/2019

For all Intents and purpose, it’s fun. Still a little cash grabby though with the gem system

Enjoy the game, griped about it for a bit at first but I kept coming back which made me realize I don’t hate it as much as I thought I did. The maidens SHOULD NOT cost gems to visit though especially when you’re trying to make better quality kids as the more intimacy you have with a maiden the more gems it cost to purposely visit them. Gems are a big factor you’ll find, and I feel it is still very excessive even with the amount of gems the game gives you daily you’ll still be lacking. Other than that it’s fun the art is appealing the story is ok, it starts off good but after the first few chapters it’s been less compelling. Still I’ll give it 4 stars.

I’d recommend though a better multiplayer aspect should be looked into other than just marring off children, maybe an rts aspect? An overview multiplayer map where you send troops to attack others but marring into families would create alliances etc. just a thought would honestly make things much more compelling imo. Cheers.

nicknamenotknownhere , 03/23/2021

Good but.....

It’s a decent game fun etc, but can be unstable seems to have hiccups and randomly close out also at times you must tap 5 or 6 times to get to the next screen which is annoying. It just seems as though the flow could be better. I got lucky enough to find a good group of people who are helpful with in-game questions so “figuring” things out hasn’t been difficult but I can understand the “steep learning curve” mentioned in other reviews. To be fair there are a good amount of rewards handed out but some times it’s just junk, and if you’re going to have a “point exchange” don’t allow for players to have points left over, it’s poor planning. Finally, I understand the devs want to monetize but the overload of events and constant cash grab is really over the top, (slot machines really? the ridiculous tables & the archery which are total jokes & not random you get “rewarded” the same lame items over & over, I have to wonder if I spent more would it be better) this more than anything frustrates the player base I encounter. Comments like “these fools are going to burn the whole game down because of their greed” have been made more than a few times, then people disappear. Slow up on all that & fix the choppiness would be my advice if you want to retain players.

Blackavar015 , 03/18/2021

Fun empire builder

First, you can turn off the naughty stuff - yay! Second, props to the developers for providing in-game translation. This is a combo hero-card empire builder rpg that is quite fun for a casual game. I love that I don’t have to rearrange my life to collect goods, engage in alliance wars, protect my loot, etc. Most of the game is very click-heavy, so if you’re looking for a more tactical experience, this probably isn’t it. The good news is that eventually you unlock “click all” features that really help minimize the click-a-thon. Like most mmorpg apps, there is a P2P contingent, but I haven’t seen a paywall like I’ve seen in some other games. Eh, with the exception that you may stall quickly in the main quest rewards if you don’t spend money. Strongly recommend that you start your account in the newest server available if you are F2P. Hope the developers can think of some additional diplomacy-related features to add. Or maybe spies? Also would be good to add some additional explanation about certain features, like kid levels and hero enhancement.
