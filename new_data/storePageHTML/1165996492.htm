ndjxndjdkakal , 05/22/2021

great app for vocabulary

i love the idea so much, it saves you a lot of hassle by creating the flashcards of the words you look up on it's own. i also love how the word you're learning is pronounced very clearly each time you flip through the cards. the themed card sets are also very useful, the app is an amazing idea in general.
however, i feel like the "out of energy" forced intervals are very annoying. i would much rather watch ads after each practice than have to wait hours until i can practice again. i can't really adjust my study schedule to the timings of the intervals.
besides that, being able to organize imported vocabulary into more sets than just the "translations" set would also be really useful. when learning multiple languages, all the looked up words go into the "translations" set, and practicing words from multiple languages at once is really inconvenient. the emojis on the flashcards are kind of unnecessary too but it's not that bothersome.

Developer Response ,

Hi, thanks for your kind words. Please note, that even if all your words are in the same group, you can simply 'filter' the desired language you'd like to learn by selecting 'Practice %language%'. Hope that helps 👋

Developer Response ,

Thanks for your honesty. The app works this way: you translate words you want to learn through the translator (second tab). By default, all your translations go to the group called 'Translations' (first tab). You also can create different groups to sort words into them. Additionally, you pick words from predefined themed groups (third tab). So, once you have your word-groups ready — you can practice them. Tap practice on the first tab and pick practice type from the menu. If you are not familiar with words yet, we recommend you to start from the simplest type which is 'Flashcards'. This way you can firstly see the words before starting to guess their meanings. Hope this makes sense. Please let us know if you have any questions. Our email is hello@rmmbr.io

ThereareOthers , 03/08/2018

Easiest vocab builder I’ve seen

I appreciate that the app immediately adds new vocabulary Flashcards. This the sole reason I downloaded and purchased the app. As a language learner the redundancy of friction to: find a definition on google, migrate it to chegg, find the part of speech (as well as proper gender and spelling) and add this to the chegg flashcard... it made actually studying the card less of a priority. This app does all of that work seamlessly.

I can’t completely walk away from other flashcard apps, however. One immensely useful functionality I’ve found in other apps and have not found here is management of study cards for grammar and conjugation. Chegg has the ability to add photos of tables which seems to be a great solution if you never need to apply the grammar and conjugation to a sentence.
