HOW IT WORKS

Just select one of our many original card designs, or select a photo from your phone. Then write a personal message on the front or back of the card with a stylus or just your finger. (Don’t worry you can type too, and we have several fun designer fonts to choose from.) You can then add up to three additional photos to your order, or a gift card. We print your cards, photos and handwritten message on premium Mohawk card stock.

Multiple card frames come in our own unique square accordion-style format, with micro-perforations between each fold. We seal them by hand in our custom, kraft-paper envelopes, add a first class hand-applied stamp and try to mail them within 24hrs.

We go to great lengths to make sure the final product is as personal and heartfelt as if you sent it yourself.

You can send a Felt card to just one person or send the same card or photos to lots of friends and family, to share and preserve your special memories. Felt is used by brides and new mom’s for sending all their thank you’s and it’s perfect for Holiday cards or any occasion.

PipeDreamer , 09/18/2018

So much potential

Here’s the rub: I really like this app and the way that they have set it up to work… You can use your own hand writing with an Apple Pencil on an iPad Pro which is awesome! The only problem is, the “pen“ option that they give you is so fat it looks like you are writing with a fat Crayola marker. The resolution of the Apple Pencil is much much higher than this app allows you to make use of which is a real pity…. The convenience of this app and the very reasonable prices(!) make this an amazing combination… But until they allow you to have finer control over the hand writing (and by that I mean, finer tips) I will cease to use this anymore. My handwriting is relatively small and I would like to zoom out there writing surface and write in a smaller script which simply is not possible. Once they allow these two features, this will be my go to app without question. I should also mention that there palm rejection is absolutely terrible… Every time I set my hand down to begin writing, my pinky ads rogue lines and dots all over the card. They definitely need to add an option where if you have an Apple Pencil active it rejects all hand input when you are at the writing phase.
