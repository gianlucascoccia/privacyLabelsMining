Sleepiest

Guide for set up and how to use sleepiest is poorly designed for a beginner. Tips are available but do not allow a transfer to actual step or setting, for example. There are explanations for the stages of sleep, but not an interpretation of actual sleep stages in report, or at least, no clear explanation of how to interpret the information. There’s no explanation for what some of the settings are that are in the section for your preferences. Most are self-explanatory, but some are only “abbreviated” (what’s that stand for...?).
It’s been frustrating. After three nights of trying to track sleep, I’ve only got one actual reading that has any solid information. I don’t think it tracked properly the first night...chalked it up to “user error”. Second night, worked perfectly...yay, figured it out! Third night, I had found some information that I had not seen before and thought, “oh! Here’s what I’ve been looking for!” That’s where I made a couple of adjustments, trying to “fine tune” everything for optimal effectiveness, only when I checked on my report, it reported “wake”. It’s an actual report recorded in my profile. Ugh...! I want this to work and I have read and searched the app to make sure I am understanding everything about setting up and selecting preferences and so on, but it isn’t very easily explained.

VikiFrost , 05/25/2020

Kind of creepy

I only tried the stories of this app (did not get to the soundscapes and meditations before my free trial ran out) and many of them had the type of content I like (fairytale, magic, woodland-type) but unfortunately some of the stories were too creepy and disturbing for me and while I fell asleep fast most of the time, some of the stories were just too disturbing to the point that they disrupted my sleep. When the trial ran out, I transitioned to the free trial on the Moshi app and wasn’t as pleased with the content of the stories (not as many stories about fairies and woodland magic, as well as a different story-telling style where it doesn’t slow down as much in the end) but they are much more peaceful and free of disturbing content that I always slept well with the Moshi stories and decided to purchase the Moshi subscription over the Sleepiest one. I appreciate the company making an app gearing their stories towards an adult audience, but a doll who has a tear in her head that her thoughts can leak out of is just too creepy for bedtime. I also enjoy the meditations and soundscapes of the Moshi app (and they help my dog calm down too) but I cannot compare them to the ones in Sleepiest because I didn’t try them there. All in all, if you’re not squeamish then get this app but if you’re sensitive then get the G-rated gentle kiddos version.
