Description

Stop being addlepated with unknown words! Don’t know what ‘addlepated’ means?

Get a step ahead. While others look up definitions, you already know the answer.

Academic, obscure, challenging vocabulary for native speakers is taken from Oxford Advanced Learner’s Dictionary’s, Cambridge Dictionary, SAT, GRE, GMAT, IELTS, TOEFL, CAT exams. We provide you with simple examples and plenty of synonyms for better understanding and memorizing new words. If you want to blow your friends’ minds we prepare etymology and extra examples.

Too busy? You can choose any time of notifications to make learning an everyday habit. Don't know how to pronounce? We offer you a pronunciation for every taste.

Become first to start a conversation. Impress your friends, family, and teachers. Read any scientific article in one breath. Keep your mind focused. Make your thoughts clear. Sound sexier.

Only one word a day. 365 words a year.

'Word of the Day' is:
- Daily widget with new word
- A wide range of academic, obscure, and challenging vocabulary
- Only trustful sources
- Like for a word you don't want to forget
- Simple examples and clear usage
- Etymology for super nerds
- Synonyms for effective memorizing
- Sharing a new word with friends
- Word Search Challenge
- Notifications for any time you choose
- A great number of voices with a choice of what English version to hear: English US (Male, Female), British English UK (Male, Female), Canadian English (Male, Female), Australian English (Male, Female) or EN India (Male, Female).

So disappointed

I loved this app so much. I loved how I always had this little learning device that I carry around with me everywhere. It was simple and never too complicated. But then it started to update...a lot. I knew this app was finally starting to kick off, and I was happy it was getting the attention it deserves. But god, what the hell was it with the ads? Ads come up every forking five seconds! How do you expect me to even finish reading the definition of the word you notify me to check out everyday? Your motives obviously show little care in actually enhancing one’s vocabulary, but rather finding a pathway to earn more money. It hurts writing this down, but I’m hoping that with this you’ll cut down the number of ads that pop up. How about just one every two minutes? That’s reasonable. Not one for every five seconds. In the meantime, I’ll be turning off notifications for this app specifically and being on the look out for new updates, hoping one of them includes less ads. I understand you need some way to earn money, but seriously though, less people will probably delete this. I have much love for this app, so please do your part at making sure the people who download this app see it as a learning tool. A tool doesn’t force you to do things.

Hello,

I’m very sorry to hear that. I understand your frustration. Unfortunately, the project can’t live without ads. They support our team and give us chances to experiment and find the way we can give more value to our users.
We are working now on adding some more useful features for our users. We would be grateful if you appreciate them in the coming weeks and increase your rate. Without adds, our improvements would be impossible.
Hope for your understanding.
