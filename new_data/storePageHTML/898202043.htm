Description

Tales with Gigi is a mobile storytelling app with over 40 professionally narrated fairy tales giving you 100% as-in-book experience. All of the tales are specially designed to challenge children to think creatively, use their imagination and motivate them to find and appreciate the moral of the story.

Features:
- Cinderella, Beauty and the Beast, The Gingerbread Man, the original Grimm's fairy tales, the classic Aesop’s fables and many other popular stories
- Professional voice over narration, helping and improving kid’s diction and vocabulary
- 100% “as-in-book” experience, giving children the opportunity to think and use their imagination
- Safe for children – no inappropriate content, ads or banners
- Made for children – easy to use interface and no inappropriate content or ads
- Edutainment at its finest - specially designed to challenge kids to think and improve their reading skills and have fun in the meantime

We wish you a pleasant time reading and bonding with Tales with GiGi!

Underf10w , 09/17/2015

My 4.5 year old Loves it! (And so do I)

Great collection of fairytales and fables. I like how the stories are not animated, but rather have nice illustrious and voice-over readings. (You can also turn off the voiceover a and read it yourself)

I quickly upgraded to full access for $2.99, so I could have offline functionality. (Therefore I didn't fully test drive the free version.). Certainly worth the 3 bucks when you consider the cost of a fairytale book and the ability to bring this on your phone/device.

There are some minor bugs, but works well on my 8.4 iOS. (I'll try to remember to update this when I move to iOS 9.0). An error message has popped up, but it didn't crash the app, and appeared to have no affect.

For offline viewing, you need to: (1) upgrade to full version; (2) watch a story while online, which subsequently is saved to your phone/device; (3) in the future, that story will be available offline; (4) while on the 'table of contents' screen for that story, click on the treehouse; (5) once inside the treehouse there is a TV that shows availability of offline stories. It was a bit confusing (to me, at least), but it works.

Looks like this app will take up a big chunk of space on your device, after viewing the stories (which are saved to your phone). After viewing ~20 stories, I'm up to 1.9GB of used storage.
