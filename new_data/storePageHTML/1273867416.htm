Price for the knowledge earned

I am one that doesn’t put a price on knowledge. Knowledge is a very important part of our existence. Is this going to be a long drawn out review? No. To put this simply, the information, guides, and workbooks you get for this program are at a good price. Yes it’s a subscription but it’s a valuable one. Learning about cooking, negotiating, gardening, business, finance from some of the most outstanding and intelligent people out there is a fantastic opportunity. I am a nobody, but I love learning about business and this has helped me tremendously from the few months I’ve had it. As some of the greats have said, “listen to each side, you might learn something you didn’t already know.” The most important part, repetition. Once you have a skill down, listen or watch it again to better hone your skill. Knowledge is not cheap, you can either learn it by your experience or speed the process up by learning from someone else in the field you want to be in.

Did this become a long and drawn out review? In my opinion, yes. Was this helpful to the next person deciding to purchase this subscription, learn new concepts, apply them in their lives and become part of a larger master mind group? Well, only you can decide. If this helps you decide to get the subscription I can promise you it’s work it.

Keep learning my friends. Be a student, not a follower

Priceless

From the moment I saw the first commercial for Master Class I said that is compelling. A year later my wife gifted me an unlimited subscription and I have been absorbing some of the greatest content from true experts that I have ever found. Master Classes are each independent and different in their own ways because the producers let the experts dictate the pace and the topics that are most important to the instructor and therefore the audience. Over the last 6months I have watched at least 25 full classes and parts and pieces of close to 25 more. There is so much amazing content. I devour the marketing/ business and off beat topics. I have learned skateboarding from Tony Hawk, Magic from Penn and Teller, and the inside story as to how Disney purchased Pixar directly from the man who orchestrated it. I see different topics that I think, that doesn’t interest me, but when I watch the opener I am usually hooked, and later find so many things that are applicable to me in my personal and professional life. I am always recommending Master Class to others. Where else can you get inside the thought process of a writer, composer, chef, skater, magician, designer, photographer, negotiator and so many more. It’s amazing. Thank you for all the time that has been dedicated to making these classes so engaging, educational, and entertaining.
