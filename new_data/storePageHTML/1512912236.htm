Description

Hi I’m Greg, your plant superhero, here to make plant care simple!

Let me guess, you bought a bunch of plants over the past few months and now you’re feeling overwhelmed? Have no fear, Greg is here! While you’ve been selling out your local nursery, I’ve been hard at work studying over 35,000 data points collected on houseplants worldwide. I’ll set a schedule for each of your plants and adapt it over time based on your habits as well as the weather and climate in your area. Prepare for the indoor jungle of your dreams!

-Get daily notifications from me when plants need your attention
-No need to set a schedule. Add each of your plants and let plant science take care of the rest
-Mark plants as watered or snoozed to help me learn. My watering suggestions will adapt over time to fit you and your plants like a glove
-Auto-identify each of your plants. My water schedules adapt automatically to each plant’s drought tolerance
-View your whole plant collection in my jungle view
-And sprouting additional features as fast as a spider plant makes babies!

My team is tackling a big mission: designing and building the best plant parent companion on the planet. Even more ambitious is our long-term goal of growing our collective understanding of plant science. We’ll apply our learnings as they relate to our global food systems and our climate crisis to support our incredible planet and its inhabitants.

Download Greg now and let’s grow that green thumb!

Hopeful turned Disappointed , 09/18/2021

So far exactly what I’m looking for

I have been on the hunt for an app to help me take care of my houseplants. I’ve paid for Blossom as well as Planta. Neither fit what I needed and their apps were filled with too much fluff and were difficult to navigate. I will say that if you want to read tips about plant care, Blossom takes the cake of the 3. Planta gave inaccurate and often completely wrong care info. Blossom was decent. Greg is less informative and more action-oriented, but exactly what I needed. I am new to houseplants (started during quarantine) and am trying to learn how to take care of the variety of plants I’ve collected. Over the last year I’ve learned that for most of my plants, what people recommend online is often not adequate. There are so many other factors - humidity, light, soil, pot size, pot material, plant size, season, drafts, etc. No other app (that I’ve found) can intelligently take all of those factors into account. But then I found GREG! Have only been using it for a day but so far it is exactly what I’ve been looking forward to. And I’m super excited to see how it starts to “learn”. Hopefully things continue to go well.

tree2727 , 04/08/2021

Simple, Pretty, Adless, and Helpful

I’ve only had this app for a couple days but I’m a huge fan so far. I decided to invest in the $30 lifetime option because honestly I’ve spent way more on my plants and I’ve got a gift card that brought it down to $5. I love cataloguing and seeing how my plants compare to others. The AI is pretty impressive especially in identifying most plants simply from photos. It seems like the beginning of a great app to integrate into my life, but I definitely see room for growth (no pun intended.) I think there should be ways to update your experiences with a plant in a social way. Instead of just seeing people having watered their plants on my feed I’d love to see propagations, fertilizing, repotting, pest control, and even plant deaths. I think this would make the app loads more interesting and fun. I’d also love a commenting feature or an advice feature where fellow plant folk can respond to inquiries for help. TLDR: Definitely worth it for someone passionate about plants so long as updates are on the way.

Alina Michelle , 08/28/2021

Super handy for more than just a watering schedule

I love the social aspect of the app, and the little tidbits of information on the home page.

This app will totally help you water more regularly, but also it just helps keep track what type of plants you own. I often forget the official names of my plants and having this information so readily on hand makes researching fertilization needs, when to repot, pest treatments etc etc way easier. It also helped me identify a few plants that I had 0 idea what kind of plant I owned.

For edits I’d love to see: I want to be able to keep more information about my plants. Which plants share containers, whether my plants are in a closed/semi closed terrarium or in water. The social and cataloging aspect of the apps has made it fun to add plants that I don’t need a water schedule but I also feel bad that those plants could affect their algorithm. I also want to add more scecific descriptions about my plants updates “turning greener” “my current goal for this plant” “testing out this propagation methods.” “This method kicked my fungus Gnar problem” I love to see what other people are thinking about their plants, and I want to share what works for me.
