Description

The Canon App is an all-access pass to faithful Reformed Christian content. Stream and download unlimited audiobooks, sermon series, conferences, debates and other Christ-centered audio resources, as well as watch miniseries, talk shows, documentaries, video courses.

Watch and cast Man Rampant Season 1 & 2 to your TV, listen to your favorite Canon Press audiobooks, along with more video and audio added weekly.

With the Canon App, you get:
• Unlimited streaming and downloading
• Premium ad-free experience
• Full access to the entire Canon Press audio and video catalog
• First access to new releases and subscriber-only content
• Exclusive author-interaction and live webinars
• Discounts on your favorite physical products
• The insider's scoop on events, book releases, and news

Content:great, App:meh

Just as the other reviewers say, you’re downloading this because the content is really encouraging and Biblical, but you may find the app dissatisfying. I am a subscriber, and wish I could recommend the app more than 3-stars worth. I’ve emailed the developer multiple times to try and solve the doesn’t-keep-it’s-place-in-books issues, reinstalled the app, etc. If you are considering this as an audiobook source you need to know that you will be frustrated. Videos, podcasts and other non-book stuff will work, it just will not keep your place in a book so you can pick up where you left off. I’m a little surprised that Canon Press isn’t addressing the issue, or at least telling subscribers that they’re working on it. Please, Canon, if you read the reviews just give us a crumb of hope that it might be fixed someday?

Chris Mascia , 11/29/2020

Great content, but app needs work.

Every bit of content I've checked out has been phenomenal. So far, "Notes From the Tilt-a-Whirl" audio book has been my favorite. If I could rate separate categories, content would get five stars.

Unfortunately, the app leaves a bit to be desired. The biggest drawback is that your place in videos and audiobooks is not saved when you leave the app. That means you have to remember about where you were and manually search for your place. It's particularly difficult for the audiobooks because they aren't broken up into chapters in the conventional way. Each chapter is not it's own track. There is one continuous track but the timer resets to zero at each chapter break.

Unless this bug is fixed, I wouldn't pay a subscription for access to this content.
