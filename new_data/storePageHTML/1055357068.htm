Description

Automatically log your tax-deductible mileage with MileCatcher! IRS compliant.

Start your 30 day FREE trial!

Classify your trips as Business, Personal, or Custom! Classification can be done in-app, by notification, or through the Web Portal!

Use MileCatcher’s Work Hours feature to cut down on battery usage. Only log trips when you’re on the clock!

Save frequently used addresses with pre-saved destinations such as: Work, Home, etc.

Includes Mac and PC access to the MileCatcher Web Portal. Bulk classify your trips, generate reports, view statistics, and more!

Generated reports are easily exportable in PDF, Excel, or CSV format. IRS, HMRC, CRA rates.
Do you want your employees using MileCatcher? MileCatcher offers MileCatcher Teams with consolidated billing! Use the Web Portal to generate employee reports!

If you’re an independent business owner, contractor, realtor, a driver for a delivery or ride share service (Uber, Lyft, DoorDash, Instacart, Postmates), etc… This mileage tracking app was built for you!

MileCatcher also comes with an array of other built in features;
- Full address and map route with every trip
- Build your own address library
- Trips are automatically filtered by classification (Unclassified, Business, Personal, Custom)
- Online backup of all trip data
- Custom business rates
- Optional automatically generated weekly and monthly reports sent to your email
- Frequently updated
- Tax rule compliant in +20 Countries
- Auto-classify repeat trips to save time
- Multi company, multi-vehicle, multi rate reporting

Initial subscription requires user opt-in.

Additional information about payment processing and subscription management:

In-app MileCatcher Premium auto-renewable subscription is available in a monthly or yearly plan.

Payment will be charged to iTunes Account at confirmation of purchase. Subscription automatically renews unless auto-renew is turned off at least 24-hours before the end of the current period. Accounts will be charged for renewal within 24 hours prior to the end of the current period. Subscriptions may be managed by the user and auto-renewal may be turned off by going to the user’s account settings after purchase. Any unused portion of a free trial period, if offered, will be forfeited when the user purchases a subscription to that publication, where applicable. Cost of the renewal is US$5.99/month or US$47.88/year or equivalent in local currency.

Terms of Use and privacy are available at https://milecatcher.com/legalpro/

Warning: Continued use of GPS running in the background can dramatically decrease battery life. This app may use your location even when it isn’t open, which can decrease battery life.

Superior mileage tracker. Very Reliable.

Certain apps makes your life so much easier. This is one of them. I am a nurse and need to track my mileage and I LOVE MileCatcher. I have in the past tried to guess my mileage and keep track by using a paper mileage log book but I would always forget and lose track. So many times I forgot to write down my odometer reading and then had to spend time end of week using Google maps looking up addresses and distance. This app makes it quick and efficient and super easy to use. This app logs all your trips automatically and stores it for free online so you don’t use much data storage on your phone. And it separates each of your drives automatically and you can quickly add notes and classify each of your drives as either personal or business.
The app does all the math for you and will give you a detailed pdf or Excel reports of all of your drives. This company is dedicated to mileage tracking and been around for more than four years. Since they have all your trip locations backed up, I do like they don’t share the trips data internally with search engine etc. and that they comply with EU GDPR privacy requirements. Highly recommended!

Best, most helpful, mileage tracker available

The reporting is awesome, although I think it could be a little improved by adding custom reporting to the report writer function.

The owner and I exchanged an email thread and he was super professional, kind and genuine. I love that by using MileCatcher I get to support someone like that. My only complaint about the functionality is that sometimes when I let my trips pile up and I haven’t classified them in a few days, the app gets “stuck” on one particular trip as though the server doesn’t get the message that I’m sending (re: what kind of trip, personal or business). All in all- a great app by a person that seems to be genuinely concerned about whether or not his creation is actually helping people.

Drewster! , 03/16/2019

End Of An App

I’ve used this app for 2 years now. It was never perfect, but for the low price of Free it did the job well enough so that I get compensation for the hundreds of miles I drive for work each week. However, the app unfortunately does not perform well enough to justify locking the main function behind a subscription. Which is exactly what they have done. Addresses are inconsistent at best, the app eats data like nobody’s business, and the blue “location in use” banner that never goes away is obnoxious and sometimes wreaks havoc on other apps. Tracking frequently failed to catch trips (again please keep in mind that I make tons of trips that need to be logged each week), shorter trips are frequently ignored, and sometimes segments of your trips will be recorded as a straight line from one spot to the next (to be fair, that particular issue is much less common than it used to be but still does occasionally happen). All of this I was willing to put up with though, for a free way to get money back to pay for my car’s wear and tear.
TL;DR, it’s no longer free to log trips. Which means my standards for the app have gone up. And the app unfortunately fails to meet those new standards. Will be uninstalling shortly.
