Description

Get real-time data about air quality for 40+ countries in North America (PurpleAir and AirNow), Europe & Australia with AirCare! The best air quality app, with over 20 international awards, no wonder our users love us, just read the reviews.

What you get:
- Clear and understandable real-time information about air pollution around you
- AQI - Air Quality Index, as well as raw pollutants like PM10, PM2.5, O3, CO, NO2, SO2
- 4 types of pollen data in your city - Birch, Olive, Grass and Ragweed (for select countries)
- Visualisation of the data with the help of graphs, charts and maps
- Breezy - our child friendly mascot that teaches children about the benefits of clean air

Love what you see, and want to support our small team? Subscribe to the PRO version, and you'll also get:
- Apple Watch app
- Home screen widgets
- No ads in the app
- Push notifications when pollution rises on your favourite stations
- A slick Dark Mode
- Access to all upcoming pro features

While this app has potential to do great; it only gives you the information from the nearest station to your current location. Which, in return if you live out in the country where there are no stations, it cannot work good enough to truly give the full affect that it can.

AirCare provides an essential service at no cost to the user.

Given the increasing number of wildfires here in the west, having easy-to-access, current, site specific information on air quality wherever I am or plan to go is essential to my health. AirCare provides that information, as well as showing me where fires are. For world travelers, air quality information for many other countries is also available. Making this information available at no cost means that everyone, regardless of income, has access to it. Thank you AirCare!

brett257 , 07/11/2021

Poor App

I was looking for a AQI app and downloaded this one based on reviews, even paid for the Pro version. I’m pretty disappointed. Doesn’t show an AQI station nearby that state agencies use for AQI reporting; the nearest station the app recognizes is over an hour drive away. I also can’t set a station on the widget, at any distance away, so the widget is useless. The AQI map is unreadable in dark mode, another useless feature just like the widget. Tried the “contact support” link in settings but the link doesn’t work. I’m running the latest iOS software so that’s not an issue. Do yourself a favor and go with EPA’s AirNow. I don’t know why that app has poor reviews; its much better than this one (and recognizes a nearby AQI station that this one doesn’t).
