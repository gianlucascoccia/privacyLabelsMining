Description

No matter where you are, your vehicle is never far away with the Uconnect® App.

Owners of vehicles equipped with Uconnect + SiriusXM Guardian* connected services, or earlier vehicles with Uconnect Access* connectivity can take advantage of the following services designed to make your life easier:

- Lock or unlock your car from virtually anywhere
- Start your car & cancel start remotely
- Flash the lights & sound the horn to help find your car
- Locate your car on a map using Vehicle Finder
- Send a destination to your car’s navigation system using Send & Go[1]
- Family Drive Alerts provides notifications for Boundary, Curfew, Speed and Valet.

Vehicles equipped with connected services will have an ASSIST and SOS/9-1-1 button on or above the rear view mirror inside the vehicle (excludes FIAT 500e).

To log into the Uconnect app, use the same email and password created when setting up your account on the Mopar Owner Site.

If you are having trouble logging into the app, please contact Uconnect Care at 1-877-855-8400 (U.S.) or 1-877-324-9091 (Canada).

Vehicle must be properly equipped with compatible Uconnect system. Vehicle must be registered for Uconnect Access or SiriusXM Guardian fulfilling all registration and subscription requirements. Vehicle must be located within the 50 United States or Canada and have an active and usable cellular connection.

StuffAndyMakes , 05/20/2019

Not bad, but a few annoyances

I don’t know if it’s necessarily the app’s fault or the network or what, but it doesn’t always start the truck. It’s nice when it is working, especially out here in Phoenix when it’s terribly hot in the middle of summer. Remote start from far away from the truck is very handy for getting the interior air conditioned before getting out to it in a large parking lot.

The truly annoying thing about this app is the fact that I cannot paste my password into the app after it forgets the login credentials, either from a restart of the phone or an app update. If you are being careful with your passwords and using a password management app, you are storing unique passwords and pass phrases for every app. I copy mine from 1Password and expect to be able to paste it into an app, but this one disallows that. Ridiculous UX. Fix that and I’ll be much happier to bump up the rating on this app. There’s no security advantage to stopping me from pasting from my password management system. In fact, you may be causing people to use simpler, more dangerous passwords.

I love my new 2021 Jeep Grand Cherokee 80th Anniversary Edition, but I hate this app. The remote start is a joke, and only works (when it finally decides to) after I cycle through multiple times.
