Description

Set course for the fairways of the world, but don’t expect an easy lie or to improve your golf swing! Get ready to play solitaire with a twist playing Fairway Solitaire, the card game that’s easy to pick up, but impossible to put down.

Fairway Solitaire is the unique card game where solitaire and golf merge into an easy to play puzzle game. Simply play one card up or one card down from your draw pile to clear the puzzle board as you take on challenging solitaire puzzles on hand-painted, scenic golf courses from all around the world.

Fun and Easy to Play!
• Simply play one card up or one card down from your draw pile, it’s that easy!
• Stay on par by removing the most cards in each solitaire hand
• If you get stuck, select a golf club from your bag
• Expertly play like a pro through puzzling solitaire challenges, from rough hazards to sand traps and rain hazards

Why Fairway Solitaire?
• Play THOUSANDS OF COURSES in incredible locations all over the world
• Play with unique golf-themed BOOSTS to power-up your game
• Use STRATEGY to master the fairway
• Use irons and Midas Clubs as WILD CARDS to aide in your strategy, or POWER UPS like the Shuffle and Preview to solve puzzling card layouts.
• Enjoy over 1,000 solitaire levels and counting!
• Get swept away to different destinations in special week-long events
• Every weekend you’ll find amazing hand painted scenery to take you far away while staying in the comfort of your own home.
• Play Fairway Solitaire daily for events like Treasure Hunts, Pro Stars, and Stamp-edes!
• Play Fairway Solitaire for as long as you like; there are no lives or limits to your game play!

Earn rewards:

Great app but I have to say...

...I was a little bummed out that I paid for this app many years ago. When I went to download it again that paid version was gone and in place is this free/IAP version. I’ve played this game for years and despite not being thrilled about the new model I can understand why they went that route. It’s just the way things are now and it’s also the reason I don’t really play iPad games anymore.

However, Ive Played a lot of games with in app purchases and I have to say I think that Big Fish approached it very fairly. You never feel forced to make purchases to keep playing, and even for people who like to get all the stars/achievements like me it is possible with elbow grease and dedication. Mostly I just ignore some of the elements they put in to make it more addicting and try not to worry too much about getting three stars on everything. It’s just a nice game to relax with before bed, on a Sunday, or during a bath, so definitely recommended!
