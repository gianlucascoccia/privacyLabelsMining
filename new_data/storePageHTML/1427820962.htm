Description

TrueLane® is a free smartphone app available to auto insurance policyholders from The Hartford in select states who are enrolled in the TrueLane program. The app uses the sensors in your phone to automatically measure how you drive. The safer you drive, the better your potential Renewal Discount will be.

JoA41 , 09/19/2020

Truelane dishonest

I used the Truelane app for 180 days to try to get a discount on my insurance, I am a careful driver and had very few incidents, only 3 minor braking for such minor incidents I couldn’t even remember them..no sudden stops, On the last day of the 180 it said I would get a 25% discount. The next day, when it would tell you your discount, I tapped on “see your discount” and it gave me only a 10% discount, with no explanation. The app drains your phone battery and I would never have put up with it for 6 months if I thought it would basically renege on its offer. Seems dishonest to me.

Update: Hartford rep responded that there is a bug in the app that causes this problem. It did show the correct discount after several days. However, I had called Truelane tech support right after noticing the problem and they made no mention of the bug. They said only Customer service could help me. Then called Customer. They also said nothing about a bug. They were unable to tell me what my discount would be and said only Truelane knew. So it would be great it the two got together and could let everyone know about the bug in advance...and didn’t keep bouncing customers back and forth. Hopefully the app will be fixed soon.

Developer Response ,

Hello
Please check your app again for your renewal discount. There is a known bug where the renewal discount defaults to 10% but returns to the appropriate discount after a few days. This issue will be fixed in our next release.

Worthless

I have already posted a review on Google, but I will continue my comments using my wife’s Iphone. This app is worthless and dings you for normal driving behaviors, especially if you are driving a Subaru vehicle with Eyesight. If a vehicle directly in front of you slows to get into a left turn lane, Eyesight will automatically apply the brakes to avoid striking the vehicle - even though it is now in a different traffic lane. TrueLane dings you for this. We just took a trip and TrueLane logged 30 something “speed events” while we drove from Salt Lake City to Fillmore Utah on I-15. Most of the “dings” were for speeding - 77 in a 75 zone...Really...I challenge any employee of AARP or TrueLane to drive from Salt Lake to Las Vegas on I-15, and log “no dings.” Driving behavior cannot accurately be evaluated in this manner. It includes many factors TrueLane cannot measure, weather, traffic conditions, fatigue, accuracy of the vehicles speedometer, etc...The first and main question to ask a driver is “When was your last collision”? If it was 20+ years ago, you ARE a good driver and should receive the maximum discount from your insurance company. AARP and Hartford already have this information, so it appears that TrueLane’s true purpose is to reduce your discount for your next years insurance premium.
