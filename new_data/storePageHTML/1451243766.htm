Description

Now with Always-on support

Lyd is a full Sonos remote on your Apple Watch. You can control your playing music, change the playlist or radio station and even send audio messages from your wrist to any speaker in your household.

Lyd is fully compatible with the Sonos S2 update and backwards compatible with Sonos S1.

List of features:
• Play/Pause and Skip for current music
• Change the volume of Sonos groups (rooms)
• Add or remove speakers from a group
• Start playlists and radio stations
• Automatic detection of your Sonos speakers
• VoiceOver support
• Control the volume of every speaker in a group
• The main menu shows which rooms are playing
• Siri Watch Face integration
• Complications for all Apple Watch faces, which show the current playing room
• Multiple Sonos households are supported
• Seek ± 30s when playing a podcast / audiobook
• Play voice messages on a speaker
• Changing play modes: Shuffle, crossfade, repeat
• Home theater settings: Night Mode, Speech enhancements
• Sign in is possible without an iPhone App

Lyd [ly:d] is Norwegian and translates to "sound" in English

Warning: Lyd only works if you have an Apple Watch (watchOS 5.2 or newer). A Sonos system is mandatory.
You cannot control other wireless speakers than Sonos.

Sonos is a Trademark of Sonos Inc.
This is not an official Sonos App.

Shimmy74 , 05/02/2020

A must-buy for a Sonos user

There are a lot of apps that don’t seem natural on an Apple Watch. Lyd is NOT one of them. This app has become my go-to tool for managing the Sonos speakers in the house particularly when I’m doing construction or cleaning. The best feature is just being able to turn down the volume quickly when someone arrives that I need to talk to.

Like any app that depends on other apps or systems to work, it can sometimes be slow to initially figure out what is playing where and give me the options to stop/start/change volume. I have 3 rooms setup and would really like it to check play status in the background, so when I open it, it’s got all of the current room data ready.

Overall it’s always getting better, continues to be one of my favorite apps, and would benefit from knowing room play status immediately upon being opened.
