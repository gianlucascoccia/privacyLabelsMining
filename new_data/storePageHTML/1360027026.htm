Description

E6 CONNECT is connecting golfers, gamers and fans to the future of golf. Experience famous courses, exciting games, and online competition with E6 CONNECT. #GolfConnected

*Supported iOS devices: iPad Pro Gen. 1 or newer or iPhone 8 or newer. E6 CONNECT may not run well on older or weaker devices such as the iPad Air or Mini devices.*

E6 CONNECT is the highest-quality and most lifelike and customizable golf simulator ever created. The game offers full tee-to-green simulation, world-class golf courses, a full suite of Pitching, Chipping, and Putting Ranges, Swing Analysis, Mini Games, and more!

***** E6 CONNECT Requires a Golf Simulator or Launch Monitor to use *****

E6 CONNECT Features:

-Local Multiplayer
-Several Modes of Play
-NEW! Events - Online Tournaments
-Player Profiles & Statistic Tracking
-Shot Replays
-Peer-to-Peer Connectivity
-Adjustable Stimp Rating
-Atmosphere Settings
-Adjustable Green and Fairways
-Flexible Putting Options and Gimmes
-3D Recreations of 90+ World-Class Courses
-18+ Driving, Pitching, Chipping, and Putting Ranges

Stavjoki , 04/04/2020

Falls Short for $300 a year

There are several quirks and shortcomings that make E6 connect not worth $300 a year.

1.) You can not have a custom set of clubs. You are stuck with Driver, 3 & 5 wood, 4 hybrid, 4-9 iron, PW, GW, SW, putter. My clubs are Driver, 5 wood, 3-5 hybrid, 6-9 iron, PW, GW, 56 & 60 degree, putter. This makes using the driving range to get data on your bag of clubs impossible. So I don’t use the driving range.

2.) Sand shots are a joke. You have to hit them like a regular chip shot and then they aren’t predictable. The same swing might go twice as far as the last time. It’s frustrating.

3.) Putting isn’t even worth it. It’s extremely difficult to guess what 50, 30, 20 feet is in a virtual setting. I’d prefer having the option of having a chipping target like some programs offer, instead of putting.

4.) Changing your aiming direct is incredibly problematic. Point the arrow in the direction you want isn’t that bad, but trying to determine a layup yardage past 100 yards is unbearably finicky. The target post move of the screen after 100 yards and to try and control it you have to roll your finger tip to keep the target post from flying off the screen. It’s ridiculous.

5.) I’ve played 4 rounds and had two of them completely crash on the back nine. The app just closes. The game is gone and you can’t resume.

Hopefully there will an app update soon to make this more enjoyable.

They ought to rename it E6 Disconnect

Got this software with my Mevo Plus, which I use with an iPad Pro.

In my mind, I just assumed that once I had the app and the device, I would open the app, turn on the Mevo, and play golf.

Nope. That’s not how it works. What happens is an unclickable warning flashes in the upper left that says “Device not connected.”

Again, not clickable, so very unhelpful. So, you then click through EVERY LINK IN THE APP looking for anything helpful like “connect to device.”

Nope! Nada! You can’t make this stuff up. Connecting to a device it’s supposedly made to work with is an action that is COMPLETELY MISSING FROM THE APP.

So you close the app, turn off the device. Turn on the device, open the app. Move the device closer, spin the device in circles, turn it upside down... nothing.

I finally resorted to just using the FS app for practice, after which I checked the E6 software and Voila! It was magically connected. So, I tried it out.

Distance to target in metric, no problem, think I, SURELY they can’t screw up how to change that!

Well, yes they sure can! When you go to Settings> Measurement there is a super helpful drop down titled “Unused” that DOESN’T DROP DOWN.

So, metric only, because that field is “unused,” whatever in the world that means.

So if you were hoping to get this app, and turn on you Mevo Plus and play golf in, you know, yards, then GOOD LUCK!
