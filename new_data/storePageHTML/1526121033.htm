Description

Protect your castle, level up your heroes and win PvP battles!

Time for a Tower Defense like no other! In Rush Royale towers are replaced by mighty warriors and wizards!

Base defense games got serious... but are still are a lot of fun! Your heroes are the castle defense, last and best hope against a rush of enemies. Watch them rain arrows and magic missiles in a valiant struggle at horde defense!

It's a unique combination of a turret defense and a collectibe card game! Just like random dice rolls decide the fate of battling armies, here you must depend on Lady Luck to win and prevail! Assemble a deck of most powerful tower defense heroes!

It's TD... but with a competitive twist! Rush against your best friends (or worst foes) - the first one to complete the castle defense wins! In a way, it is a real time PvP defense - what few TD games have!

Protect your kingdom! Clash with mighty enemies and fearsome bosses, unleash magic and steel upon your foes in battle! It's one of the most fast paced tower defense games... and it looks as cute as a merge game, too!

Use your turret defense resources wisely - like in good base defense games, the smartest general wins!

Collect, defend and dominate! Rush Royale is here!

Love it!

This game is probably the greatest game to come through in a long time. I love that it’s not biased in the sense that when you merge towers it’s all in the fate of the logic. That’s also the caveat, sometimes you can really get screwed on which towers drop and how they merge, it’s a real love-hate relationship, but I still love this game. I recommend it highly to anyone! You don’t have to spend money to get good cards, you just have to grind it out, but it’s still so much fun.

The only con: when you do a co-op match, if the other player leaves and disconnects, you’re screwed. I wish they had a feature like in StarCraft, because I played so many tower defense games in StarCraft and WarCraft which is why I love this game. But when the other players leave the game, you get control over their units, it would be nice when the other player fully disconnects from the match, you get control over spawning their units, merging their units, and getting the mana for their kills. I’m sure coding all of that is much harder than just making the suggestion, but that would be sweet. I’d give the game 100 stars if I could, great work! Keep it comin’!

Love the game, but there’s still issues

Don’t get me wrong, I really REALLY love this game. Like, I’ve spent almost 100 dollars on it so far. But while I love the game even in its current state, there are multiple issues with the game that need to be addressed in future patches. The main issue is the deck variety. As far as I know there are currently 3 decks and you have to run one off the three to do good, with slight interchangeability between the cards in each. What the decks are is besides the point because that’s not what’s important. What’s important is that there is only 3 DECKS viable for top tier players. 3. In a game where variety is the best aspect of it so many cards being virtually useless is not good and makes the game grow dull after a while. Another issue is how you HAVE to pay to win. And not just a little bit. Like I said previously, I’ve spent almost 100 dollars on this game. That wasn’t all because I’m addicted. It’s because I HAD to in order to play against high tier players and stand a chance. Despite how much I’ve payed so far, I still am only at around 6K trophies and struggling to push because of Shaman players. Devs, if you’re reading this, which I’m 99.9% sure you won’t, Nerf Shaman. Please. I can’t take it anymore. And stop charging us so much for stuff. We’re not rich snobs. Oh, and one last thing. C L A N S.

Puzzld33333 , 09/06/2021

Poorly Balanced Supercell Clone

This game is a poorly balanced mess. The general concept and card variety is fun, but there is no meaningful balancing between cards. Many of them are inherently better than others regardless of how much you upgrade them or how you use them, and legendary cards (ones you basically have to pay for otherwise you will never unlock them) are so much better than others that you’re almost guaranteed to win if you have them and your opponent doesn’t.

The whole game is a blatant cash grab, and the prices are so much less reasonable than other comparable games. $12 for a season pass, and most timed unlock packs for legendary cards will cost you $10-$40. That’s crazy expensive for a supercell ripoff when you could get a lot more for a lot less in their games. There is a decent, fun idea underneath it all, but someone decided to overtune the pay-to-win to make it all about spending money.

I had fun playing it for the first couple days, but I’m at the point where it’s not fun, and I’m unwilling to spend money to stay competitive in such a poorly balanced game. On top of the cards being so poorly balanced (the one thing you can control as you assemble your team) there is no meaningful balancing to the “boss” enemies. Some of them are insanely disruptive and will ruin everything you put together in a match, while others do nothing. Way too much of the game comes down to chance, which adds to how frustrating the poor balancing is.
