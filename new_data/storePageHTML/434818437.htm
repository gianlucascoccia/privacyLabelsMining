Description

The brand-new Jerusalem Post mobile app is the most comprehensive, accurate, and content-rich source of news from Israel, the Middle East and the Jewish World!

Here are just a few of the many features of our new app:



Brand-new UX layout for ease of use
Improved push notifications for Breaking News/Article Linked Push Notification
Convenient sharing capabilities
Frequent updates to ensure the latest news is always at your disposal
Commenting
Bookmarking (Online and Offline Capability)
Swipe Navigation for Article and Section Views
Continued Improvement to Load Time and Stability
Support for iOS 8
Connect to our $5 Premium Plus subscription to receive exclusive content and an ad-free browsing experience. The service will automatically renew each month unless you choose to cancel it.

Send your comments, questions, and suggestions to support@jpost.com

Kwahoos , 07/13/2019

Adequate

This is the only adequately functional Israeli news app I have found. The others are seriously flawed. This one has reasonable news coverage, it’s fairly stable, and load times are fast. The full-screen ads are annoying but usually brief. I wish it were easier to see how to dismiss them. The list of articles jumps up and down as ads are displayed inline, which is annoying.

Beware for those updating: the latest update wiped out all my saved bookmarks!

Ethel Mermaid , 03/23/2019

Needed features

Just installed this for the first time. It runs fine on my iPhone XS Max. I have no complaints about how it runs. And the categories to the left and scroll bar on the top work great. But the Jerusalem post is more than just the latest news. What I’m looking for:
1) I’d rather pay for the app than be subject to ads;
2) I’m really looking for access to the archives, which again, would be worth paying for.
So, nice job on getting rid of bugs, of which I am only aware based on reviews. And, please offer ad-free, & archive access. Get those past editions online!

Jellette , 08/29/2021

4 out of 5

I would rate this as 5 stars, if not for a glitch. 1% of the time, clicking a notification takes you to the story. 99% of the time, clicking a notification takes you to the story, then an ad pops up, and when you close the ad it loads the homepage instead of the story. Leaving you to search for the story, which is sometimes nowhere to be found because it was a breaking story and doesn’t have a link on the homepage yet.
