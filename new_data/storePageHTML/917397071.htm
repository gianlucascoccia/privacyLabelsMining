Description

Get the accountability, support, and tools you need to build healthy habits. Whether you want to manage anxiety, lose weight, cope with stress, or beat diabetes, Vida’s therapists and coaches can help you.

PERSONALIZED FOR YOU
Choose a therapist or health coach and tailor your program to your unique goals and preferences. With Vida, you get a concrete plan that matches your goals and fits your life.

ONGOING SUPPORT AND GUIDANCE
With Vida’s mobile app, better health is in the palm of your hand. Check in, set goals, form habits, and stay in touch with your coach through messaging, phone calls, and video sessions. Vida integrates with more than 100 devices and apps, including Apple Health, to track your progress and share it with your therapist or coach.

EVIDENCE-BACKED PROGRAMS
Vida’s personalized programs include therapy, stress management, weight loss, diabetes, sleep, and more. Each day, we’ll guide you how to develop new, healthy habits and thought patterns.

PROVEN RESULTS
Vida works. On average, Vida members in therapy reduce anxiety by 58%, depression by 60%, and stress by 33%. In weight loss, they lose 7% of their body weight. 75% of those working on blood pressure reduce their hypertension by one full stage. And those working on diabetes see an average A1C reduction of 1.4 points.

A TRUSTED PARTNER
Vida is trusted by Fortune 500 employers and national health plans, many of which cover the full cost of Vida Health. Vida is in-network with Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Illinois.

Extremely impressive program

Vida is extremely impressive. I didn’t think in the past that I could keep up any diet or exercise plan. Certainly never both at once. In the last 6 months I’ve not only lost 20 lb and gotten to my ideal weight, I’ve run a half marathon and started regular meditation. All these things have become a regular part of my life that I feel enthusiastic about continuing. I won’t credit them all to Vida. I started the running program beforehand. But where I think Vida has been enormously helpful is in guiding, monitoring, and suggesting tweaks, and having someone to talk with for support. That’s been helpful to give me the confidence that I can basically manage it myself.

Needs work: the food logging, an essential function, is very slow to respond and does not allow you to easily repeat meals, which makes it extremely painful in comparison to myfitnesspal. Now I just use myfitnesspal. The program itself has extensive information about diet relative to other sources but in my opinion very little about exercise or other topics.

Utterly flabbergasted , 04/13/2021

Program great, app horrible

Im relatively new to the program, but my coach has been a great help so far. I thought she was going to be more of just a “cheerleader”, which so many of these programs are. I don’t need a cheerleader - I’ve been fairly self-motivated for a while now. I need to refine what I’ve been doing to make it more effective, and she’s been very helpful with that (as well as a cheerleader). I recommended the program to a co-worker because I think she’d benefit from it. So good on that front.

But what drives me to comment is the app. It’s little more than a conduit to communicate with your coach via text and video. Otherwise, it’s pretty useless. I’ll get pre-recorded messages when I hit my goals (which I just find annoying - thanks, I know I worked out today, like I’ve been doing most days for years). I used the space provided to record my weight, waist measurement, etc, for days, but when I look at “History” there’s nothing there, just what I’ve entered that day. Hopefully my coach can see a history so she can see how it’s going?

Anyway, horrible app plus decent coach averages out to a 2-3 star rating.
