Description

Discover the next evolution of game management with the Moultrie Mobile Cellular Trail Camera System.

Receive your trail camera images on your smartphone, computer or tablet with the Moultrie Mobile app. Monitoring remote property has never been easier or more affordable. View your game camera images whenever, wherever and however you prefer using the Moultrie Mobile app. The Moultrie Mobile app works in combination with Moultrie Mobile Cellular Cameras and Field Modems. The application enables users to receive Moultrie game camera pictures directly to their mobile devices without having to drive or step foot on their property. Plus, you can remotely change all of your game cameras settings from the comfort of your own home while using the app.

The Moultrie Mobile app is a powerful hunting tool that harnesses Artificial Intelligence (AI) to quickly scan photos for deer, buck, doe, turkey, person and vehicle. With the touch of a button, users can quickly choose to look at buck images only or turkey pics only. Inside that app you can setup custom notifications to alert you when a person, vehicle, buck or more is caught on one of your mobile cameras. App users also have access to and full use of Moultrie’s Interactive Maps. With interactive maps you can completely map out your hunting property by dropping pins of Mobile cameras, treestands, shooting houses, food plots, bedding areas and even for blood trailing in real time. Use the maps feature to pinpoint how bucks are moving through your property so that you can come up with the best game plan possible. The Moultrie Mobile app is also loaded with user-friendly filters to help you quickly decipher deer movement based off of time, temp, date, moon phase, barometric pressure, day/night images and more! Plus, when using Moultrie Mobile you receive 24/7 cloud-based storage of all your images and they will never be deleted. Even if you cancel your plan, the images are yours to view anytime. You can even download high-resolution images of those big-buck pics you’ll receive on the app.

The Moultrie Mobile system is simple: Use the app or go to www.moultriemobile.com, create an account and register your Moultrie Mobile Cellular Trail Camera or Field Modem. The Moultrie Mobile app features a built-in scanner to quickly and easily register your Moultrie Mobile device. Simply scan the label on the inside of the camera door and the app will autofill the important information so you don’t have to type it out. From the same dashboard, choose an appropriate plan with nationwide coast to coast coverage — no contract required, and it doesn’t matter who your cell phone carrier is! Next, add the approved batteries and SD card to your camera and follow set-up instructions. Once the camera is taking and sending test images, log into the Moultrie Mobile app and view them. Now, you’re ready to deploy your Moultrie Mobile cameras in the field. It’s that Easy!

Commissioner Ripper , 10/28/2019

Great tool that could be even better

I’ve used Moultrie Mobile on multiple devices for a year now and overall am happy with the experience. There is no alert that comes in on my phone that gets me more excited than a Moultrie Mobile alert. Year round scouting keeps me more interested than ever before in hunting.

The Smart Tags auto tag your images with what it thinks is in the frame (“deer” “human” “buck”) and are pretty accurate. That allows you to quickly filter out all the images of squirrels that you don’t want to see anyway.

Sometimes the app takes a while to refresh new images but if you wait a couple of minutes it typically works itself out.

The biggest downside to the Moultrie Mobile service is that I lost the ability to capture video. The Moultrie Mobile Modems will only send pictures. I understand that to send video over cellular data would require more data than is in the Moultrie Mobile data plans, however, it seems like you could still have the camera capture video to the SD card but upload a thumbnail image of the video to Moultrie Mobile. My current remedy is to have two cameras at each scouting location; one for Moultrie Mobile and one to capture video to the SD card.

minicharzard , 05/08/2021

Terrible app

The cameras themselves are ok, but the app is the worst, most un-userfriendly app that I’ve had for trail cam monitoring. I’m pretty tech savvy and the way the all is setup it’s almost like a 75 year old guy made it. There are options that would be very convenient that aren’t even offered.
You have to look at your pictures from all your cameras in one library. They are not separated by date, you have to open the picture and see what date it was taken. I’ve had many times where a “new” picture pops up from the past that wasn’t originally shown that day, but other pics from that day sent already. Pictures also disappear from the library so you better screenshot them.
The most maddening thing was when my camera went over the pic number for the plan I was paying for and didn’t reset after the 30 days. It tells you how much longer you have on your 30 days, and mine said 0 for 2 weeks. The customer service rep I dealt with had no knowledge of their product or their service plans. It took about 6 emails for her to realize they were in the wrong.
I have cancelled my yearlong subscription for the 2 cameras and have to wait until Oct to stop paying for their “service”
It’s not the camera and app setup you are looking for. There are several others in the market that are 10 times better. I’d steer WAY clear of Moultrie.

v3gamer , 01/04/2020

Check data plans/costs before buying

Moultrie has always produced quality products in my opinion. However with the new 7000i series, images viewed through the native app are extremely poor quality and the data plans are the largest “catch” from what I have seen to date. I purchased 5 of these cameras and like any outdoorsman wanting to learn what is on their property along with patterns, would like to have more than a few images per camera per day. Running these cameras have cost me more than $80/month and I still reach “plan capacity” and images quit coming to the app. They do still store on the local SD card but the point of having a camera that will send images to your phone is to send images to your phone. After turkey season this year these camera will either be sold or used like the cheap camera models Moultrie produces and I’ll go back to using my Spartan cameras (which send high quality videos and images to my phone with no volume cap nor reduced quality). Hope they are able to correct this data issue but if I knew more about what I was buying before I bought them I would have simply purchased more Spartan cameras.
