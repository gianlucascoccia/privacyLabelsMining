Description

Whether you want to check the current moon phase, moon age, moonrise and moonset times, or find out when the next full moon comes, our app is there for you. Watch into the future and look up the necessary information on a certain date and time and at a particular location. This most comprehensive and accurate application keeps you informed about important sun and moon times and is even perfect for both casual users and serious moon watchers.

Track and read detailed moon information in your city and multiple locations all around the world. Search for a specific location by city/town name.

Would you like to get notified exactly one hour before the moonrise? No problem, Moon Phases and Lunar Calendar has you covered. Receive customizable notifications that will alert you to upcoming moon and sun events you're interested in. Create alerts for your exact location and the favorite cities you select. You can also add moon and sun events to your calendar.

On the main screen, you can swipe down and explore the beautiful interactive moon.

Discover in what tropical, sidereal or astronomical zodiac sign the moon and the sun are.

Our highly customizable widget will allow you to see all the essential information at a glance whenever you need it.

Important sun information is also there. Get sunrise and sunset times for any day anywhere in the world.
Find out when is the best time to take pictures outdoors: golden hour and blue hour have you covered.

Is the moon rising or falling? How much time left before the moon's culmination? Whats is the actual distance to the moon right now? When is the next moon eclipse? Get all the answers in one place. Moon Phases and Lunar Calendar is an intelligent, yet easy to use application that will make tracking the moon a breeze.

Helpful for Earth Moon Earth communications de KA1WHT

I use the application for my hobby, amateur radio. The altitude and azimuth are helpful when predicting where the moon will be at specific times. Knowing the moon’s location in the sky is critical, as our closest celestial neighbor moves 1 degree every 4 minutes. The data helps me plan where to point my parabolic dish to transmit or receive extremely faint signals bounced off of the moon by other radio amateurs.
