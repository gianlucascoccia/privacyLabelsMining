Description

New in Launch Center Pro 3.1, create App Store quality icons for use in Launch Center Pro or on your home screen using Shortcuts!

Speed up everyday tasks with simple shortcuts. It’s like speed dial for apps.

Launch Center Pro saves you time by launching complex actions in a single tap.

Message a loved one, search Yelp for the nearest coffee shop, jump straight to the camera in Instagram, all with just a flick of your thumb. Create shortcuts for email, Safari, Twitter, Facebook, phone or FaceTime calls, Google searches, and more. If you do it on your iPhone constantly, Launch Center Pro will make it easier and faster.

“Launch Center Pro is an app of verbs. Search. Shoot. Call. Text. Add... With a simple tap-and-slide, you can launch apps, perform actions and more... It's so simple and fast that before long your muscle memory takes over and you don't even have to look. ” —Dave Caolo - TUAW

“Perhaps the highest praise I can give Launch Center Pro is that it finally kicked Safari out of my dock...” —Lex Friedman - Macworld

taliwalt , 12/25/2018

Not amused.

I paid for this twice already - before it became a universal app. Just so we're clear that's $10. Now you want me to pay another $25 so that I can use it with Siri shortcuts?
Here's what bothers me the most. All the unnecessary add-ons can be purchased individually, but for the only one that matters, you have to buy the whole package.
I get that you have to make money but what about giving loyal customers a little leeway. Give us the full functionality for 6 mo then hit is with the new pricetag.

Developer Response ,

When most apps do any kind of a ”paid update” they force users to download a whole new version of the app, and give very little, if anything, away for free. We worked very hard with 3.0 to make sure that everyone who previously paid for Launch Center Pro got to keep all the features they paid for AND got all the bug fixes and other improvements. If you don't need the new features, you can keep using the app for free and it’ll keep getting better and better. We’ll never take away any of the features you paid for, but we do need to charge something to make it financially viable to keep adding new features to the app. That said, deciding what to charge for and what to give away for free was a touch choice, and we got zero feedback from our beta testers on that. Now that the app has been out a couple weeks and we’ve had time to collect feedback and consider what we got right and what we got wrong, we agree that charging for Siri Shortcuts wasn’t the best move. We’ll be making that free to all users in an update in early 2019.
