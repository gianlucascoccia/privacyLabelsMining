Description

A news app with no biased algorithms? This is Gawq. Local, national, & world news from over 850 top media sources. We believe that you should have easy access to news from all sides, and without being manipulated by a click-hungry algorithm. Filter the noise and stay informed with 360-degree coverage on breaking stories happening near you and around the world.

- 850+ top sources
- No biased algorithms
- No in-stream ads
- Filter opinions, celebrity, politics, or non-news
- Local news & weather
- Disable sources with paywalls
- Follow your favorite topics & keep up with the latest
- Be the first to know with major news alerts
- Compare how different outlets cover the same story
- Bookmark stories to read later
- Mute topics or sources that clutter your feed
- Clean reader mode for easy browsing
- Review media coverage to help build a better news platform for all
- Dark mode for browsing that's easier on the eyes & the battery
- Discuss stories & see what others are saying
- Compare the latest fact checks across the web

Major media available on Gawq:
- US & World: Reuters, Fox News, CNN, NBC, CBS, BBC, NYT, NY Post, The Guardian, Newsweek, Axios + more
- Politics: The Hill, Politico, American Conservative, Politico, + more
- Sports: ESPN, Bleacher Report, NBA, NFL, NHL, MLB, MMA, Sports Illustrated, + more
- Entertainment: US Weekly, Vanity Fair, TMZ, Billboard, Rolling Stone, + more
- Business: CNBC, Bloomberg, Fast Company, Forbes, + more
- Tech: Wired, TechCrunch, CNET, Polygon, Engadget, Gizmodo, The Next Web, + more
- Science: NASA, National Geographic, LiveScience, HowStuffWorks, + more
- Finance: Barrons, Benzinga, TheStreet, MarketWatch, + more
- Lifestyle & specialty sources, from gaming to automotive, crypto, fashion, + more

Jchasko , 01/09/2021

By far the best news site out there.

Just discovered a wonderful feature that compelled me to write a review. The news consists of a list of headlines. This shows links to two news sources, with a number under it like +18. When you click the number, you get all the stories they curated with a slider to adjust to the left or right of the political spectrum. This is great, because I turn off the lying outlets like CNN NPR *BC, etc. on my main feed but sometimes you want to see what they say. It also has what seems to be the best fact check on the Internet. Very factual and balanced.
One glitch: the news Reader View has ads and formatting removed but sometimes you lose videos, and when you switch back to the regular page view it is very temporary. So I suggest turning off reader view.

Smallest Sparrow , 11/01/2021

Update

I’m going to give this another chance and upgraded to a 4…will change to a 5 if promised fixes come through. Before the app began having issues it was phenomenal—wide range of articles, truly balanced selection, let’s you filter out those behind paywalls. I got an immediate response after my complaint, and fixes are in the works. I can be patient.

MrAnalogue , 12/17/2020

Lots of Potential - Not ready yet

There is a lot of potential for this app and I love their concept but it just feels like Beta right now. Many things don’t work. I’ve experienced several crashes. Lots of spinning balls waiting for content to load. Sometimes it doesn’t load and you have to force quit and start again. iPad is vertical only which is annoying. Favorites category doesn’t work, just spins forever on iPhone and iPad even with 1.01 bug fix update.

I also really don’t appreciate they make you sign up with email address AND phone number to actually use the app. Without signing up it’s not really useful at all. There’s no technical reason you would need to sign up, they just want to collect our info. Is that for a future sale of our personal info? They aren’t clear and they do leave that open to possibility on their website. I really cannot recommend this app until they make a lot of updates.
