Description

Poemhunter app is the poetry library in your phone with a huge archive of poems and poets of the world literature.

Whenever you feel like reading a poem, tap on Random Poem or swipe right, and see what this brings you. You will find the best poems for your mother or for your valentine.

Poemhunter has over 1.4 million poems from over 100,000 professional and amateur poets. We also keep adding a lot of new poems and poets to our archive every day. Continue to discover new poems and poets with Poemhunter app forever.

Poemhunter treasures are waiting for poem hunters.

- Read an endless list of random poems. Find the very best (and maybe the very worst?) of our archive
- Discover new poems and poets
- Add poems or poets to your favorites list
- Search for poems or poets
- Share poems whit a beautiful screenshot image in everywhere

Poemhunter apps library has more than 100,000 poets including Maya Angelou, Roberts Frost, William Shakespeare, Emily Dickinson, Pablo Neruda, Edgar Allan Poe, Walt Whitman, William Wordsworth, John Keats, Langston Hughes, Khalil Gibran, T. S. Eliot etc.

Growthought , 01/01/2020

Fundamental Problems

There are two problems with this well-conceived but poorly executed app: the presentation and the selection.

Firstly, no matter what poem you get, the verses that ride over the length of your screen do not have their remainder indented on the second line. The second part of the line justifies left, just like a normal line beginning. This (lack of) technique doesn’t just look bad. It makes one struggle to scan where a line ends. The reading experience suffers immensely on a phone screen. (I will leave out my petty font gripes.)

Secondly, the selection of poems are completely unfiltered. That doesn’t just mean amateurish, silly, laughable poems sprinkled in among the interesting reads. That means user-submitted transcripts of classic poems riddled with spelling and capitalization errors. An effort at curation would be most welcome.

I love the idea of poetry, randomly selected and assigned to you. The exploration, the surprise, and the possibility of discovery are thrilling, like how a Pandora radio list of old may have flung artists you never heard of at you and thereby teach you of a new artist. On top, this app adds the possibility of a contemporary creative community. But this app lacks the features to fulfill such promises.
