Love this service but there are some annoyances

I found out about this service a couple of years ago via review articles from mainstream media. I was skeptical and tried it out. I was converted immediately. It simply works for me. I only use it to focus on work and there is something magical about those binaural beats. The subscription fee is a little steep but I need this so I am still a subscriber.

I hope the developers are listening because I have a few annoyances I wish they would fix. I saw a previous review that covered the issues with downloading music. I understand this is fixed in an upcoming update which is wonderful to hear. It’s just so annoying to wait a few seconds for each song to play. Along the same lines, the app takes too long to startup. Also, If I’m listening to a song and I get interrupted by another app or phone call, it doesn’t continue playing after the interruption. The app doesn’t seem to be persistent enough. I also find that the same songs play over and over and in the same order. It would be nice if they played in a random fashion. Also, given this is a subscription, I assumed new music would be introduced more regularly. Maybe it is and I am completely missing it. Not sure but these annoyances have made me look elsewhere at other services. I hope these things get fixed soon so that I can continue to be a satisfied subscriber.

Meep Merp 2 , 08/01/2020

This app will eventually drive you crazy

When this app works it is wonderful. The music for the different moods is a great fit. But if you ever want to listen underground or on a plane or someplace without strong cell signal ... get ready to be angry.

To begin with, the app does not download the various music to your phone in advance. Everything is streamed. If you want to listen to anything offline you must plan in advance and guess what moods you will be in and then try to download the song tracks that you may want. This is an unnecessary pain. The developers could easily include at least 10% of the tracks in the app so that thousands of users will not be left with literally no tracks when we are not actively connected to the net.

And what makes it all worse is that the download process for individual tracks doesn’t seem to work at all and has not for weeks or longer. In order to download a track you need to find it and then open it while connected and then click the download icon in the top right of the screen.

But then prepare yourself to see the icon spinning in a circle forever with no indication of whether anything is downloading or how long is left.

I have tried downloading in half a dozen locations with strong internet and it just never works.

UPDATE AUG 1, 2020 - The newest version of this app is in beta testing and I am a beta tester. The download and listen offline feature works! So, once the new version is released I will update my review.

Carlos of Suburbia , 07/28/2021

Missed opportunity: Doesn’t accommodate my ADHD

With my ADHD, I don’t just have a hard time focusing. External structure and tools to help me maintain focus are important for me and many others with ADHD. Paradoxically, however, the more rigid that structure is, the more stifling and difficult to adhere to, and the more likely it will be to do more harm than good.

I thrive best under fluid structures and systems of time management. For example, I usually work on 25 minute intervals, but on some days, I may find that 7 minute or 3.5 minute intervals are what I need. I was dismayed to find that, not only was I not able to customize how long the timer was, but the five options I had to choose from were prohibitively long. Maintaining focus for 30 minutes is taxing. For 60 minutes? That requires herculean effort. At 90 minutes, I’d begin to lose focus every 30 seconds or so. And 120 minutes? Imagine how your brain would feel after reading the dictionary for six hours straight. That’s me after just two hours of any given office task.

Maybe this app wasn’t designed for my particular variant of ADHD. Maybe it wasn’t designed for ADHD people at all. That’s fine; neurotypical people need focus timer apps too. But I think that, since ADHD people are more likely than most to seek out focusing apps, designing your app to accommodate our challenges by allowing more flexibility and customizability may help a sizeable chunk of your user base.
