Description

ScoreStream allows you to share scores, photos, videos and chat with fans all over the world. This year scores shared on ScoreStream are being used to create realtime scoreboard geofilters on Snapchat. Also, Buffalo Wild Wings stores will show your scores, photos and videos as the game happens on B-Dubs TV. Additionally we have TV partners in most local markets including NBC, ABC, CBS, FOX and CW affiliates across the country.

ScoreStream is the largest fan-driven, sports sharing platform on the marketplace. Join other fans to create games, provide real time scoring, share picture highlights of your star players, and have a real time chat around the game. Find scores from thousands of games covered around the country. See how your rival did! We have both regular season and playoff scores and photos from across the country. We also support video clip playback.

Our scoreboard widgets allow you to embed our scoreboards on all kinds of sites. We currently have scoreboards deployed on Wordpress, Wix, League Athletic, Shutterfly, Varsity News Network, Sportsengine, SI Play and Big Teams sites. Our scoreboard widgets allow you to get scores from all levels of play. We also support rankings so you can make a scoreboard that supports the ranking you have for your teams. In addition to the scoreboard widget, we have photo and video widgets.

Scores from across the country show up on our partner sites like:

Any type of organization from youth to professional can be covered. Some of our most popular communities are High School football, Triple Crown baseball, PAHL hockey, NCAA football, and AYSO soccer. We have over 45,000 teams already in our system but if you don’t see your team you can create them right from the app. Team support includes varsity and preps but also middle school, elementary and youth. We recently launched support for Canadian Junior A Hockey and have scores from British Columbia, Alberta, Manitoba, Saskatchewan and Ontario. There is coverage of English Premiere League, Bundesliga, La Liga, Liga MX, Serie A, Ligue 1, and English League Championship.

We have scores in states like Alaska, Hawaii, Texas, California, Florida, Georgia, Pennsylvania, North Carolina, Illinois, Mississippi, Arizona, Alabama, Louisiana, Ohio, Missouri, Indiana, Minnesota, Virginia, New York, Arkansas, Oklahoma, Tennessee, Utah, South Carolina, Wisconsin and others.

We also have settings that allow you to score games for competitive, recreational, travel ball, college, NJCAA, CCCAA, NAIA, NCAA and professional games. Set up your teams and share the results with friends.

Teams have been loaded for MLS, NASL, NWSL, CFL, AHL, FIFA, FISU, MLL, NLL, OHL, PRO Rugby,USL,NBA D-League and WNBA.

International teams have been loaded for CONADEIP, ONEFA, BAFA, IFL, GFL, SAFV,Gridiron Australia,AIHL,ANZ Championship,MLU,AUDL,A League,LVBP,NGL,Brasileiro Série A and many others.

Wild child 79 , 09/04/2019

The App Developers Decide Which Scores You See

Despite selecting only baseball and football as sports I’m interested in, I am inundated with women’s volleyball and soccer scores. I contacted the developer thinking maybe I had done something wrong. Nope. Nicole wrote me back to let me know the scores are based on which schools are in my region, not which sports. So what’s the point of asking which sports I am interested in if the developers insist on pushing the scores of sports I’ve never watched on me? Right now there’s so many high school girls volleyball scores on the “Featured Games” section that I can’t easily find something I care about. I’ll just delete and move on.

Edit: The developer’s comment is completely false. They stated, as you can read below, “you don’t select sports on our app, you select teams or schools.”

The user absolutely selects sports in which they are interested. I have screenshots to prove my point. I don’t understand why they chose to either lie about it or have someone post a response that is not familiar with the app.

Developer Response ,

I am sorry you didn't like our explanation. As I said to you in email those choices when you set up the account are not part of a sports filter because we don't have filter by sports. We are gauging interest in things. Looks like this is not the app for you.
