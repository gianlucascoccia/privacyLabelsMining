Description

Take your place in the world’s biggest motorsport with F1® Mobile Racing, an official mobile game of the 2020 FIA FORMULA ONE WORLD CHAMPIONSHIP™.

Develop and customise your own F1® car from the ground up, or race for one of the 10 official F1® teams, to challenge opponents from around the world to thrilling multiplayer duels. Featuring all the teams, drivers, helmets and the original circuits from the 2020 season, including the street circuit of Hanoi in Vietnam and the return of Zandvoort in Netherlands, F1® Mobile Racing lets you compete against the greatest drivers on the planet such as Lewis Hamilton, Sebastian Vettel, Max Verstappen or Charles Leclerc.

It’s you against your rival with the simple aim to be the first to the finish line!

•AN OFFICIAL GAME OF FORMULA 1®: F1® Mobile Racing is an official mobile game of the 2020 FIA FORMULA ONE WORLD CHAMPIONSHIP™, featuring this season’s teams, drivers and race helmets.
•HIGH-STAKES EVENTS: Race in time-limited Grand Prix™ Events for big rewards, and combine your scores over each event to rank on global leaderboards.
•REAL TIME MULTIPLAYER: Duel against players from around the world in real-time 1v1 races to make your way to the top Leagues.
•DEVELOP AND UPGRADE YOUR OWN F1® CAR: Collect parts to upgrade your car’s performance and find the best configuration for each Grand Prix™!
•STUNNING GRAPHICS: Enjoy the feeling of being a real F1® driver with gorgeous visuals.
•THRILLING RACES: Take part in the greatest racing spectacle on the planet with a console-quality gameplay experience on your mobile.

bruzzi12345 , 02/07/2020

Top 2 if not #1 of racing games

Developers have definitely set the bar high in racing games. Pretty hard to find another with the same graphics, feel, reality! F1 has some issues or let’s say the embedded level of racing. Upgraded to your cars is perpetual / never ends! No significant difference in performance when in the upper levels. Beware of out of control competitive matchups. You can be up 5 seconds and running top three and out of nowhere comes a last place car that is all over the track. Getting wiped out by lessor competition is normal event. Then u get the events and how gold level racers are winning their category by 20 seconds is in reality not real! So the game developer has embedded several layers of keeping the everyday gamer in the bronze and silver levels. Starting every grid and sprint race behind without Qualifying is annoying. More Laps in racing other than single mode would add another level of intense racing. These one lap dog events are boring. Pit stops would add a Dimension of pure adrenaline. Overall hours of fun, frustration and accomplishment rate this game at the top!

Now if only NASCAR could come up with same standard for racing games! Or perhaps F1 could add a Nassar segment.

nicknamethagoat69 , 03/02/2021

It acts like a pretty old game but there can be improvements

So I have been playing this game for almost 6 months and I have to say there are some pros and cons. The pros are that the cars feel realistic, the steering action is amazing and the drs part is amazing. But there are a lot of cons. First of all the graphics of the scenery of the circuits. In abu dhabi and austria the sky is just a pixelated image. It is not like it is realistic or hard to catch. When your driving, you can see that it is a pasted image without putting any effort in blending the image with the circuit. Second is the connectivity part. Suppose you forgot to turn up your phone screen brightness while the game is matchmaking. If thats the case, then you have to wait for the game to match make, complete the race and then you can increase your brightness because if you do it before, it will lose connection and restart the game again. Lastly is the point system. I really hate this part. The reason I hate this is that it makes it really really hard for players to increase their r&d points. If someone is racing and they came in seconds they will have to go through a -30 point loss because they came in second because of a .2 second difference. Maybe the devs should have a point system in which if there is no contact and a clean race the second place winner should have 15 points and first place winner can get 30 points. So yeah, because of that last con I do not want to play the game anymore. Thank you!

happy.go.lucky , 12/09/2020

The Good, the Bad and the Uuuugggly

First of all ... fun game, fun pastime. Great graphics. Fun to “build” my own machine. I give it only a 2-star rating for all the questionable things. If I wanted to play bumper cars I would get a different app. Since when does an opponent in a race get to drive aggressively, shove other drivers, including me, off the track, cause collisions and not get penalized? Instead, now they have the lead which cannot be overtaken. And I’m the one who gets penalized in my driving performance, even if I run a perfect, “clean” race. Similarly, since when does an opponent in a race get to cut a corner, often in a huge way, come back on the track and not get penalized? Or their penalty is so short it is as if they never went off the track. If I do the same thing on the same track at the same corner my penalty is so long that I might as well forfeit the race. If I run a perfect, “clean” race on one of the most technically difficult circuits and happen to win, why is my reward a so much lower in points, whereas, on the same circuit I run the same perfect, “clean” race and lose with a penalty of much higher points. My point is there are a lot of holes in the app, in the racing logic, in the overall delivery of the app. Fix all the holes, make the racing more like the real thing with penalties where there should be penalties, rewards where there should be rewards, equitable scoring where there should be equitable scoring and I will consider a higher rating.
