Description

ZUS is a smart car charger device that not only charges your USB devices, but also helps you locate your car with the ZUS app. When you need to find your car, just open the ZUS app and walk in the direction of the arrow to find your way back to your car. The app connects to the ZUS device through Bluetooth LE. Therefore, a device with iOS 8.0 or newer is needed to use the app.

Besides being able to automatically save your car's location, ZUS can also identify what device it's charging. It can then send the most optimal power and thus charge your device at its fastest speed. The two USB ports can output a maximum of 4.8A at 5V. This is enough to power two iPads or two phones simultaneously. Get two times the ports and speed with ZUS.

ZUS APP is now compatible with certain third party OBD-II devices that can be used to scan your vehicle like the Torque or OBD II Scanner APP. Compatible devices are listed as follows: OBD II / QBD 66 / Vgate icar pro. Others are coming soon. Add this type of device by clicking the settings icon and select the Smart Vehicle Health Monitor, then click on the ELM 327 compatible devices to proceed.

How ZUS works:
· Whenever you park your car, ZUS will save your car’s location automatically.
· When you need to find your car, open the ZUS app and follow the arrow. The changing of green radian shows the direction of your car.
· Choose between the Compass and Map view to locate where you parked your car.
· The app will indicate “Your Car is Nearby” when you are just around your car.

Notes:
· ZUS may not get location in indoors and underground parking lots. Because of ZUS didn’t get your location without GPS signal.
· Continued use of GPS running in the background can dramatically decrease battery life.
· ZUS relies on the GPS signal in the mobile phone to locate.
· Requires iOS 8.0 or later. Compatible with iPhone, iPad, and iPod touch.

ThisThangSucksGreat , 02/18/2020

Amazing Potential

I got the OBD2 for free with a special deal they were running (just paid a few dollars for shipping) and it arrived within a week. I have a Ford Focus that I have been using it with and I love that I can see my coolant temperature, speed, RPMs, and engine load in real-time. I’ve also really enjoyed how it logs my trips and that I can classify them for personal or work purposes afterwards. Another cool feature is the built-in sensor and error scanner that reports if your components are working properly. When you park your car it will automatically place a GPS location pin on their built-in map. When you go to find your car you can use the handy compass feature that will send you in the right direction.

I think this app and the device have a lot of potential. I would love to be able to look back at the line graph that you see in real-time while driving. You’re able to view an exported CSV file but it’s not very useful by itself. I would also love to see a vehicle-specific shift marker feature. An efficiency and a sport mode, when the best time to shift in your car is, provided it’s manual. I know there is a pro version, but I’m not quite ready to jump into that without some more visually-appealing and useful features. Having said all that, I still highly recommend this app and the OBD2 device to anyone wanting to be more in touch with their vehicle.
