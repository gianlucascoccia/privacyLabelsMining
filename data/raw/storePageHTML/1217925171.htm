Allie in the USA , 12/31/2020

Have never had to pay for anything

I’m currently at level 412 and I’ve never had to pay. You simply have to figure out how to play better. There is skill to this game, including knowing that you can replay easier levels to earn coins, with which you can purchase needed bombs or shuffles, etc. to make it through tougher levels. Also, each day the game offers you bonus coins plus sometimes other bonus items for completing certain challenges such as earning 40 teddy bears, etc. And lastly, every few days there are 3 days of a tournament either to earn blue stars or a tournament to complete new levels and earn trophies. At the end of the 3 days, a prize is sent to your mailbox in the game. The prize varies according to your achievement in the tournament, but it’ll be items that will help you to get through the levels such as coins or unlimited lives for an hour or longer and/or bombs and reshuffles. So if you play this game more wisely, you’ll never need to pay for anything.

tr1999 , 03/04/2021

Big hits and and big misses, balky rewards

In the best of times this app is five stars. It is an innovative and fun take on typical mahjong solitaire. The support team is also responsive and helpful.

I have wanted to give it five stars for a long time now but haven’t due to two reasons. In the year that I have been playing, the rewards system has had several bugs or changes that cause you to lose out. You couldn’t view videos to get additional rewards, or gift boxes show up empty or have only one or two coins and nothing else. Power ups that used to be available via certain coin purchases are suddenly no longer available. These bugs or changes last months before getting fixed, or the developer decides to change the rewards structure again.

The second issue is that not every puzzle has a solution, leading sometimes to days of frustration with certain non “hard” levels. I have literally been cornered on some occasions after 5 or 6 moves. In the past you could rely on power ups to get you out, but given the rewards issue I no longer have incentive to use rewards, which means I do not play as frequently. The net result is game usage has gone from perhaps an hour plus per day and 5-10 videos viewed to a few minutes and one video.

plowens11 , 08/08/2020

Fun But Expensive

First off, the game itself is pretty fun. I was hooked from the start. I went over a month of playing without spending money. I kept collecting coins that I barely used. It was when I got to the harder levels that I ran into trouble. I kept having to use my coins on shuffles. I ran out of coins pretty quickly. I decided to pay just $3 to open my piggy bank. That didn’t last long. I then decided to do a cheap day trip bundle. I ran out of that pretty quickly as well. I eventually got a point where I couldn’t move forward without doing shuffles, which I didn’t have enough coins for. I think it’s worth it to spend a few dollars every so often if you really enjoy an app, but I didn’t want to spend all my money on this game. At the very least, I think they should lower the number of coins you need for a shuffle. The first shuffle is 90 coins, and then it costs more if you have to shuffle more to beat a level. That eats up the coins you earn very quickly. I eventually found a different Mahjong game that doesn’t have in-app purchases. I’m kinda sad to leave this game because I really liked it. It’s just too pricey.
