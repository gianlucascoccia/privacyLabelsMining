Description

Make sense of it all with The Times & The Sunday Times. Subscribe today to receive our award-winning journalism straight to your iOS device. Keep up to date with the latest UK and global news developments and discover unrivalled insight and analysis from expert writers. We’ll give you the stories behind the headlines. With UK and global politics both dominating the public consciousness there has never been a better time to enjoy our news coverage and journalism. Know your times with The Times & The Sunday Times app.

Why The Times & The Sunday Times?

UPDATES THROUGHOUT YOUR DAY
Stay up to date on the issues that matter most with in-depth UK & global news, politics, business and sports, refreshed throughout the day at 9am, 12pm and 5pm. Carefully considered news stories, written to the highest quality, picked by our expert editors.

READ YOUR FAVOURITE WRITERS AND TOPICS
Stay well-informed and entertained with our trusted and insightful journalism.
Join the conversation with other readers and our journalists when you comment on articles.
Enjoy collections of articles curated into topics.
Find work from your favourite writers on their author pages.
Get access to exclusive content from The Times and The Sunday Times including The Rich List Best Places to Live, Good University Guide and Parent Power.

ENJOY PREVIOUS EDITIONS
With access to all articles from the past 6 days, it’s easier than ever to revisit that must-read article everyone is talking about, find that recipe you’re dying to cook or read that in-depth interview.

SAVE ARTICLES & READ OFFLINE
Save articles so that you can enjoy them later without Wi-Fi, at a time that suits you.

NOW AVAILABLE ACROSS DEVICES
Enjoy our new app across both iPhone and iPad.

CROSSWORDS & PUZZLES
All your favourite crosswords & puzzles from The Times & The Sunday Times. From Cryptic Crosswords to Sudoku, play the puzzles you want whenever you want.

SkySwimmer206 , 11/08/2018

The Royal Navy of the mind

I rely on The Times through the iPhone and iPad apps for reporting that cuts through the brawling styles and personality cults displayed by many formerly respected newspapers in the socially fragmented, partially woke and yet stupidly wealthy country where I live. The Times iOS apps do a simple job well: Get out of the way, so that the thoughtful, informative work of The Times’ reporters and editors can shine. The subscription price is very fair. The app category list count, design details and typography scale appropriately across larger and smaller screens. The lack of oppressive inline videos and annoying adverts feels like a treasured oasis of Times content, compared to the brain-melting advertising glare at many other news media outlets. These apps extend the reputation and persuasive strength of The Times across the Internet, a bit like a Royal Navy of the mind.

AOHappy , 11/12/2019

love it, period. BREATH OF FRESH AIR!

If you live in the U.S., and want World Class news coverage and commentary, AND have tired (as had I) of nauseatingly biased reporting by the New York Times (I had read religiously for 60 years!), you now have a superb option with the digital or Kindle versions of the Times of London and the Sunday Times. You can expect voluminous yet insightful coverage of world events, including important events in the U.S., unparalleled writing, humor, and, while being sensitive, a refusal to bow down to slavish political correctness. It’s a regular part of my day and I’m loving it.

Perseid28 , 04/17/2019

Good news source hampered by annoying ads

Even as an American, this was my go to news source for two years as it allowed me to skip past the annoying day-to-day political annoyances of the U.S. and read news pieces that were not simply clickbait. This changed a few weeks ago when the app introduced annoying ads that require you to drag the text above or below (dragging the ad does not move the article along), a task made more difficult by ads that are nearly the height of my screen. Additionally, even with my ringer turned off, some ads play annoying music that completely destroys my trust in being able to discretely read the news. I’m especially bothered because I’m paying for the subscription; I should not have to contend with annoying ads in a payware app.
