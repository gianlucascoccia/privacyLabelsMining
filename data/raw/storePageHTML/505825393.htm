Description

Are you tired of having to take your heavy laptop, tangled charger, and clunky card reader with you on trips? Would you like to be able to quickly read and respond to encrypted emails from home or while on the road?

The U.S. engineered Sub Rosa secure web browser supports web site access from an iPad or iPhone with strong, two-factor authentication using a CAC or PIV. Sub Rosa is the only mobile browser available that allows you to access Defense Travel Systems (DTS), and sign/encrypt/decrypt email through Outlook Web Access (OWA). Full access to calendaring makes managing and coordinating meetings easier than ever.

Sub Rosa is FIPS 140-2 validated and approved under DISA's DoD Mobile App Store.

Web, web email, portal, and collaboration site access are supported, including Microsoft Outlook Web Access (OWA), Defense Travel Systems (DTS), and SharePoint, for example, U.S. Military AKO, NKO, and AF Portals.

Thursby is the market leader in secure Apple Department of Defense (DoD), government, and highly regulated enterprise integration, delivering the security features absent in native Apple and generic enterprise tools. Thursby is US owned, operated, and General Services Administration (GSA) listed, delivering innovative Apple products and services for three decades.

You can find our User Guide here -
http://download.thursby.com/ios/subrosa.pdf

Thursby technical and commercial support is by real people, over the phone and by email from Arlington, Texas. Please contact us via support@thursby.com, sales@thursby.com, or +1 (817) 478-5070.

The Sub Rosa app license is bundled at no additional cost with licensed card readers (Thursby or Identive). To use a Precise Biometrics Tactivo Reader please purchase Sub Rosa Pro.

Sub Rosa supports industry and U.S. Government standards including ISO 7816, NIST IR 6887, HSPD-12, OMB-11-11, FIPS 140-2, and FIPS 201 (with an approved reader). Most popular smart card types are supported including CAC, Dual Persona CAC, PIV, and PIV-I.

** NOTES **
• Subscription based feature allowing PDF and XFA documents to be digitally signed using your smart card
• Sub Rosa's Enhanced OWA Client only supports Microsofts Exchange OWA (2010, 2013, 2016, and Office 365)
• Full support for HTML5 and JavaScript (not the same as Java) standards for cross platform secure web access
• Perceived performance depends on Wi-Fi or cell bandwidth, internet “weather” and back-end data center or cloud infrastructure
• Apple's iOS iPad and iPhone operating system DOES NOT support Adobe Flash, Microsoft ActiveX or Oracle Java (not the same as JavaScript)
• Sub Rosa is an integral part of the Thursby PKard Toolkit secure app development and policy management ecosystem
• The iTunes app is an individual/consumer distribution, custom builds are available for organizations

Used this for 4 months now, here’s my feedback.

As a commander I’ve been looking for ways to work around the laptop tied to VPN. Thought this $100 solution for the browser and the hardware was worth it. BEWARE cost of ownership is greater than the initial sticker price.

BLUF: If you have extra money laying around, go for it. Otherwise there are ways to access the same content but better and saving yourself the money. It matters if you care about carrying a 16oz iPad or a 3.4lb laptop.

After 4 months, I realized this is just a glorified way to read OWA emails. Access to share points, DCS conference, and other NIPR-work sites are not fully compatible. Part of this is due to iOS and Adobe Flash, other times it’s because the Sub Rosa browser just fails to load. On top of that, dev wants money by charging a monthly/annual subscription to sign PDFs in their app. The most basic of work tasks usually required. I can do this on my MacBook for free or my work laptop for free. Why bother giving these devs more money after initial purchase? Cost doesn’t outweigh the benefits. The only irreplaceable feature with this is that I can read encrypted emails on the go so if I really care about blotters or PII then this is single feature is what makes this app worth it.

Before you use this app or purchase the hardware look up workarounds on popular websites to achieve the same end state.
