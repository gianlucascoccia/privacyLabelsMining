Description

UniFi Protect allows users to set up, manage, and stream video from UniFi camera networks, directly from your smartphone or tablet. With no recurring fees, UniFi Protect offers one of the most cost-effective security solutions for commercial and residential applications. With this app, you can:

- Set up your UniFi Protect deployment in minutes with a simple in-app camera connection module.
- Stream low-latency HD video that dynamically adjusts to your network’s bandwidth to ensure uninterrupted live feeds.
- View key motion events with a concise in-app timeline that can be expanded or compressed at your fingertips.

For more information, please visit our UniFi Protect FAQ page at help.ui.com.

Decent, could be lots better

The app does what it needs to do decently but the UI is confusing and certain features hard to navigate.

Like... when viewing recorded video the buttons to save or export a clip are irritatingly hard to find. The buttons that show up in the video frame aren’t it and it’s not clear at all you have to click the thing in the lower left that is 9 dots in a square.. the icon makes no sense

Also OMG please stop the gyro motion effect when zoomed in on a video. Or at least provide a switch to turn it off, or honor the iOS “reduce motion” accessibility setting. Some of us have shaky hands and it’s incredibly irritating when I’m trying to look at something zoomed in on a video frame and it’s wiggling all over the place because of my tremor. Not very thoughtful.

stiefel.io , 09/08/2020

Definitely Improving!

Recently left a less than flattering review after discovering I lost local access to my Protect controller from iOS when our internet went down. Of course I was not the only person to find this surprising but I was absolutely shocked at how quickly the issue was addressed by Ubiquiti - Maybe a week later an update went out and Protect now works whether the WAN is up or not!

There seems to be similar improvement in the Network app as well. Just wanted to say thanks for hearing us and jumping on the problem. I feel its important for those of us investing in Unifi to have absolute local control but somehat understand the complexity that adds to also having webrtc for remote cloud access. That said Im stoked to continue expanding both our network and cameras as a result of Ubiquiti making a clear statement that cloud dependencies will not supercede our ability to view and manage the hardware locally.

DoYouKnowHowIam , 02/06/2020

Good but have some suggestions

I was a Google Nest camera user, but started to have connection issues and the fact my video is stored on the Google servers made me uneasy.

So, I upgraded my network to UniFi and am very happy so far. This app has been working well, very snappy on viewing cameras and time lines. Navigation in the app is smooth, no lag at all. Tip of the hat to the iOS developers from a iOS developer.

Few things that would be a great addition:
1. Add live view with audio support for multiple cameras like the web has. This way I could use these cameras as baby monitors, having 4 kids, need to know who is yelling for us every night.
2. Better iPad large screen support, using new development features in iOS you could do some cool dynamic layouts based on how many cameras you have
3. Add notification settings when going to settings for each camera.
4. When tapping on a camera, start audio. Make it configurable in settings.
5. I have a G3 micro and Connection is showing 0 in connection details
6. Add ability to see connection speed/strength over time if connect to WiFi camera
7. Ability to change bit/frame rate from app
8. Add motion gif image to push notifications when there is motion. Also, a image right before it is disconnected. Nest app has this and it is really nice

Thank you.
