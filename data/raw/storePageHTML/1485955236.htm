Description

Pain and anxiety management, but done in virtual worlds with biofeedback! What!?!? Check it out!

“Flowly helps manage not only the physical aspects of living with pain and anxiety, but also offers invaluable support to overcome the mental challenges that come along with it.” — Dr. Aman Mahajan, Chair of Perioperative Medicine at University of Pittsburgh Medical Center

“Flowly stands out in the field of meditation and self-improvement apps. Flowly’s virtual worlds help users reclaim a sense of calm and safety at the emotional and physical level, thus allowing them to reduce activation in their nervous system and pain in their body.” – Pierre Etienne-Vannier, Mind-Body Practitioner

*We know you’ve tried everything.* Managing chronic pain and anxiety is tough to say the least. Flowly works to ease those challenges, and is here to provide an all-in-one platform to help with the BIO, PSYCHO, and SOCIAL experiences of having pain and anxiety.

The BIO : comes from learning how to regulate your nervous system through easy biofeedback for relaxation training
The PSYCHO : beautiful immersive environments with mindfulness voice over so you can relax while you learn biofeedback
The SOCIAL : an in-app community where you can share experiences and connect with other Flowly users

How Does It Work?
Flowly combines biofeedback for relaxation training and interactive experiences to create an accessible tool for your pain journey. There are two ways to engage with Flowly: just on your phone or with a VR headset (comes with Pro subscription). By being able to see your Heart Rate and breathing, you can learn to auto-regulate your nervous system to help manage how you feel.
(Note: iPad not yet supported)

Can This Actually Work?
We won’t say it will definitely work for you. However, Flowly does marry two powerful approaches: biofeedback for relaxation training and Virtual Reality. There are over 1,000+ studies dedicated to both types of therapies. Flowly itself is partnered with UPMC and USC for upcoming clinical trials with a grant awarded by the National Institute of Health.
We have also conducted case studies where we were able to reduce pain on average by 46% after each session, significantly help our case study subjects sleep better, and increase daily functionality.

Dr. Robert Tien, the former Chair of Neuroradiology at Duke Medical Center, says this, “Flowly's experiences help enhance a user's Vagus Nerve function, which plays a crucial role in preventing inflammation, increasing heart resilience, and treating symptoms of chronic pain and anxiety. Flowly is fun and accessible, but ultimately, can seriously improve overall life quality with consistent use.”
