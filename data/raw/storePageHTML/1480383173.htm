Nice app, only a few things to work on

I’m just going to start out by saying, this is a great app, and I love the fonts and the app covers. The fonts are fairly nice, even though I don’t use them too often, but they’re always a good option. I love that the app covers have specific categories that keep them organized. It’s really nice to have app covers that all match and look well together without having to make them all yourself in shortcuts. Also, even though some of the things are premium, you can watch 3 ads and you get everything. It’s really nice to be able to actually use an app without paying. There are only a few things I think could be better, such as the number of apps you can connect to the app covers and the selection of covers. When I was doing my home screen and putting all of the decorative apps in there, I noticed that there’s a very limited selection of apps that I can choose from to connect to the cover through this app. The others, I had to screenshot the decorative cover and do through shortcuts, which is what I was trying to avoid. Also, even though they’re very nice, there’s not a huge selection to choose from, but I get that that will be improved through time. Anyways, I’d recommend this app, It’s pretty useful for fonts and a nice help with an aesthetic home screen!

I copied and pasted the information below:

Third Party Keyboards provide an alternative way to input keyboard data. These keyboards can access all of the data you type, including bank account and credit card numbers, street addresses, as well as other personal and sensitive information. These keyboards may also access nearby text or data, which is useful for improving autocorrect functionality.

If you enable Full Access, developers are permitted to access, collect and transmit the data you type. In addition, if the third party application containing the keyboard has your permission to access location, photos, or other personal data, the keyboard can also collect and transmit that information to the keyboard developer’s servers. If you disable Full Access for a third party keyboard, and then re-enable it, the keyboard’s developer may be able to access, collect and transmit what was typed while the network access was disabled.
