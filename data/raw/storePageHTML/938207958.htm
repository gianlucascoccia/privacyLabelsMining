Description

Embark on a magical journey in this addictive Mahjong Solitaire adventure!
Enjoy a new take on Mahjong Shanghai as you help a young girl and her cat traverse the globe in search of her lost parents. Make your way by pairing tiles in order to dismantle hundreds of layouts. Collect achievements for special accomplishments while taking advantage of innovative gameplay twists like Shuffle, which allows you to get out of a difficult situation, or Firecracker, a tool for blowing up multiple pairs of tiles. Visit the Forbidden City, Virupaksha Temple, the Taj Mahal and the famous Mount Fuji as you fulfill challenging quests and earn gold stars for completing levels as fast as you can!

● Master 2000 immersive levels (with hundreds more coming in free updates)
● Match 1806 types of tiles in 43 different tile sets (with more coming in free updates)
● Earn over 40 desirable achievements and utilize four groundbreaking power-ups
● Enjoy gorgeous graphics, rich sounds and regular free updates with new levels, tile sets and more
● Game Center Support
● iPhone XR support
● iPad Pro Display Support

Forget it !!!!!!!!!!zzzzzzzzzz

I enjoy the game but am often frustrated that it is necessary to purchase game pieces . I am a senior citizen and I cannot spend money on game pieces that would be needed for other necessary items be it paying a bill or buying groceries. When you live on a budget it can make games have to take a backseat. I often find myself wishing I understood the game more. When I first started playing I had trouble following how the game should be played and still find myself scratching my head over what to do next. I realize you are in the money making business on these games but couldn’t you come up with a game for seniors that would not require that you purchase game pieces. Surely you can understand that just because you are an older person doesn’t mean you do not enjoy playing a game but are limited in playing since it cost money to play. If I were a professional game player I could understand the game wanting you to pay to play. I will continue to play the game but when it gets to the point that I go against my principles and my wallet then at that I will no longer be able to go forth and prosper. I mean it’s not like a person makes money off of wins, you are however wanting people to play to be able to play.
I have found on other games that I use to enjoy playing that they are now charging people to pay to play. Where is the fun in that.
Thank you for allowing an older person to vent my frustrations.

Developer Response ,

We are grateful that you took the time to express your concern. Note that the game is completely free for downloading and you can have fun and progress in the game without making any in-app purchases. Our players’ support really helps us develop the game further and create more exciting content.

This game is addictive!

I really enjoy playing this game but I don't understand why there are requirements to achieve a certain number of stars to move to the next level. The timing to achieve 3 stars is generally so fast that I don't have a ghost of a chance to do better than one star. I'd like to move forward but I find myself having to play the same level over and over in hopes of adding stars to advance. Update: I enjoy the extra mahjong cakes that are offered. Last one was required hot air balloons to play and this time it requires surfboards. I'm frustrated because there should be a way to earn surfboards without paying and there is not. Perhaps there's a bug in the game.
Update: I've gotten stuck at the end of another level without sufficient points to keep moving. I don't plan to pay to keep moving to new levels so I'm starting from the beginning on my phone. It's fun but only if you can achieve forward progress. I suspect that when I get stuck next time, I'll delete the game because it's too frustrating to not be able to start new levels.
Update#2: I took about a two year break from the game and just started playing it again. It’s enjoyable but I wish there was a way to ask friends to give you a life so you didn’t have to shut down the game while you’re still having fun.
