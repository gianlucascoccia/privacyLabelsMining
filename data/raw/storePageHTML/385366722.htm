Description

Looking to have Bangkok Post at your fingertips? Then look no further than Bangkok Post app from NewspaperDirect. With Bangkok Post, all sections are presented as full-content digital replicas, in their original layout. The application is free, and you can link your existing account on Bangkok Post SmartEdition to download your favourite publication wherever you are. Buadhai , 01/07/2011

Well Done

The app is free, but the newspaper is not. But, when home delivery stopped in my area I was happy to get the online version of the Bangkok Post at about one third the cost of having the paper version delivered.

The underlying application is well written and easy to use. You get facsimile pages of the entire newspaper, including the ads, the comics, the special features and even the classifieds. Navigation is a cinch. You can read articles either by expanding the text or opening them in a separate, nicely formatted reader window.

Those who found navigation difficult probably didn't realize that a single tap on a page shows/hides the navigation buttons at the top.

It's hard to imagine how this application could be improved. You get the look, if not the feel, of the print edition. Read the Post anywhere without having to worry about lighting or having the wind blow the pages around.

One feature I especially appreciate is the ability to email an article. Email to your Instapaper account and you've got the article saved as long as you like for future reference.

As I said, well done.

Good content but the app lacks

This app has one of the best designs that I have seen in periodicals. Unfortunately it also has bugs that make it quite annoying to use. The paragraphs are often cut off, missing the last line, the pages get "stuck" and often some articles don't even link up between the front page mode and the article mode. It's close, but detailed work is needed.
