Description

Mevo products are designed, in detail, for a seamless live streaming experience. The Mevo Start, our newest dedicated all-in-one live streaming camera, and its companion iOS App, empower you to stream in 1080p to multiple platforms (i.e. Facebook Live and YouTube).


MAIN FEATURES

Control your Mevo
Using the Mevo Camera App, you can control every feature of your Mevo camera.

Instantly Stream to Multiple Platforms
With a few taps, you can stream instantly to one or multiple destinations including Facebook Live, YouTube, Twitch, Twitter, Periscope, Linkedin, Vimeo, and Livestream.

Custom Streams
Setup a custom RTMP stream or stream via NDI.*

All New Webcam Mode
Plug into your Mac or PC and enable Webcam Mode to use your Mevo with conferencing platforms (i.e. Zoom or Google Hangout).

Gesture Controls
Produce dynamic and compelling content by tapping to cut, pinching to zoom, and swiping to pan.

Facial Recognition and Autopilot
Enable autopilot and the built-in AI will track faces and make live edits for you.

Adjust Video and Audio Settings
Control exposure, white balance, audio levels and quality.

Add Graphics
Add custom graphics including lower thirds, corner bugs, and full screen images and video to elevate your production value.**

Multi-Platform Streaming
Stream to one or many platforms at the same time if you need to reach a wider audience.**


COMPATIBLE WITH

- Mevo Start
- Mevo Plus
- First Generation Mevo


SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS

iOS 11.0 or later


ADDITIONAL SUPPORT

Visit help.mevo.com


FOOTNOTES

* Streaming via NDI is only available with the Mevo Start
** Requires a Producer Plan

NoFreeNicknames001 , 07/05/2020

Another live stream ruined

Both the app and hardware are stylishly designed and seem to check all the boxes on features, but don’t do it! It’s nice when it works but it is prone to random disconnects with no possibility of reconnecting during the session. This happens even with rock-solid 5GHz WiFi.

It’s also strange that the app demands that Location Services be enabled. That has nothing to do with connecting reliably to a WiFi network.

If you go for it anyway, make sure you have other cameras at the ready and use OBS so you can cut over in real time when the Mevo quits on you mid-stream.
