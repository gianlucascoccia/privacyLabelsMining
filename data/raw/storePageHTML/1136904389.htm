Review from a Social Work Student Studying Teens

Overall, the Nerve app is a valiant effort at creating a game that can be played in person and online, creating opportunities for peer relationships to form in both face-to-face interactions and online. Lisa Damour, a clinical psychologist, discusses the issue of technology “interfering with a teenager’s ability to heave healthy in-person relationships” and the live component of the app could encourage more social meet-ups for adolescents (Singer, 2016). While there is ongoing research and conversation on “the link between social media activity and psychosocial adjustment” for adolescents, the Nerve app has the opportunity to create “greater connectedness with others via the ease with which they may identify and/or interact with others on social media” throughout its platform (Barry, et al 2017). That said, this first attempt to create a fully interactive game for adolescents doesn’t fully provide a robust enough experience to keep a player engaged within the game, without participating in the monetary upgrade, which may very well be their goal. As Lisa Damour shares on The Social work podcast, adolescents “aren’t addicted to technology, they’re addicted to each other” (2016).
