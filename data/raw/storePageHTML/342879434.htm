Description

The Nespresso app allows to buy coffee capsules, espresso, machines and accessories on the go or at home on your iPhone and iPad.

Do more with the app, with Prodigio* and Prodigio&milk*, the first Bluetooth connected Nespresso coffee machines.

Ensure you never run out of coffee by getting notified on time when your capsule stock is running low.
Ensure the best coffee experience cup after cup by being informed when the descaling is needed or the water tank is empty.
Easily maintain your Nespresso machine thanks to a set of tutorial videos.

Take advantage of the exclusive Nespresso services such as fast and convenient delivery options or finding the closest Nespresso Boutique or recycling point.
Enjoy the perfect Nespresso shopping experience anytime and anywhere:
Order your Nespresso coffee capsules, espresso, machine and accessories.
Stay up to date with the latest Nespresso Limited Edition coffee, machine launches and events.
Discover the nearest Nespresso points of interest on a store locator. These include Nespresso Boutiques, other stores selling Nespresso coffee machines and coffee capsule recycling points.
Take advantage of our extensive delivery services*, such asNext Business Day Delivery

*Availability depends on your geographical area.
**Availability and conditions of each service depend on your geographical area

Great - with room for improvement

Just used the app to order 15 sleeves. Simple, intuitive to use. Good descriptions on the coffee capsule choices. Easy to make and change selections, pay and be done. One downside though, was that the app awarded me 3 free sleeves of decaf intenso capsules at the bottom of my shopping basket (great!) while at the same time giving me a pop-up warning that decaf intenso is sold out (not great). It gave me no option to choose another type of capsule for the freebie (which I assume is a benefit of having bought as many sleeves as I did). Freebie failure. It left me feeling, somewhat irrationally, like I lost something as a result of my purchase. This is probably not a great way for an app to leave a customer feeling. This seems like a fixable glitch in the app, adding the ability to catch when you're sold out of your freebie item and offer alternatives.
Overall, though, I'll definitely use the app again when I don't have time to go to a Nespresso boutique. Oh - one more suggestion: at checkout, include the option for users to request a capsule recycling mail-in bag. Thanks.

bgraham84 , 05/18/2020

Quite possibly one of the most fluid user designed apps

So, without going too deep into my background I’ll let you know just a few things. One, I work at a five star-five diamond urban hotel and have access to fantastic traditionally made espresso on a daily basis. Two, Nespresso coffee is as good if not better than this espresso. Also, the milk frothing device that came with our Vertuo bundle is amazingly consistent at providing fantastic foamed milk. I know the amount of technique and patience it takes for an individual to master using a steam wand and IT IS NOT AN EASY technique to perform. So, that a machine can produce professional results with the touch of a button is the sign of some highly intellectual engineering; kudos on that. Lastly, for the app itself, its a joy to use. From remembering previous orders, keeping information secure, streamlining the ordering process and ease of use, and (admittedly not a result of the app, per se) the timeliness of the orders arriving is hands down some of the best customer service around. If you’re thinking about an upgrade from your single serve coffee machine, look no further. Also, beware when you travel from the comfort of your kitchen and are forced to drink lesser coffees again, as it will most likely be all the time. Nespresso is THAT good.

Fl0werp0wered , 12/27/2020

Just an app for ordering, has connection issues to machine

First, let me preface this by saying that I have nearly thirty years professional IT experience, including advanced technical and system admin support. Pairing the app was simple enough, but where I ran into a problem was getting the app to connect to my home network. It kept insisting I had the password wrong. I can assure you I know my home network password, but of course anyone can make a typo so I re-entered it. One thing I noticed was the Vertuo kept turning off so I kept turning it back on so it would hopefully stay in communication with the app. Then I backed up a couple of screens and tried the steps again. Then I restarted the app and started over. It still wouldn’t connect to my network, even when I tried to assign a static IP, which didn’t work. I then just went ahead without connecting to the network. The app shows my water level is ‘OK’. I can also see my serial number and date of purchase is today, which is two days after Christmas, but close enough. I’d been hoping that if I preloaded a capsule the night before the app may do something useful like let me start my coffee from bed the next morning, but no. The main thing the app is for is ordering. I don’t know why the whole syncing bit other than finding the machine model. And agonizing over the network connection was useless.
