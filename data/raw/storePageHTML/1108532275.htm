Description

With Splice, musicians and sound designers can browse, audition, and create with millions of sounds across all genres from anywhere, anytime. Sounds sync across devices for a seamless workflow.

Artists, composers, and bedroom beat-makers use Splice Sounds to create Grammy-winning songs, movie scores, club hits, and more.

Splice iOS App Features:
- Browse, audition, & search for sounds
- Filter sounds by instrument type & genres
- Try out sounds in Beatmaker
- Like sounds on the app and save them for later
- Sync sounds with the Splice desktop app

Inspiration can strike at any moment. Now, you can make progress on your music outside of the studio, no matter where you are.

Unable to download my samples to my device

I’ve been using a splice for quite some time now, and I have quite the collection of sounds and samples. I got this app when it first came out, but only really started to need it now as my laptop has broken and I’m away from home for awhile. So, I go to my library of sounds that I had downloaded on my PC and my broken laptop, and I am unable to download them onto my iPad. I am able to play them, but when I press the options. Futon and press save sample to files, I’m met with an endless loading circle. I legit left my iPad open on the app for two hours to see if the sample would download. But still it was just loading. I don’t know if my iPad is just cheeks or if this is a problem with the app (though I’m quite certain it has nothing to do with my iPad). Either way, I want to be able to download my samples to my iPad.

Bojangles Butternubs , 08/30/2020

Minor Fixes NEEDED

Firstly, I’d like to start this off by saying thank you for bringing Splice to mobile devices! Mobile production is slowly becoming more popular so I’m happy to see we’re getting proper recognition. Thought there are a few minor issues. Ever since I’ve had the app, it’s performed very well. The ability to download samples directly to your device is extremely convenient. Although when trying to view your Likes and All Downloads, you are met with either a blank screen or samples that you’ve never touched upon before. I’d give the app a solid 5 if these issues were fixed. I was extremely disappointed to find that samples and packs I’d come across were completely lost unless I loaded up Splice in my computer. The searches within the app also seem to be somewhat broken. There have been several occasions where I typed in the publisher or exact pack names and had completely irrelevant results pop up. I believe if you were to optimize this app, mobile production will have another solid base to put it on the same level as PC production. I think this is a business that’s worthy to invest in!!
