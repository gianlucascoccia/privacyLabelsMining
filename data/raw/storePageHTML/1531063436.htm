Description

"Just astonishingly fun." — John Gruber

*This app requires a subscription*

BETTER WEATHER EVERY DAY
What if you could play the daily weather like you play a song? Scrub through a day and experience the shifts in weather over the course of a day like a timelapse shot.

REALTIME WEATHER SIMULATIONS
This is the *real* weather, not a simplistic interpretation. We pull every weather data point (cloud coverage, wind speed, precipitation, visibility) to simulate the real weather in a single scene you can feel. Sunny and windy and raining all at the same time? No problem. More honest, more accurate, and yet simpler.

PLAYS LIKE A GAME, WORKS LIKE AN APP
Next generation user interface designed and built in 3D with animation and sounds inspired by video games. Hear the rain and feel the thunder. Wind that blows rain based on actual wind speed and direction. Real moon phases.

MORE ACCURATE WEATHER DATA
Tap to reveal the underlying data.

NEXT HOUR FORECAST
Know when rain is coming down to the minute.

MOON PHASES
Track an accurate representation of the moon phase.

BIG & READABLE
Big numbers you can read across a room.

WIDGETS
Always see the current weather right on your home screen.

SKINS
Skins change the whole look and feel of the app. New skins and collaborations every season so you can keep it fresh.

Award winning design but shallow (for now)

TLDR: These (not boring) apps deserve a design award. They are simply stunning, unique and fun. But they totally lack utility and much needed theme options.

I love the concept, enjoy the fun design. The widgets are amazing!!

The idea of gameifying a "normal" app is wonderful.

While these apps definitely deserve attention, to justify paying for a yearly membership (which I am totally willing to do) I would really need to see some more themes and/or view/style options.

I would love to see their design on a full page weekly calendar view for the app.

This very much feels like phase 1 of what could be an AMAZING set of apps.

However for now I'll be uninstalling them and using a Carrot Weather subscription and Calzy calculator. But you can bet I'll be keeping a close eye on these apps for future updates.
