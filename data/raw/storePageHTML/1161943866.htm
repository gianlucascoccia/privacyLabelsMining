Arg001 , 12/20/2019

This app really stinks

Good news is many of the play-by-play announcers do a good job.

Those assets are lost on an app that does nearly everything poorly. Most of these comments are in regard to the Apple TV app, but are relevant here because iPhone/iPad screens are just too small to see anything - especially since the camera operators never zoom on the action or players. The only use for the mobile apps would be for data entry or device control of an Apple TV.

1. Apple TV app: every week I have to scroll through hundreds of teams to get to ours. And the app scrolls either too slowly, or way too fast. Simple solution is to add an index bar to the right!

2. No integration between Apple TV and iPhone/iPad. This would allow data entry on the iPhone to control the Apple TV.

3. Apple TV app: no Favorites! This would solve #1, to an extent.

4. No useful game info. A viewer needs several pieces of information to understand the game context.
- what period?
- what is the score?
- how much time left in the period?
- is there a penalty, and how much time is left?
- SOG is a nice to have
Doesn’t seem technically daunting to provide this. When it is displayed it is only time and score, which is almost always wrong.
5. The camera never zooms.

For $30/month they need to invest more on improving the entire platform. If there’s one ‘worst’, that would be #5 - without it we’re just looking at a video, not a hockey game.

Bennihana , 10/23/2020

So Bad It Should Be Criminal

This product is terrible. I could talk about the one-camera shoots that never zoom, or zoom too much. The poor quality of the feed when the announcer is doing his own camera work, the burdensome and clunky interface for finding games or the number of games that just don’t have an announcer. But all of that is tolerable because you want to see your kids play hockey.

But the number of games that just aren’t shown is a disgrace. Paying $30 a month, you expect to get something. But with Hockey TV, there are an inordinate number of games with “technical difficulties at the rink” for which Hockey TV takes no responsibility.

You read that correctly. Hockey TV sells you a product, but disclaims any responsibility for providing the product... so if your kid plays eight games this month, and there are “technical problems” at six, Hockey TV keeps your $30, and you get the shaft.

This app should be de-platformed by Apple, because that’s how bad it is. By giving them access to the App Store, Apple is participating in this blatant rip off.
