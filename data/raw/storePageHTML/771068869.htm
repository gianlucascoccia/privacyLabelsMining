Description

"Piano 3D" is the most popular piano learning & music discovery tool for the iPhone & iPad. Download now and immerse yourself into an unparalleled audio & visual experience!

**As featured on the front page of the App Store**
**Unapologetically Canadian App Store Collection 2017**
**Top Apps from Montreal App Store Collection 2017**

Features :
- Jaw-dropping real 3D graphics powered by Metal.
- Ultra realistic multi-sampled piano sound.
- Numerous piano instruments to choose from.
- Amazing Augmented Reality (AR) effects.
- Huge user generated song library.
- Music Composer: Write your own songs and share it to YouTube, Facebook or with other app users.
- Import any song (midi file) from Mail app and other sharing websites or using iTunes file sharing*.
- Full on-screen multi-touch 88 keys keyboard with 3D Touch support.
- Slow down the song at any time or pause it and start learning it note by note.
- Switch to learn mode to help you sharpen your skills further and differentiate left hand and right hand.
- Built in Audio/Midi/Video recorder*.
- Full core midi support: connect your midi keyboard via Apple camera kit or any other iOS supported midi adapter and watch your performance turn into 3D art*.
- Record on-screen performance and instantly share it to YouTube and/or Facebook right from inside the app!
- Ultra low latency feedback.
- Midi Out/Virtual Midi Out: control any midi capable device and apps that supports background audio*.
- GarageBand integration using Inter-App Audio & Audiobus support.

jwedel , 11/11/2018

Great tech, unusable free version

Piano 3d is the best looking piano app I’ve seen, and works great on the 2017 iPads. The 3d and AR is great tech, Unfortunately my usage is extremely limited as the free version does not let you really get into it without blasting upgrade notices at you.

I have no problem paying for good usable music apps, mostly guitar sims with effects packs. This app doesn’t let me see if I’d really use it to learn. Crippling MIDI support is poor business. Developers do not make good business people. Leave enough stickiness to bring prospects back daily, and start them on a learning path, and hook them for a few months before scaring prospects away with confusing and unclear offers, is this $8 per week, $5 per month, $40 per year ?? information overload. Make it simple, unlock the app to hook people, and then gradually expose upgrade and customer acquisition strategy. This was fun to check out, and shows much promise, but my quest for a piano learning tool continues.

rent-a-spy , 05/30/2019

Love The App!

I really do love this app. The sound of the pianos is pristine when listened to using my second-generation AirPods or the included EarPods that came with my iPhone Xs Max 512GB. The selection of music available is excellent both in depth and breadth. The AR aspects of the app are just phenomenal and a true pleasure to use. It’s like having a dedicated piano parlour within your home, or on your front yard, or in your office at work! I love it.

The app is excellent at what it does with music and visuals, and there are tonnes of amazing camera angles that are like masterpiece art collections, including a constantly changing "moving camera!" My favourite, however, is to position the camera so that it’s looking at the piano chords themselves while the piano is playing. That’s something I have loved doing ever since I was a little boy. The enchantment of watching this in augmented reality is still present, every bit as much as it was when I was a child. I didn’t realise it was also a time machine! Ha! What a rare, true treasure!

I recommend this app without hesitation or reservation. You will not regret it, I promise. Have fun!

Technically challenged , 07/09/2019

Frustrated piano player

I always wanted to play the piano. Is one of my unfulfilled dreams. Now I am just too busy and probably too old to start taking piano lessons. So this is the best next thing. It's format allows me to enjoy playing beautiful melodies and I am having a great time. I love the note by note feature that allows me to navigate through very fast movements. Two suggestion:
1) it will be nice if you put a time frame over the line that marks the evolution of the song. That way, when I stop practicing late at night I could remember next day where I ended the night before.
2) This app is going to used probably by people like me, with no musical background. It would be nice to include more melodies from contemporary artists that have fairly simple (but delightful) melodies, like Yanni. Most people that can play Mozart don’t need this app.
Excellent app I love it!
