Description

"It's a game-changer!" - Staci Perry of VeryPink
Keep track in ANY pattern. Great for knitting, crochet, beading, weaving, cross-stitch, cooking, and more.

knitCompanion is a pattern tracking app for knitters and fibers artists. Our patented* tools make tracking easy so you can enjoy the fabric you are creating, make fewer mistakes, and tackle new techniques. Join the knitCompanion group on Ravelry for ongoing fun KALs and community.

**** Works with ANY PATTERN ****

Doesn't require internet so you can knit what you want, anywhere, anytime.

Basics:
* TRACK your row and progress along the row
* COUNTERS for every project
* LINK to Ravelry & Dropbox
* SYNC with iCloud
* Supports iOS multi-tasking & Dark Mode

Essentials:
* CUSTOMIZE settings and use our popular inverted marker.
* KEY always visible.
* HIGHLIGHT your pattern.
* Add NOTES to your pattern.
* VIDEO links to easily learn new techniques.
* LINKED COUNTERS.
* Add PDF (great for mystery knit-a-longs).

Setup+Essentials:
* SCRIBBLE all over your patterns.
* HANDS FREE with voice commands so you can keep your hands on your knitting.
* MAGIC MARKERS to count, highlight, and color stitches.
* INTELLIGENT CHART RECOGNITION makes chart setup a breeze.
* JOIN CHARTS or WRITTEN so you have one row marker for the whole row.
* REMINDERS for "at the same time" instructions and repeats so you never miss a step.

With free kCBasics you can create as many projects as you'd like from your pattern library and use any of the thousands of kCDesigns. In-app purchases enable knitCompanion's patented* tools for keeping track. Visit us at knitCompanion.com. We also have a very active Ravelry group (knitCompanion). *Patents 8,506,303 & 8,529,263

craftyhilary , 08/05/2018

A magical tool

I hardly ever review apps, but this is a special case. I’ve spent years trying to optimize my use of print patterns through magnets, highlighter tape, extensive written notes, and more. This app has been life-changing for me. It has a steep learning curve, and the new version (this one) is even more complex, so I watched tutorials for over an hour before I dove in. (I was also nervous I’d lose modifications on my old patterns if I converted, but they came through fine.)

Some amazing features: add the pattern key, such as translations of cables, to a part of the screen directly under the chart, so you don’t have to keep referring back to another page, or cut up and tape paper pattern pages together. Highlight your row in a chart, color different rows different colors, add pattern notes under the chart that notify you on a given row as to any other special instructions (especially useful for at-the-same-time increase or decrease directions). Make stitch counts appear in the chart for fair isle patterns. Stitch charts together that appear on paper over multiple pages.

This has dramatically improved the speed at which I can knit complex patterns, and also the enjoyment I get from knitting. It’s well worth the modest annual subscription fee. I can’t recommend it enough!

Skiplet , 03/10/2021

A game changer

I’m a relatively new knitter. I found this product a wonderful and extremely useful app. It took me about 10 minutes of use to decide to upgrade. I found all the instructional videos extremely helpful. It’s so well done and the upgrade is inexpensive. The free version is great and very useful. I could have just stayed with that and been pleased and happy. The tools are just what a knitter needs. I’m a senior and a bit technologically challenged and I’m able to use the app and it’s features without difficulty. I’ve linked to my Ravelry page and added patterns that I’m working on to KnitCompanion. I’ve added PDF patterns not on Ravelry and it was easy, no tears of frustration from not being able to get the technology to work for me. I just started using charts and the ability to make those dinky little charts larger for older eyes was wonderful. Being able to highlight the row was wonderful. Like I said, the app is a game changer. One thing I wish was included would be general knitting reference material like needle size conversion chart for mm to US sizing and the standard yarn size and names chart. When you’re new to knitting that information is not in the memory banks and when you’re a senior half the time you can’t remember stuff anyway.
