Description

With over 30 million downloads, Jorte is one of the most popular calendar/organizer applications.
Jorte has many features and themes to change the UI to reflect your personality and interests.

Testimonials:
TNW: "Seriously, if you’re into customizing the look of your calendar, colors, backgrounds, (...)then take a look at Jorte."
App Judgement: Robust Calendar, Events and Task Manager. Totally customizable to suit your needs.

** Features **
-Sync iOS default calendar, Google Calendar, Google Task, Evernote & you can add sports events, weather forecast & more.
- Apple Watch support.
- iOS Widget support, check upcoming events at a glance.
-Jorte Calendar Sync & backup with Jorte Cloud and Sync with multiple devices.
-Monthly, Weekly, Daily, Vertical views.
-Adjust the starting day of the week; it doesn't always have to be Sunday!
-Customize Colors/Background Image/Fonts-Change the color of the day, bands, event titles etc.
-Set Repeat events Weekly, Bi-Weekly, yearly, even 1st Tuesday of each Month, in any repeating format.
-Adjustable Font Size.
-Calendar Tabs: Group & view which calendars you want.

***-Jorte Cloud-***
Jorte has its own cloud syncing system to sync your calendar, back your data up on the cloud, and share the calendar with someone else.

You can access the Jorte Cloud on your Phone or on your PC, use it to plan your personal schedule as well as for work. It’s the best way to back up your data as well. First step is to get a Jorte ID, Free of Charge, and sign in to Jorte Cloud.

Jorte Cloud - https://jorte.net/

*Attention*: If you are syncing with iOS default Calendar or Google calendar through iOS Calendar, your previous data shows depending on the length of the sync
setting of in iOS Settings app. Please go to: Phone’s Settings Settings > Mail, Contacts, Calendars > SYNC > and select the length of the calendar data you want, from 2 Weeks Back, 6 Months Back, or All Events.
－>Then the previous data you’ve input will start to reappear on your calendar as event data was never deleted, but was hidden based on this setting.
