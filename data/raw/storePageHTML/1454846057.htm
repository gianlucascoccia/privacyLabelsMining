Description

Rally, the new free-to-play game from MLB, lets you call the shots. Make live game predictions, earn points , and climb the leaderboard to compete for daily cash prizes. Trust your gut and play along, predicting live results as baseball’s stars take the field.

****Rally Features (iPhone)****

Make pre-game and at-bat predictions during live games

Earn points with correct picks

Follow the pitch-by-pitch action as it unfolds in real-time

Climb the leaderboards by earning the most points

Earn cash prizes each day by making the best predictions

No payment necessary to play and be eligible for cash prizes

NO PURCHASE NECESSARY. Open to legal residents of the U.S. 21+ to compete for prizing. Entry period and prize for each contest varies. Limit one (1) entry per person, per contest. Void where prohibited. Restrictions apply. See Official Rules, which govern, for complete details: http://www.mlb.com/rally/official-rules.

****How to Play Rally****

Make Your Picks - Predict what will happen before and during the game! Earn points for picks you get right

Follow The Action - Make picks each day across the entire league. Play along with the action and predict the outcome of each at bat

Climb The Ranks - Rack up points to climb the leaderboard and win

Compete For Cash Prizes - Winners take home cash

Every day, winners with top scores take home a share of a large cash prize

What’s New

Oct 8, 2020

Version 1.6

NEW FEATURE: Broadcast Delay!
---------------------------------------
Rally is lightning fast... maybe too fast. Starting with this release, if you're watching the game on another device, and Rally is spoiling the results of the play before you see it happen, you can set up a Broadcast Delay to prevent spoilers. Setting it up is simple: Click the Delay Icon on the Games tab, choose where you're watching the game, and either go with the suggested delay amount, or fine tune it to make it perfect. No more spoilers!

Addicted and Fun but Scoring flaws

I paid more attention to the MLB more than ever before because of this app. Don't get me wrong, I've always loved the great game of baseball. This app turned me into a analytics nerd.

Scoring needs to changed. A lot of the home runs a way overvalued. You're nailing all your picks, creating some great streaks, 1st place all day.. Feeling very confident that you've got it locked up. Then BAM! One HR hit sets you back 3 spots in the rankings because it was 6500 points. Some people select that option EVERY SINGLE AT BAT.

If my prediction percentage was higher by a pretty significant margin, shouldn't I be declared the winner? This is a game of baseball knowledge and outcomes right?

Justin verlander vs YU darvish , 07/27/2020

Major Flaws

Don’t get me wrong the concept of the game is great, however it’s completely unfair and frustrating. First off, if you only watch one game and it’s a pitchers dual, and you predict almost perfectly/dominate your single game... you could still lose. Simply because others spam homerun for the entire game and end up in a slugfest. You have NO shot because 1) you were dealt different cards that restricted you from winning no matter what 2) Homeruns are overvalued. When there are no homers you can predict 100% for multiple innings, and still have less points. In order for this game to be enjoyable and actually competitive would be to either 1) being able to win on individual games ie. only compete against people who are dealt the same hand or 2) lower value of HRs.
