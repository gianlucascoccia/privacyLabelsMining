Description

Full of accurate tools more often found in expensive sound analysis software, Spectrum Analyzer is perfect for amateurs and pros alike.

Spectrum Analyzer is full of fantastic advanced features you won’t find elsewhere easily!

This app offers a fast and accurate spectrum analysis suitable for both amateur hi-fi enthusiasts and professional A/V engineers. This incredibly precise, pro level tool is perfect for tuning your home cinema, car stereo, subwoofers, and more. Spectrum analyser gives insight into your sounds and deconstructs the audio spectrum, showing the levels of the various frequencies in your audio signal.
The built-in sound meter provides high-quality measurement results when measuring ambient noise levels in a multitude of real-life scenarios.

Features include-
● Octave Band Real-Time Analyzer
● FFT Plot - Fast Fourier Transform
● Smooth HD Graphics
● Multispectral Harmonic Analyzer
● Integrated Sound Level Meter (dB-A weighted)

FFT Features
● FFT size from 256 to incredible 16384
● Hold Max and (unique feature) Min peaks
● Linear and Logarithm scales
● Various window functions: Rectangle, Hanning, Hamming, Tukey, Cosine, Lanczos, Blackman
● Max frequency live tracking
● Selected frequency live tracking
● Fullscreen mode

Octave RTA features
● Bars and unique spline plot modes
● Full, 1/3, 1/6 octave modes
● Hold max mode
● Selected bar tracking.

And much more...

jimlistens , 07/05/2019

Great App to Analyze a Strange Noise in Home

We had encountered a buzzing noise that came and went. The noise was coming from the ceiling my office like it was originating in attic. Was it a bee hive or hornet infestation, wind causing vibrations in eves or loose roof components or more serious issue some kind of electrical hum or buzz. I thought if only I had a spectrum analyzer I could rule out an electrical issue if the noise was 60 HZ or a 120 Hz harmonic. So I thought my phone has a microphone and it certainly could analyze sound . So I found this App for my iphone.
It showed the primary vibration was 101 HZ so I felt relieved. But what was it? The room next door was a bathroom and the noise was louder but still coming from the ceiling. So then I thought toilets can cause funny noises. I took off the tank lid and waited until the noise was loudest and touched the float arm hitch was connected to the auto shutoff valve . It was vibrating like crazy and the noise went away . But I still could not understand why the noise was coming from above. Then I thought the plumbing stack vent was just like a pipe in a pipe organ. The vibration in the tank made the column of air in the stack vent resonate.
Strangest annoying sound and plumbing issue I have ever heard of in my life. I fixed it by turning the float adjustment screw just a bit.
The technology in a smart phone seems to be unlimited.
