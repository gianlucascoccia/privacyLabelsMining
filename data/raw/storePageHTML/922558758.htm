Description

Conquer the world and battle nations from throughout history in DomiNations! Build your empire and command nations as you make them grow from a small village to a thriving metropolis, and battle as one of the great civilizations from the history of the world.

Build a nation, starting as an early settlement and grow through the ages, from the dawn of history to the modern era. Study war strategy, technology and more under history’s greats at the University, like Leonardo Da Vinci and Catherine the Great. Build Wonders of the World and create technology with historically accurate advancements.

Play strategy games as your choice from eight nations and embark on historic campaigns advancing through history. Each civilization, from the Romans to the Japanese, has a special strength and unique units. Build your army then test your historic might against other players in PvP combat, or form alliances to conquer the world together.

Wage war, build your empire and embark on a historical journey of conquest! Download DomiNations today!

Editors’ Choice

Build a nation, establish alliances, go to war, expand, evolve. From legendary game designer Brian Reynolds (Civilization II, Rise of Nations) comes DomiNations, a build-and-battle game that mixes some of our favorite parts of Clash of Clans and strategy classic Age of Empires, while implementing tons of innovative gameplay. As you connect your formidable empire with roads, create wonders of the world, and team up with friends in warring factions, your country will advance through history—from the Stone Age all the way to the Space Age.

Great game, few issues

Let me start by saying this game is awesome. Much more fun and involving than some of the others. Really interesting how you have multiple options and variables to chose from. Only problem I have is the paying, I think people that can play have a very unfair advantage. Only way I could possibly equal it out is with crowns, but those are so hard to get for free that it becomes impossible to enjoy fully. I think it would be nice if you could only buy same age armies instead of stuff like atomic and higher up age troops. So for example if a medical age could only buy armies that are available in the medical age. I’m sorry if this is confusing I just hope you understand what I’m trying to convey. If it seems like I’m highlighting the bad about this, I'm defiantly not. I’m just trying to explain what I consider as drawbacks. Aside from this it is really great. The alliance I’m currently in is very active and friendly. Science I joined I’ve been able to improve my game. I really hope Big Huge Games reads this and can continue releasing updates. Already they have a massive success, I just think if they continue fine tuning it would be even bigger. Again this is just my humble opinion please take this review into consideration and keep up the good work! ( Note: I think this game turns into more of a cash grab as you progress into the ages. Honestly I think we need more ways to get free crowns easily.)

killer kouki , 08/17/2018

Super fun game despite pay to win and bugs

This a super fun game and have been playing it for many months. The ability to build your town and design a separate war base is really great because it allows you to express your creativity and find new ways of defending yourself. You also have a lot of variety offensively as you get to choose from a variety of different nations, each having their own perks and special army units. It’s a social game too. You can join an alliance and war against other alliances in a coordinated effort to destroy all of the opponents heavily fortified bases.

Now for the bad. The developers are always selling armies and technologies that cost real money and give people an advantage in battle. A lot of those bonuses don’t apply to alliance wars which is a very good thing. Secondly, their chat feature is so incredibly buggy that it becomes impossible to coordinate with others or leads to teammate miscommunications which is resulting in anger and confusion. It’s been at least a couple of months since the developers released an update to their chat system and it’s still super unreliable. They seemed to be more focused on selling premium content than addressing core game issues, which is disappointing.

All that said, the pros outweigh the cons by a lot and I still think it’s worth picking up.
