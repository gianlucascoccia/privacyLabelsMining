Mr. Pay4662667 , 12/06/2019

Quite Noice

Been grinding this game for about three months now. I haven't been bored once. It combines the simplicity of an idle grinder with a much better than average rewards system. It does come with some play to win aspects but I've ranked up a fair bit with spending just a few bucks. It's very possible to rank up and get powerful without the need to spend any money at all. The game play itsef is very entertaining. It has awesome graphics, the animations for the varying characters ultimates are very well put together, and the rewards system gives you stuff for basically anything you do. The Guild system is very rewarding, you can clan up with your friends or just join a random one. You fight guild bosses, do guild wars, donate hero fragments (what you use to summon a hero), and collect "tarot" cards, all of which give rewards. The competitive aspect of the game is simple but fun and effective. Overall the game is definitely a Five Star game. If you're reading this as someone looking to try it out, I definitely recommend it.

RKSpear , 11/11/2019

Fun at first, but too many updates too soon

I decided to check out this game a few months back. It was fun at first, especially for an idle game. But recently, the developers have just constantly rolled out one update after another, to the point where it’s just tiresome and annoying. The constant updates and changes to the home screen interface are tedious, and frankly unnecessary. Not to mention the changes they made to some of the various game modes (labyrinth, fusion shrine) and the milestone/reward systems associated with them being changed in ways that were unnecessary and in no need for any improvements to begin with. In an effort to offer improvements, the devs fell into the age old trap of trying to “fix” something that was never broken to begin with.

Through the unnecessary insistence to keep adding new bells and whistles, the game has really just turned into a jumbled mess. For players who don’t mind investing a few dollars in a game here and there, I will say that the prices of the various “subscription” bundles are quite reasonable, however, with all the recent updates and game modes being added I have a sneaking suspicion that they will soon become a necessary investment for anyone who wants to keep advancing and staying competitive in the game. I will likely keep playing for another two weeks or so, since I did sign up for the starlight pass, but once that’s done and payed out, my interest in this game will have probably run out as well.
