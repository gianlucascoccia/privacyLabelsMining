Description

At Chopt, we go to crazy lengths to create the most flavorful salads imaginable. We travel near and far to pair the highest quality local ingredients with the most craveable recipes we find around the globe. But you? You should have the world at your fingertips. This app ensures that nothing gets in the way of you and your favorite salad.

Place orders for pick up, delivery or dine in and pay through your phone, all while earning exclusive rewards for every transaction. Here are some of the awesome features you'll get through our app:

- Earn rewards for each and every transaction
- Avoid the line! Order from your phone and your salad will be waiting for you
- Pay with your phone - no wallet required!
- Find your nearest Chopt
- Keep up to date with our latest travel and news
- Check out our menu, nutrition & allergy information

Questions or comments? We're listening. Find us on Facebook (https://www.facebook.com/choptsalad), Twitter (@chopt), Instagram (@choptsalad), or email chopt@thelevelup.com

Salads are great but...

I was confused about this app initially. The $150 limit monthly billing is a little credit account. You can order like normal and at the end of the month pay your bill. If it exceeds or reaches $150 within one month, you will be at that time. You can opt to pay as you go, but you’ll have to call LevelUp to have them set that up for you manually. And for the first month, you’ll be charged as you order to ensure that the card can be trusted to pay up. Sort of like a month long credit check. This is to save companies on credit processing fees. It’s less expensive to make one $150 charge than fifteen $10 charges. I dig it.

Chopt App , 01/11/2020

New Look and Feel is Inefficient

The new Chopt app is designed to make ordering an extremely inefficient affair, compared to the prior version. Gone are the “Past Orders” section and the tappable tiles under each category. Gone is the ability to quickly see the available menu items under each category. You now have to scroll through multiple items to find what you want. You’ve introduced unnecessary taps in the ordering process. All these extra user actions indicate that you aim to grow the amount of time it takes for a customer to complete a transaction in the app, compared to the prior version. This is a poor decision, because now customers will no longer be able to place orders in less than 30 seconds. How many customers will you lose? How many app users will you annoy? How many fewer orders will you now need to only fill? I can’t believe Chopt decided to allow app-based orders to be become more inefficient—who is going to use your app now? Ordering on your website will now be faster.

This app is a nightmare! If you order the same thing every time, it stores it for a while and then randomly erases it. If you try to correct a typo in the notes, it erases the whole note.
