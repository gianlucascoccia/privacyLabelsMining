nara909 , 05/26/2020

Evil, clumsy marketing

So I sign up for the free trial. I’ve yet to do the first course and I’ve been spammed a bit more than I’d like. That’s fine. But boy is the language of some of the communications aggressive. TO THE MARKETING DEPARTMENT: guys, I don’t know if fear mongering is a tactic you adopted when covid-19 hit or if it’s a staple of your strategy but you have to stop weaponising users’ anxiety about their future. It is one thing to identify pain points and to try to address them. It is entirely different to keep telling users their future is uncertain every time you interact with them. These users are a) doing this bc they DO worry about their future and are looking for the most valuable tools out there to help, or b) have a bit of free time and an interest in a particular subject and come to you with a positive attitude, excitement and a sense of wonder. Your marketing strategy is off putting to either of those user types. For “b” it’s just a way to crush their excitement and start things on a gloomy note, for “a” do I even need to say? It’s cruel and, perhaps more importantly, pretty transparent. Don’t patronise your users. Don’t link your product to negative emotions. Yes, we live in uncertain times, those who profit from it won’t be remembered fondly.
The content so far seems interesting and they replied to this review right away.

Developer Response ,

We are really sorry to hear you had a poor experience. The product team are looking to improve the journey on the apps, I will pass this feedback on to them.

Strange but not all bad

Ok first off, I am learning from the lessons. I signed up for the free trial. I ended up getting the toolkit because it seemed mandatory, not true but it had already charged me for it. I’ve done 3 classes so far and I like them. I wish you could go at your own pace and not just 2 classes per week. I’ve got more time and would love to do more if I can.
My complaints though are 1.) the app doesn’t work. It literally is blank. It shows a drop down menu at the top for “ok going” and “on demand” and then at the bottom you can add courses or go to the profile. But even the profile screen is blank. The app I would give no stars to. 2.) The marketing is super spammy. From unknown calls frequently on class days, text messages and emails. I turned off the app notifications because that was about the only thing that worked on the app but they’re annoying. Way too spammy. You’re pushing people away hardcore. 3.) The video wouldn’t play on my computer so I watch them from my phone web browser. I can’t enlarge the screen correctly so I have to pinch and zoom that way and it’s still weird to view. 4.) I wish it would show what tools are needed when you sign up. I couldn’t find anywhere a supply list or a syllabus type thing. Would be helpful.
Definitely a lot of work to be done but being able to take some of the classes free at first and see if it’s worth it to you is cool. Room to grow for sure.
