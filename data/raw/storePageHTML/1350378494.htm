Description

Join millions of fans of the fantastically popular anime –“Is It Wrong to Try to Pick Up Girls in a Dungeon?”–in its first-ever fully-localized mobile RPG, Memoria Freese!
The DanMachi Light Novel has sold more than 12 million copies.

Memoria Freese transports you to the world of Orario where you can build your own powerful team to battle through a sprawling dungeon! Play in a dynamic timeline that changes everything from environments to character dialogue depending on the time of day you play!

Featuring:
● Level up your favorite characters and unlock new skills!
● An unparalleled mobile game adaption!
● Exclusive stories written by series creator Fujino Ōmori!
● Brand new costumes and character designs!
● Original voice actors from the anime!
● Online PVP battles!
● Dress up your favorite characters in unlockable special costumes!
● Create your own tactical team with your favorite characters!
● Explosive super attacks!
● Test your might with Normal, Hard, and Very Hard difficulty ratings!
● Gauge your strength progression in monthly challenges!

Join millions of players worldwide in this global mobile RPG sensation!

Familia request

I’ve been playing this game since it was first released and loved how it has progressed thus far. But I did just get get from a bit of a break to see an update for the third anniversary! Congrats on that! However during my few moments of play I mostly saw some visual issues regarding the borders that pop up when entering the familia tab or going to check on characters. 1) In the main hub of town Orario I noticed some greyed out boxes with lines through them where one would normally see a number which I found strange. 2) You couldn’t actually see the back button. For a veteran player like myself it was nice since it’s stayed in the same spot and allows you to go back when you click in that area. 3) Last but not least when you go to the interactions tab to change outfits on each outfit it separates into three parts the top which is covered in what looked to be a white light of sorts, the middle which is normal, and the bottom which looks like someone circled some with a black editing tool but it cuts off the circle. I Love the hard work put into this game and look forward to future updates and events. Thank you!! ^ - ^

~ Gamer101

bambibear99 , 01/23/2020

Addicted to this game!

I have been a fan of the show for a little while and stumbled upon this game a few weeks ago. Ever sense my friends and I have been playing nonstop. The game is similar in gameplay mechanics to Nintendos Fire Emblem mobile app but rather than hustling you with micro-transactions, this game leaves you plenty to do a few weeks in and gives you enough free currency from completing tasks as to not pressure you into buying anything to continue having fun. The only things I would want to see change for player convenience would be that when opening pages to browse through characters, shops and episodes, the page after refreshing, takes you back to the top rather than where you left off. (I.e If I’m midway on episode 21 and then beat quest 21-1, The screen will automatically scroll back up to episode 32, causing me each time to have to annoyingly scroll back down to 21 to complete the rest of the quest list), and also adding an auto skip option for quests that have already been S cleared. I’ve seen this implemented in much older games of this genre and would love to not have to sit through a 30 second fight sequence when I’m grinding for items, but everything else about this game has proven to keep me coming back despite the minor issues! I have been recommending this game to friends left and right!

LuckyPoorMan , 04/09/2019

More than a game....

I just had to take the time to write this review after playing chapter 46 of the Danmachi/Date-A-Live crossover. This is more than just a review about the game itself....I am more impressed with the all the new content & stories that the game creators add that help the world & characters of Danmachi to grow & feel more than just your run of the mill fan service anime series when I saw it on Crunchyroll. I have to especially give praise to the voice actors (seiyuu) more than anything else. They give great performances that give 2-dimensional characters with no movement more depth than some 3-dimensional actors in most Hollywood movies I’ve seen lately! I’ve only spent about $10 over the past year on the in-app purchases and wish I could give more to help support the development of the game, but my current financial situation makes that complicated. I do wish it were easier to get the more stronger characters with out spending too much money on Iris (even though I was able to get Eren during the Attack on Titan crossover without spending much money and he’s the strongest character I have). Despite that, I will continue to support this game (as well as the 2nd season of the anime) as much as I can in the meantime and I look forward to more new game content in the future!
