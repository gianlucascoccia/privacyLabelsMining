Description

Express how you REALLY feel. Vent helps you connect to a supportive, positive, and understanding community, making it easy to share your true feelings with people around the world.

On Vent, you’re never alone. Our whole community is just waiting to hear what you have to say. Join now and discover how much you have in common with… well, everybody!

- Express your true self with our broad range of emotions and colors!
- React to other people’s vents using our special response buttons
- Our community understands what you’re going through and will provide support 24/7
- Make friends and chat privately in real-time without sharing personal contact information
- Join Groups to connect with other people that share similar interests and experiences

What are you waiting for? Express how you really feel and get the support you deserve.

The premium subscription in Vent includes a 7-day free trial. If you choose the option Unlock All Emotions, after your free trial, payment will be charged to your iTunes account and your account will be charged for renewal 24 hours prior to the end of the current period. Auto-renewal may be turned off at any time by going to your settings in the iTunes Store after purchase. Current price for the monthly Unlock Everything subscription is USD $2.99 / month. This may vary from country to country. Any unused portion of the free trial is forfeited after purchase.

OmalleyDaAlleyCat , 06/12/2018

Useless turned Everything

I had lots of complications just getting my account set up on this app and initially deleted it on my iPad, but it remained unused on my iPhone. I stared at the little icon after a pivotal moment in my life where I had to stay strong, but all I wanted to do was scream as loud as I could on the highest mountain top... Or I guess a pillow would suffice. Either way, I tried to log in after an iOS update one last time and suddenly I was in.

Right now this is one of the most exciting new apps I’ve seen since GetGlue or TVTime. No, ‘Vent’ doesn’t center around media... But there’s a closeness in the community that echoes the strength of the original GetGlue, but with the supportive feel of TVTime. ‘Vent’ allows me to connect with people going through similar issues, and even narrows that lot down with similar interests. Groups are great, the users are there to build you up, and if you have something tough going on in your life; if you’re battling personal demons; or you just need an outlet to react off that pesky first emotion that hits you in the gut... This is the best kept secret in the App Store. Shhhhhh, lets keep it that way by putting our all into making the vision the developers came up with a reality... TOGETHER.

emilyo2001 , 08/03/2020

The immaturity of the staff is unbelievable.

I have been using Vent on and off since 2015, so I believe I’m more than qualified to speak on this issue. For as long as I’ve been on the app, users have taken issue with the way that the staff runs things. Run by a seemingly tiny and ill-equipped group of people, Vent staff has a more hands-on and personal vibe than most other apps. This also means that accountability is more easily placed on individuals, and users have long feuded with mods and programmers over such things as constant error messages, slow loading times, certain rules (such as the ban on callout/namedropping posts), and the unfair removal of users. Very recently, though, it has all come to a head. Staff have taken to labeling criticism of their app as “hate speech” and suspending/banning users for daring to speak about them in an unfavorable light. Known abusers, pedophiles, and transphobes receive nothing more than a slap on the wrist, while those trying to warn others about these abusive users are persecuted for it. My feed is nothing but dunks on Vent staff. Things have always been tense between the staff and their users, but never more than right now. If it’s not clear, I’m siding with other users here. Vent’s handling of issues is extremely unprofessional, and we are starting to get tired of it. There’s been serious discussion of migrating to another app. If this review actually gets published, I’ll be surprised.
