Lisgo reads web articles saved in Pocket aloud to you with a natural-sounding voice.
With Lisgo, You can enjoy interesting articles while you're cooking, exercising, or commuting.


◆Supported language voices?
English, Japanese, German, Spanish, French, Chinese, Portuguese, Russian, Arabic, Italian.


◆What is Pocket?
Pocket(formerly Read It Later) is a free service which allows you to save articles you encounter online.


◆How do I use Pocket?
Create your account for free at http://getpocket.com.

Then, install Pocket's browser extension or a bookmarklet on your browser, so you can save articles to your Pocket queue in one click.

Pocket is also integrated in many kinds of apps for News, RSS, and Twitter including Flipboard, Reeder, Tweetbot.

For example, if you're using Twitter, you can configure it so that links embedded in a post can be sent to Pocket in two clicks.

With Lisgo, you can fetch your Pocket reading list, and listen to them on the go.


◆Does Lisgo support RSS feeds, Instapaper, or other services?
Lisgo only supports Pocket.

By the way, if you use https://ifttt.com/, you can import newly updated RSS feeds into Pocket automatically.

*Why Voicepaper 2?
Hi, I had been updating the original Voicepaper and Lisgo until iOS6 era, and since iOS7 came out, I couldn't update it because of iOS7 bugs technical reasons. Since then, I couldn't update it and stopped updating it because of tech, voice engine contracts, and cost reasons.

Since then, I'd been paying the Voice engine fees and only doing user support, but a lot of people still keep downloading Voicepaper and Lisgo even though they are old iOS6 eran apps.

in 2017, I decided to develop a new text to speech app from a scratch using a different voice engine for Voicepaper and Lisgo users by combining Pocket, Evernote and Dropbox supports.

Finally, in Sep 2017, I could release Voicepaper 2 just before iOS11 release.
It supports 25 languages with male and female voices without additional purchases, and you can edit pronunciations too.

Speaking of voice engine, if I use enhanced voices, I personally like it better than the previous Voicepaper and Lisgo ones.

Because the previous Voicepaper and Lisgo had one-time purchase payment, please understand that you need to pay again to use Voicepaper 2 so that I can keep it developing in the future.

MaryThomas , 09/27/2014

Still most used app on my phone!

I found this app shortly after it became available two years ago and have used it daily since -- more than any other app on my iPhone. It's simple and straightforward. I've tried a lot of other similar apps but only Lisgo does what I want it to.

Here's how I use it; maybe it'll help others:
I've set up Newsify in article list. I long press titles that are of interest and they are automatically sent to Pocket.
After refreshing content and starting the audio, I keep Lisgo in article list too, and swipe right to delete articles when I'm done with them.
If I want to save any articles for future reference I don't swipe them. At the end of the day, I open Pocket and send those remaining articles to Pinboard, tagging them on their way, and then deleting them.
I use Pushpin to access my Pinboard archives.

I seem to have lost the dark mode availability in Lisgo on my new iPhone 6... I hope that feature will be returned. Thanks.
