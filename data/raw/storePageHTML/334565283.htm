Description

Track your favorite TV Shows with a simple and elegant app featuring:

- iCloud sync between your different devices (iPhone and iPad app)
- A Today widget to see what's coming Today and Tomorrow at a glance
- Notifications when a new episode is about to air

And even more:

- Browse all the episodes ever announced for each of your favorite TV Shows.
- Get a quick overview of all the upcoming episodes and those you didn't watch yet for all of your shows.
- Display as application badge count the number of episodes you didn't watch yet ( count live updated as soon as new episodes air).
- Get the synopsis, a screen capture for each episode.
- Mark the episodes you've already seen to remember exactly what you have to watch next.

I know that many people aren’t very happy with the new version of this app for some reason. Zopefmaw , 05/17/2020

I wish it were free too, but it isn’t.

I had used the previous TV Tracker app because I tried several other apps before choosing it to be the most user-friendly for my device and needs. My only previous complaint was that it did not work in landscape mode on my iPad so that was a drag. So...that is one improvement I am enjoying. For me, I guess it is a trade off of the new landscape mode for the google ads.
I think sometimes we get a little bit spoiled when we are asked to now pay for what once was free. Instead of getting angry, why not feel grateful for having used it for free as long as you did. I am sure the developer has his/her reasons for charging.
If you can find a better TV guide for your device, for free...go for it. I am going to take the plunge. I think when I stopped buying TV GUIDE magazines they were costing about $1.25 per week! In the BIG scheme of things...it’s a pretty small price to pay for a pretty simple yet elegant and efficient TV show managing app. Plus....if you contact the developer you will get a friendly and helpful response.

trevo123 , 05/30/2020

The best show tracker! A great refresh!

I've been using TV Tracker (now TV Club) for a very long time. I recommend it for anyone looking to keep track of where they are in a show.
The app was feeling a bit dated, but still very functional. And then this new update dropped giving it a pretty decent overhaul. Now your shows have very nice artwork with all the info still there. I haven't used the Club feature, but you have to appreciate a developer wanting to push themselves and add new features.
As for there now being a paid tier, my feeling is I'd rather have paid apps that incentivize developers to make great stuff and keep it updated. I've spoken with the dev because there seems to be a bug, and they've jumped instantly at looking in to it and making it right. You're not going to get that from free apps. Consider it not much money very well spent.
This is well worth the price of entry if you enjoy organization and keeping track of your shows!
