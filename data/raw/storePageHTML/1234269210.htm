Description

Lifecraft is the first digital life coach, with dedicated apps for iPhone, iPad, and Mac. And it’s currently on sale for 25% off!

Lifecraft is the first digital life coach. A good sculptor can look at a stone and see the figure within. In the same way, Lifecraft can help you discover the person you want to become. The magic starts with Lifecraft’s exclusive system:

1. COMPASS
Lifecraft sends you messages every day that inspire, motivate and teach.

2. CHALLENGES*
Lifecraft includes a growing library of challenges that will help improve all areas of your life and boost your confidence.

3. EMOTIONS
Improve your emotional intelligence by capturing your emotions as they happen.

4. JOURNAL
Journaling is one of the most effective mindfulness tools of all time. Daily reflection will help you take charge of your life and live more deliberately.

- - - - - - - - -

iCLOUD SYNC*
Lifecraft Pro uses iCloud to sync, which is both secure and reliable. Your Compass messages, challenges, emotion log, and journal entries are automatically synced to all of your iPhones, iPads and Macs.

ENCRYPTION
Your information is always encrypted whether it’s stored on your device, in the cloud, or on its way to the cloud. No-one (including us) can access your information.

FONTS, COLORS, THEMES
Lifecraft doesn’t use special text editing modes or obscure formatting codes. Instead, you get familiar font tools, even on iPhone/iPad. Journal at night using the “easy on the eyes” dark theme or the vintage “Hemingway” theme.

UNLIMITED JOURNALS, PHOTOS, MOVIES*
Create as many journals as you like, including sub-journals. Attach any number of photos and videos to your journal entries. (Free version allows one journal and one photo/video per entry).

WEATHER*
See the hourly weather forecast for 48 hours or daily forecasts for the next week. Weather is automatically recorded for all of your journal entries.

IMPORT/EXPORT
Lifecraft for Mac includes multiple import/export options (Day One, JSON, text). Your data belongs to you.

OTHER
• Password lock (including Touch/Face ID) on iPhone, iPad, and Mac*
• Journal entry templates and tags
• Insert calendar events into your journal entries
• Supports 72 primary & secondary emotions (emotion names can be edited)
• All main views include calendars for easy navigation
• Mac version includes menu bar “Quick Entry” window

Sariko , 04/03/2018

Happy convert from Day One

I’ve journaled almost all my life and have used many forms of journaling: art journals, bullet journaling, online journals (remember Livejournal?)...you name it. I found Day One several years ago and it has been my preferred method of journaling for at least five years. However, Day One upgraded and suddenly their apps stopped syncing, now you need to buy the app (again, in my case), and for what? Minimal features? Enter LifeCraft. Everything Day One is and $10 cheaper with an added layer of providing emotion tracking and an intelligent assistant. I think it’s innovative. LifeCraft has definitely tried to innovate in a genre that is saturated and still manages to provide something new. I appreciate that. I also appreciate that there is an easy import option for me to get my entries from Day One. The interface is straightforward, the intelligent assistant unobtrusive, and the emotion tracking is well done. It looks as elegant, if not more so than Day One. Their Mac App looks great. It synced rather seamlessly with my MacBook, iPad Pro, and iPhone 8. All of that is a recipe for success. Good job, LifeCraft!

Developer Response ,

Thank you for your thoughtful review! I just wanted to clarify how you can change fonts while typing. When you’re editing a journal entry, you’ll see a plus button above the onscreen keyboard. Tap that button to see the text style toolbar. You can change the font, size, color, style (bold, italic, etc.). You can also add bulleted/numbered lists and checkboxes.

Regarding emotions, Lifecraft now has an Emotion log where you can record multiple emotions throughout the day along with notes so you can remember why you’re feeling that way. At the end of the day, you can copy your emotion log to your journal entry.

Thanks for the feedback!
