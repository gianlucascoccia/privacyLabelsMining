Description

Imagine a complete weather tool, always with you, that you can consult at any time, wherever you are.
Weather conditions, atmospheric pressure, wind, temperature, humidity, moon phase, altitude and solar uv rays, you can find all this in The Weather Station. The information is on one screen, so that you have a clear and complete view of the climatic conditions of the place where you are.
The graphics is treated in detail for a pleasant and quality use.

[ Features ]

In detail the components:

WEATHER - weather conditions with visibility and amount of precipitation.
BAROMETER - atmospheric pressure value. You can choose between internal or external (city) barometer.
HYGROMETER - humidity percentage.
THERMOMETER - real and perceived temperature.
MOON PHASES - moon at various stages; sunrise and sunset.
ANEMOMETER - speed and wind direction.
ALTITUDE - altitude at sea level.
UV - ultraviolet radiation level with security guidelines for various levels.

More:

Good app but needs last viewed setting / user defined default station

I live in an area where some data is not reported such as precipitation amount and wind speed.

It would be nice to have a setting that would allow me to set a user defined default station (or last viewed station) rather than just automatically go to the closest GPS reporting station.

As it is now, I have to manually change the station by going to my favorites every time I open the app just to see the wind speed.

Landscape mode on iPad would be nice

Oskrmike , 03/07/2017

Nice, simple current weather app

I just downloaded this for a dollar to check it out. It has all of your typical weather and lunar info on one page, so you can see everything at a glance. It's nice, but would like to see a couple of changes:

1.) It doesn't appear that you can change the units of the anemometer and altimeter from metric to English. I know my conversions, but it would be nice to just see it in feet and mph (even as backwards as the rest of the world thinks that may be.)

2.) The "Current Location" gives you a nice map that can switch between a generic roadmap style, satellite view and a hybrid view. I would like to be able to go here and see weather radar imagery if possible. It's a nice tool, but I think there is some added functionality to get out of it.

Nice app. I will continue to use it. It appears to be very new. I hope to see some neat functions in future updates.
