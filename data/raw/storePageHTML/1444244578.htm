You know, most of the time we think about something and feel different emotions. We may be happy, sad or anything in between. Sometimes it's tricky to understand what exactly is happening and what our feelings are.

This is what we'll be doing together — we'll chat about how you spend your days, what's running through your mind and learn to be more aware of it all.

I'll try my best to guide you through practices of acknowledging feelings and emotions, and share some tips on how to accept, cope and become more mindful.

So, let's get on this journey together!

Features:

DAILY MOOD TRACKER
Tracking your mood and journaling situations that influence it has never been easier—it's a couple of taps a day.

TIPS AND PRACTICES
I'll suggest what you could do about your situation, or how to improve your mood after each session.

PERSONAL INSIGHTS
The more we chat, the better I get to know you. You'll receive personal insights into how you've been feeling over long periods of time—it's a great way to learn more about yourself.

COMMUNITY
Sayana is not only a self-care app, but is the community. You can chat anonymously with thousands of people who are going through the same situations in their life, and get love, advice & support.

Great app in every way

I saw this app advertised on Instagram. I really liked the art and was intrigued by the concept so I figured I’d try it out. I am so glad I did! I do a lot of journaling and the like so getting into Sayana and opening up wasn’t a hard concept for me at all. The difference is that Sayana can respond and give me coping mechanisms. And oh my gosh! They’re so good! The animation is so beautiful. The music is truly relaxing. The tips are actually effective. I’ve seen a major improvement in my productivity and positivity and I’m not saying it’s all due to this one app but this app certainly shared some very useful tools that helped get me here.

The only thing that does bother me about this app is that it sometimes has a one track mind. It doesn’t quite pick up nuance and instead asks you to look inward to find it. Totally cool with that, it’s a good idea, and it helps people recognize their thought process. It’s one of the reasons Sayana is so great. But when I say, “I’m feeling so positive and happy today even though I felt anxious for a bit.” Sayana only hears the anxious bit. And then it asks why my whole day was bad. Which it wasn’t.

This is such a small problem and doesn’t matter at all. I’m just saying that it can be very clear cut but that’s also what makes this app so great. It’s simple to use and absolutely beautiful.
