Andrew Brel , 07/31/2020

Need more friends

Facebook is so bot influenced and censorious there is demand for an alternative. This Liker platform is easy to navigate. Works well and show steady improvement. My sole concern is migrating my 5,000 Facebook friends to Liker. After a few weeks of daily posting I have 30 friends. The same post on Facebook that shows me 100 likes show me 5 on Liker. On the up side, none of them leave Russian troll messages, so I will persist.

Dead Kenny B , 06/15/2020

Almost Impossible to Use

After finally being able to sign into the app, (had to reset my password multiple times before figuring out that you have to go to their actual website and sign in first before the app will let you sign in) I just can’t figure out how to use it. There is no way to invite friends. I guess you have to email or call them. You can sign into Facebook to invite friends from there, but you have to enable the app platform which leaves you open to all kinds of annoyances from Facebook. You can’t actually “like” a post (which is okay because I have no friends on there anyway, I guess.) You can view who else liked a post, you just can’t do it. Navigation between different areas is challenging. When you try to contact support, you can only email them. Well, supposedly you can email them. The link doesn’t actually work. In short, this the perfect anti-social network.

EGray14 , 06/11/2020

All alone

I’ve been interested in transitioning away from Facebook and so I just set up an account/profile. There seem to be bugs with this app that don’t allow me to add a picture to my profile, either through taking a new picture or uploading one for my camera roll. It closes me out of the app. Additionally, there doesn’t seem to be any way to reach out to anyone at Liker. No report a problem button for tech support. It also doesn’t save your selections for personalizing your feed. I’ve gone through and taking the care and time to select from a list of interests that is quite long and I’m getting tired of doing it over and over again. This app has potential but this lack of user friendliness has me thinking I’ll stick with Facebook.
