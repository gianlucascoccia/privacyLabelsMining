Description

Reach out to everyone you miss. Mail your iPhone, Instagram, and Facebook photos as beautiful photo postcards for only $2.

You create. We deliver.

- Thick glossy 4x6-inch postcards printed at 300dpi resolution
- Delivered anywhere in the U.S. for $2 – or $3 for foreign addresses. (Price includes postage.)
- Photo pops out of the postcard as a 3x3 inch photo print (so you can keep it forever)
- Delivered to the U.S.A and Canada in only 3-7 business days and to anywhere else in the world in one or three weeks.
- Choose photos from your iPhone, Facebook, Instagram or Dropbox. Or just take a photo inside the app.
- Include a personal message with emojis!
- New postcard designs change seasonally. Easily customize the colors of any design to fit your unique style.
- Purchase with Apple Pay or scan your credit card at checkout.

- Vacation postcards
- Invitations, wedding & birth announcements
- Prints of favorite photos
- Pen pals

- Just because …

Jerrel Bethune , 06/13/2020

Fantastic. Now they need a letter writing version

This has been great while deployed overseas. Sometimes it can be tedious sending letters, but for events I’ve missed out on I can send something special, like this and they keep it as a keepsake. I’ve sent one for every month I’ve deployed, and my wife loves it because I’ve made them into collectible trading cars for her.

When the covid19 pandemic hit, mail at my location came to a stop. And during black out periods, calling home seemed next to impossible. But when I could get a signal, I sent Postagram a to nearly all of my friends, family, my wife and kids especially letting them know I’m ok. For Christmas, thanksgiving, birthdays and more, I revert to Postagram’s sister app INK to send special made cards. I cried tears of joy when I saw my kids holding the cards up and my wife received hers with her birthday gift.

I love this app. And the INK app as well. I do wish they had a letter app to accompany these two apps, as pen pal letter writing isn’t dead, and I want to say more heartfelt words into paragraphs and not 170 characters
