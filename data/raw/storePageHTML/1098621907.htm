Description

TextMusic is a quick-notate App based on Jianpu ( Numbered musical notation ), all musical sheet elements are composed of texts. It build-in 90 kinds of instrument, and support up to 32 voice parts.

Learn more about Numbered Musical Notation:
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Numbered_musical_notation

Main features:

‣Rational designed keyboard for quick notation.
‣Build-in 90 Instruments sound
‣Exports MIDI, PNG, PDF, TXT, M4A
‣Support Multi-parts, Chords, Arpeggio Chords, Tuplets, Portamento,Tied note,Tied chord,Tremolo,Glissandi,Grace note,Flower note(Guzheng technique),Trill, Loops,Jumps..
‣Musical Tags
‣Varying tempo, Varying Volume, Varying Instrument..
‣Font adjustment
‣Color adjustment
‣Automatic layout notes by inputing bar-line
‣Playback,Loop playback with selected range, Select, Copy, Cut, Paste, Undo
‣Friendly UI
‣Piano keyboard

Totally not sarcasm , 08/27/2016

I like the idea, but not the execution

Let me start of by saying I love this app, it's great for composition when I'm bored, but there are somethings that just aren't as good as they can be.

1. Random crashes - the app just randomly crashes. Ok maybe it's not random... It happens when you exit the app (to the home screen) and open it up after a while. When you try to play the song no sound will play, and when you stop the song, the app will crash. Luckily the app auto saves everything you do, so it's just a minor inconvenience.

2. "Help" isn't very helpful - Help (when I say "help" I mean the manual in the settings) is missing a lot of things, it's also not very easy to access. I would like it if it was right next to the settings button for easy access. I would also like for more info to be added to help.

3. Annoying keyboard - the keyboard is really aggravating, important things like; sharps, and flats are off the side and require you to scroll to get to them.

3.5. The keyboard layout is confusing - The thing I would like the most for this app out of all the things is: Numbers with note letters under them, also, when changing the key signature I would like the letters to have the respective sings next to the note letters.

William Sharkey , 08/10/2017

Excellent

I never review apps but this one deserves to be noticed. It gives westerners a refreshing way to compose music. It is both minimal but deep. It is a really different feeling from using sequencers, piano rolls, etc for composing music, and is much less cumbersome than using western staff notation. It's a good fit for mobile phones keyboard.

I really just want to emphasize that this program is well made and focused on composing. The customized input keyboard is a nice touch.
