Description

IMLeagues Is Changing The World Of Collegiate Recreation!
Intramurals - Clubs - Fitness

Getting better and better! In a few short months we've built this entire app, and there's lots more to come!

What We Do For Participants:
IMLeagues makes playing intramurals, fitness, and club sports much more fun for participants by giving them access to a vast array of interactive features, essentially turning each participant into a fantasy player with lifetime stats, trophies, and achievements!

What We Do For Administrators:
Used by over 95% of collegiate intramural sports programs (and now fitness and club sports programs too!), IMLeagues gives recreation department staff the ability to set up sports leagues, schedule games, track players and statistics, run Greek life/group leagues, send mass notifications on cancellations, make changes from any internet connected computer, and more.

SiberianHawk , 11/13/2018

Ads take priority

Just like the website (which can devour even the strongest computers), there’s so much focus on trying to jam as many ads in as possible that it borderline breaks the app. Schedules glitch out and jump around as ads try and load everywhere. Scrolling around can cause one to somehow open an app at the bottom of the screen. Every time you want to open a new window, ad. I’ve been on IMLeagues for 4 years now and the running joke with my friends is that the website and app’s most functional parts are the ads, while everything else seems to be off the priority list. The app gets lost while you navigate. If you try and go through too many pages it gets amnesia and as you try to hit the back button it keeps opening the same page until you get back home. The buttons are unintuitive and even downright invisible. It took me ages to find out that tapping different parts of a line of text could open up different menus. And this is just the app. The website takes eons to load because it tries to fill half the page with ads. It never creates a proper cookie to remember your information when you come back. The navigation is a maze, and if I have more than two of three tabs open my 16GB RAM desktop will start to cry. But you can bet that those ads will still work regardless of anything else.

Ad blocker? Haha. The one part of their website that’s consistently working is making sure you don’t have one of those on.

Ccope19 , 08/25/2020

Crashes and Cluttered with Ads

Every time I try to open this app there’s a screen that asks to remove ads for $4 and when I hit continue in order to keep the ads, I’d give it a 50/50 shot of crashing the app. When I do make it to where I want to go... it looks like a slightly modified version of their website with the only change being that there are tabs at the bottom (which refreshes the page and requires that payment screen again). I like what they’re trying to provide but their website is cluttered with ads (at least put the content in the center people) and the app seems like a hastily ported version of the website that is ultimately just a web browser that’s restricted to only their website with a mobile overlay slapped onto it.
