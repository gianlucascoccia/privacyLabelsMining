Description

Get super hearing and the ability to record things from far away. Hear up to 200x what your microphone normally picks up. EQ the sound and explore our ((Spaces)) feature.

Hear Boost gives you enhanced hearing and recording ability to easily capture far away conversations. Hear the world around you in an enhanced way like you may have never heard it before. Listen and record up to 200x what your microphone normally delivers.

A recording and hearing tool for journalists, students, parents, the curious or anyone who wants to enhance their hearing.

Our microphone input and voice filter switch make this app the perfect tool for journalists to record interviews and students to record lectures.

Hear Boost can work in the background so you can place your device anywhere with its display off while it’s still monitoring or recording. There is no limit to recording. It will keep recording until you stop it or your storage has run out of free space.

We suggest you use headphones. Without headphones connected it will feedback on itself and make a very unpleasant sound. Use with any Bluetooth headset.

nicknamesarehardertofind , 10/25/2019

Can you reduce the delay?

I was classically trained on piano and string bass and taught myself other instruments. I’ve continued to play and compose as a hobby all my life. I recently have experienced hearing loss due to Meniere’s that makes it difficult to hear pitch when playing. I tried Listen mode with AirPods but the delay is too long. I downloaded the app and purchased the equalizer hoping there would be no delay. It’s much less but still too much for playing and singing while composing.

This app with wired headphones works for conversation since the delay is tolerable. AirPods don’t due to the delay. The delay is tolerable when listening but I have to take the AirPods off whenever I talk, then put them back on to listen. I guess that’s why it’s called Listen mode! That’s OK for certain meetings, or a lecture, or maybe a concert - but not conversation where you are talking. Of course you have to use wired earbuds or headphones.

Developer Response ,

Thank you for your review! So sorry to hear about your hearing loss. We definitely recommend you always use wired headphones with Hear Boost. The AirPods are Bluetooth which as a technology has a delay when using live. We’ll work on reducing the latency! 😊
