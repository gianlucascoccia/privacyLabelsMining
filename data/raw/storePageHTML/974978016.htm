Description

FastHabit helps you start and stay consistent with Intermittent Fasting. Set your fast length, log daily, and use advanced features like reminders, weight tracking and Health App syncing, Apple Watch integration, calendar views, data export, and multi-day fasting to track your progress and accelerate your results.

SIMPLE, FLEXIBLE TRACKING
- Choose how many hours you want to fast
- Keep your motivation in view- See percent complete, time completed, time remaining, and target end.
- Easy editing. Stop, restart, or adjust your fast at any time.

SYNC EVERYWHERE
- Apple Watch integration: Leave your phone at home and still track your fast.
- Save and transfer your fasts to a new device with iCloud syncing
- Sync your weight tracking with your Apple Health App profile

SET REMINDERS AND NOTIFICATIONS
- Choose the days and times you want reminders to start fasting
- Get a notification when your fast is complete

MOTIVATIONAL STATS AND PROGRESS:
- 10 day Snapshot with ‘streaks’ to show how many days in a row you’ve fasted and how many days you’ve met your goals – a great way to stay motivated.
- Calendar and weekly views with daily details
- Weight tracking views with fasting overlay

MORE GREAT FEATURES
- Apple watch complications
- iPhone widget
- Getting started guide
- Enhanced support for longer, multi-day fasts
- Export your data

Fast Habit has been featured in GQ, the Guardian, and Fast Company.

Turrican888 , 08/12/2020

Simple, effective. Apple Watch is killer feature

I was surprised how hard it was to find an app that could work as an effective ‘glorified timer’, Fast Habit is the best of the five I tried. It doesn’t try to bilk you for subscriptions, and the free functionality is more than enough if you’re just getting started (though I paid up for the pro upgrade after a week because the app is so good). My fave view on the phone is it’s calendar; it’s like using Jerry Springer’s ‘don’t break the chain’ productivity hack to keep a streak going!

So saying I’m addicted to my phone so have taken to keeping it powered off in a drawer in favour of the watch, so having the watch complication + modular watch face is just perfect - it’s pretty much how I use the app daily now.

If there was one thing I could add to the pro version it would be for a reminder option that counts down from when you end you fast, rather than a fixed time of day. So if I’m 16/8 but I end up doing 15 or 17 instead, I’d like to set a reminder when my 8 hour window is up (though I can ask Siri to do that anyway so not the end of the world).

Thincor , 09/28/2020

Simple, effective fasting app

I tried all the popular IF apps, and this is the only one I stuck with. Why? It’s simple, and the Pro Upgrade is a one-and-done payment not a subscription. Very worth the $2.99 upgrade. It’s a tracking clock that is completely editable, a weight tracker, and a few different ways to view your progress, including a graph that superimposes your fasting times on your weight chart, so you can see which fasting schedule works best for your weight loss. The fasting clock lets you view your progress by counting down (time remaining + % complete), counting up (time fasted), or just % complete.

You can set a fasting window, but can edit it on the fly in the main screen, which makes this a truly useful app for those of us who do not stick to rigid fasting windows, plus you can add or edit fasts any time (if you forgot to start or stop the clock).

No social media, no need to be connected to the internet to use, just a simple, well designed fasting app. I’ve been using it for over a year now. Love it!

Lalandmine , 03/04/2019

I like it so much I paid!

A fasting app doesn’t need a ton of complicated features. I downloaded a few and the others didn’t work out for me because of their rigidity with times/schedule. Sometimes I eat on accident or sleep late, or I want to fast longer than I intended. Other apps made it hard to adjust for those things “on the fly” while you have a fast going.

This app shines for its simplicity and also its versatility! I’m new to fasting (few weeks now) so I can’t always make it to a “set time”, but that’s ok! You can set a goal (16hrs for me) and then click start when you’re done eating. The app will remind you when you’re close to reaching your goal, but you can easily go past the goal or stop early if needed. You just click stop. I don’t know why other apps make it harder than that.

Seeing my fasts on a weekly timeline has really helped me see the overall picture of how I’m doing and motivated me to beat my best times.

ALSO THE WATCH APP IS PERFECT!
I can easily check my wrist for time elapsed/time remaining if I’m driving or when I need to quickly set a mealtime with friends. I don’t want to always drag my phone out to do that. Also, being able to START and STOP a fast on the watch is invaluable. If I didn’t have the watch app I would likely forget what time when I went to add it in my phone.

Edits are easy too.

I would pay MORE for additional display options like:
different style timers
analog/digital
Color options
Different graph options
