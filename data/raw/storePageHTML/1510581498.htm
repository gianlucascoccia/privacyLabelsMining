What’s New

Mar 4, 2021

Version 1.1.2

We take our reviews seriously and love our #grifinfam, so we’ve made some big updates based on your feedback and requests!

“How do I contact support?”
We’ve added multiple support channels for you! You can now reach out to us through the new built-in messaging system on the settings page in the Grifin app or email us at hello@grifin.com.

“I want to use my cash to buy more stock”
Our newest and largest release to date! You can officially use your cash fund to purchase more stock in the companies you’ve unlocked. Add as little as $1 or as much as you want.

“I want to use the app, but I need an access code”
Thank you to the thousands of you that have already downloaded! We’ve removed the access codes so anybody with an iPhone, iPad, or iPod Touch can get started with Grifin now.

“Why can I only connect one credit or debit card?”
We’re currently working on a way to connect multiple cards. Grifin loses money running the app and each additional card doubles our cost. We’re doing everything we can to give more of you access and keep Grifin free. We always want you to have the best experience, so please reach out to us if you have any unique ideas!

“Why does it take a week for a transfer to go through and buy stock?”
This helps prevent you from overdrafting and incurring unwanted fees. Grifin adds up all of your purchases throughout the week and does one deposit every Wednesday morning. You can track your upcoming deposit in the app. Feel secure knowing how much will be added into your Grifin account.

BluebennyPie , 03/08/2021

Really wanted to like it...

I like the idea of auto investing in places that I like, but I have a few problems. First, when you sign up it offers to let you retroactively invest. I realized that I wanted to change my funding account and skipped this step. It wouldn’t let me go back and when I reached out to ask for help, I was told this was a one-time chance that I missed. Okay whatever I’ll get over it. Then I received an email today saying that I had a pending transfer, so I went to see what they were auto transferring. The majority of the purchases were being processed as “cash.” I didn’t know (until I just looked) that I was also committing to sending $1 for each purchase total, not just companies with stock. I got this app expecting not to have to think about where I wanted to invest money - it would just be on companies on the stock market based on my previous purchases. If I wanted to do a lot of thinking, I’d just go with Robinhood or Stash and get Starbucks or Home Depot that way. Anyway, I guess this is all my mistake, but I agree with those saying this isn’t ready for prime time. Not enough customizability. I also didn’t like that companies are “locked” until you make purchases with them. I’ll be happy to come back if these changes are made... I really like the idea of the app.

Developer Response ,

Hey! Thanks for the review. It's very honest and reflective of where the app is right now. We just launched in November and have been working to get it ready for prime time. We just didn't expect that to literally happen over night.

A lot of those customization requests are coming over the next couple of weeks. Stay tuned for an app update. I think you're going to love it!
