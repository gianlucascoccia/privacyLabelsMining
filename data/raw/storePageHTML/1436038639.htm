Description

Swap and match gems on thousands of puzzling levels to collect resources and bring the ancient Roman village to life!

Travel back in time to Ancient Rome in Jewels of Rome™! A struggling settlement in a remote but beautiful corner of the Roman Empire needs your help to be restored to its former glory. Play thousands of match-3 levels, meet charismatic characters, follow the suspenseful storyline and build this close-knit village into a sprawling Roman city!

This game is a unique and epic combination of city building and match-3, tied together with a storyline full of twists and turns, Mediterranean culture, and an atmospheric and vibrant setting of Ancient Rome. You’ve been named the prefect of a troubled settlement in a distant corner of the Roman Empire. Help the determined residents revive their community after a disastrous betrayal by your scheming predecessor, Cassius. Reconstruct an impressive settlement that’s worthy of legends, thwart Cassius’ evil machinations and ensure that fortune favors your citizens once again!

While this game is absolutely free to play, you have the ability to unlock optional bonuses via in-app purchases from within the game. You may disable in-app purchases in your device settings

Enchanting

I am really enjoying this game. I played a number of other games before I found the G5 games. Now they’re about the only ones I play. I love everything about them, The artistry is beautiful the thought and planning that goes into them surprising. They aren’t as simple as some of the other games you can find around. But mostly I like that they are fair. Of course they want you to buy supplies and stuff that’s why they created the game and I get that. But so many games are outrageously unfair in how much they want you to spend before you can progress a tiny little bit. When I found the G5 games I realized that these are games I could play, spend what I could afford, and really enjoy myself. This particular game is enchanting because of the era, But that’s what’s good about all of them, the themes. I like completing the collections, and I think even the Match3 games especially in the higher levels, while challenging, they are doable. I like that you can earn resources during the game and not have to buy every one. I love the different characters that turn up in each of the games. Overall I am highly impressed not only with this game but with all the G5 games that I have tried so far.

LizHimeII , 08/30/2020

Excellent game! But could it be better?

This game is so much fun. The artwork is gorgeous, with an intriguing story that keeps you plain to learn more. I am currently on the wedding event and I cannot play this and fast enough to find out what happens next before time is up. I also love the use of education. The game has words of Latin for language and references to Roman history. I think future events could build on the potential educational aspect of the game. Perhaps a quiz event where characters educate the players and ask questions to see what they learned about history culture and language used in the game and event. It would be excellent for younger players and older players alike.

Shop_Diva , 03/08/2020

Started out good, but...

This is just like so many of G5’s games. Survivors the Quest was the only exception and they seem to have stopped providing updates for that one, as well as several others. In Jewels of Rome once you progress to level 3 in a location it starts getting stupid hard and after that forget about it if you don’t want to spend big bucks for tools or waste your energy allotment and time trying to complete a quest. Plus many of the collections require so many combiners (up to 90+ for non-event collections and 113 for an event collection; those combiners are not giftable). You need a ton of friends who gift daily because it’s impossible to complete tasks in the locations that award the combiners. The graphics earned the 2 stars, which are generally great in G5 games but that is all the praise I can offer. The support staff are put in a truly terrible position because they really can’t do much, but it’s not their fault. They seem to be great people who work for a company that genuinely does not care about anything but money. I get it; it’s a business. I actually don’t mind spending for games I enjoy, but it should be reasonable...and enjoyable. It’s not. Peace out G5.
