Description

Drew and Jonathan Scott, famously known as the Property Brothers, need you to help clients achieve their home design dreams! With the brothers at your side, you’ll demo, renovate, customize, and design spaces with the same charm and style the twins have become known for. Play along to go behind the scenes with the brothers, learn their design tips and tricks, and discover fun facts about TV’s favorite design duo. Relaxing, fun puzzles let you earn coins to spend on décor. Exclusive voice-overs from the twins will give you all the encouragement you need to become a design star yourself!

Features

- Transform shabby spaces into beautiful places with bold accents, pops of color, and stylish fixtures and finishes.
- Discover all you’ve ever wanted to know about the twins with everything from tried-and-true design tips, funny childhood stories, and good, old-fashioned brother vs. brother antics.
- Renovate a variety of fixer uppers that need your design eye: kitchens, bathrooms, exteriors—and yes, you can add that open floor plan!
- Re-create your favorite Property Brothers moments!
- Relax with some of the best puzzle gameplay that features hundreds of levels and a variety of game modes.
- Design and play on the run with offline mode.

Couple suggestions

Great game, fun to play when I need something to pass the time, but there are a few things that could make it better. First, limited time challenges should be longer than three days. Once you get further in the game, it’s harder to win levels, which means harder to earn tickets, and less chance of completing the limited time challenges before they expire, especially when every change to the room costs around eighty tickets. When you only get around twenty tickets in one level (although in some you earn much less) it will take a while to actually make one change. At the very least, changes should be less expensive. You could also make it to where on hard levels, tickets are doubled just like the coins. Second, we should be able to choose when we get to use the prizes. If we earn a one hour life prize, we should be able to choose when to use it, not just when we earn it because sometimes I’m not able to use it right then and then it just goes to waste. Finally, it should be easier to earn diamonds. Except for the occasional time when you can watch a video to earn ten diamonds, the only time you can get diamonds is if you buy them. So once you use them, you’re out of luck unless you want to spend real money on them. All in all, this is a fun game that could be even better with just a few adjustments.

Graysmompcb , 04/16/2020

Too Much of the “Other” Game, Not enough decorating

So, here’s the deal. I love the part of the game when we get to make choices (although you only choose between 3 options that may or may not be your style and don’t get to choose the placement, as it appears in the Ad I saw) and decorate/revitalize spaces. However, the amount of time you have to spend on the “other” game you have to play to earn “money” or “tickets” far outweighs the time you get to spend playing the game you actually want to play. I’m talking at least 10:1. So, for example, spend 10 minutes playing a candy-crush offshoot type game to earn money in order to be able to decorate/paint/etc. 4-5 items/area within, say, 1 minute. Then you are out of “money” and have to play more random games for more. You don’t earn or accrue any of the game’s currency over time/daily, etc. You can play the games to get some, but diamonds can only be purchased with actual dollars from your bank account. I think I’ve spent like $20-30 this week and have decorated 2 rooms and have completed only part of a home exterior and garden. So, in a nutshell, lots of real money needed and time playing another random game to get very little time playing the decorating portion. I hope this gets fixed! Otherwise, I’m going back to playing “Design Home”. Sorry guys! I do think your placement and timing for pop up ads for purchasing things is genius. Tricked me several times! I give you a slow clap and head nod for that!
