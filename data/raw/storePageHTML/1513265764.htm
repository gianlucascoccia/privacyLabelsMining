Description

You've been looking forward to the summer break, but now you have to go visit your grandpa in Paradise Town. This mysterious place, your doddering grandpa, and your mother's old bedroom... You can't help but feel that there's a secret hidden here.

A dusty old lectern shall become the gateway between reality and the world of books, and open up the way to a beautiful, magical adventure.

Step into Versailles, and fight the chaos threatening the kingdom over an opulent necklace; obtain stunning palace attire, and immerse yourself in 18th century Rococo beauty. Of course, you will also meet a special person in your life, and face difficult decisions in critical situations...

-Unique and beautiful attire and accessories
Every story will have its own style that suits the world it's set in: ancient, modern, eastern, western, and more.

-Dramatic story-changing choices
The story's ending and the fate of its characters lie in your hands.

-Highly customizable clothing DIY
Use your imagination and apply special styles, patterns, and colors to customize everything to your liking.

-Relaxing and fun pet system
Collect cute kitty cats of various colors and markings, and send them out to help you collect materials. There's no need to replay stages over and over again. Get all you need in a fun and carefree manner.

-Make friends and share your wardrobe
Make friends with fellow players all over the world, and share your clothes and your creativity!

AmelieSweet , 01/18/2021

Agree w GeekyNixie

I uploaded the game to play while stuck in bed after surgery. I found that I enjoy it much more than I expected to. Adore the clothes and the story line is very well written. Definitely done by someone with a good knowledge of English, unlike some of the other games I’m playing. As I explore the game, which I don’t find super intuitive (but I’m an adult) it’s also nice to find lots of ways to earn more diamonds and coins. The cons- once you get stuck bc you need an outfit with certain attributes, you can’t play other than to wait and collect diamonds and coins. And even while you collect wealth, if you need a particular item to craft the clothes needed for a level that item may or may not be available in Parvens shop. So you can be rich and you still can’t move on in the game. The solution is to borrow clothes from other players, but I like the privacy of playing alone. You also need a crazy volume of items to craft clothes and that is where the temptation to make purchases w real money comes in. If the items are available you want to be able to get them. So, I love the game and will keep playing but I can tell it’s going to take a while unless I cave and borrow clothes. I also wish there was an option to spend money to buy pieces of clothing with diamonds or coins instead of crafting everything. That would help if you are rich but stuck waiting for an ‘ingredient’ to craft with.

Easily the most fun game I have ever played

There’s really no two ways about it, I simply love this game. It is so much fun, there is always something to do, there are different events, and new things to try all the time! I love the stories, the music, the characters, the clothes, and absolutely the cats! I’m sure I’ve missed something I like but that’s okay. I play this game every day. I love doing the daily missions and puppet games. I especially love the Fantasy Fair mini game. It’s so fun. It stresses me out just a little because getting the coins in time before the fair ends seems kind of impossible. I truly hope we can get several shots at each outfit (that is several sessions for the same outfit reasonably interspaced). If there are any older people reading and wondering if this is a good game, IT IS. I’m 22 and I enjoy it so much. There’s somethings I would change, but it’s never going to be perfect so that’s fine. Regardless, I loved this game when I got it, I have loved every update that has come my way, and I am excited to see what the producers have in store. These game designers are geniuses and I am so glad I took a chance on this game.

GeekyNixie , 09/08/2020

Terrible Crafting System, Fun Story

It’s a nice game for the most part, but the crafting system leaves a lot to be desired. Basically, it’s one of those games that makes you wait to get things done unless you buy and waste a lot of their special currency for real money. That alone is pretty normal, but the availability of materials is inconsistent too- you have to catch when and where the materials are temporarily for limited quantity sale and won’t be able to advance in the levels without amassing increasingly large stockpiles of them. So there’s very little to do once you hit a wall with the crafting. Yes subsystems/minigames exist, but they aren’t fun and don’t really do much.
But, a huge positive: the story is fun and the animation isn’t bad. The interactive element is not just a ploy used in advertising- the story can go multiple ways and you can go back later to explore the other paths that were available. You can even send gifts to the characters in whatever story you’re playing through. Except when they’re “busy,” which might be exactly when you need to give them gifts to unlock a level. The clothes are pretty, even if crafting them takes a lot of patience. If it weren’t for the irritating crafting system and ease with which all the fun grinds to a tedious halt, I think I would very much like it.
