Description

Weather forecasts and radar for weather nerds!

*****
Featured by Apple, MacRumors, CNet, App Advice and more! "Weather Nerd is a comprehensive package in a gorgeous wrapper."
*****

WHAT'S NEW?

- Optimized for All Sizes of Devices
- Siri Support
- Bolder, Larger Text
- Tons of New Measurements Like Humidity, Pressure, etc
- Forecasts for Your Calendar Events

TOP FEATURES

- Detailed daily reports (up to a year out)
- Minute-to-minute precipitation (see coverage below)
- Weather radar and satellite
- 3 widgets (next hour, day, week)

Purchase includes one year full access. After a year, you can renew via an in-app purchase.

########################################

COVERAGE

Weather reports are available for nearly anywhere in the world. If in doubt, go to http://darksky.net and try your location to see if you like the report.

Radar is currently North America only.

The hour forecast is currently limited to US, UK, Ireland.


DAILY COMPARISON

When you're getting dressed, what do you want to know?

"How does today compare with yesterday?"

Weather Nerd includes a nice daily comparison:

"Today is 9° cooler than yesterday. No rain."

This makes it easy to grab the right coat.


MINUTE-TO-MINUTE FORECAST

Weather Nerd also gives you minute-by-minute rain predictions for your exact GPS location. Powered by Dark Sky's cutting edge "hyperlocal precipitation forecasting system", these reports show you a nice graph of the rain intensity for the next hour based on your exact location. For example, you might see:

"Light rain starting in 10 minutes, stopping 15 minutes later."


DAILY FORECAST

The daily forecast screen shows you a graph of the temperature, rain, and wind for today. Touch anywhere on the graph to view the forecast for a specific time. See more details at the bottom of the screen.



WEEKLY FORECAST

The weekly forecast shows you a quick graph of the projected highs and lows for the next week, along with the chance of rain. Touch any day to view the full forecast for that day.


BEYOND 7 DAYS

Need to see the forecast for two weeks or two months out? No problem. Weather Nerd's calendar lets you view the forecast for any date within the next year.


NERD OUT

For the real weather nerds, the "Nerd Out" feature will list out ALL of the weather information.


NERD ALERTS

Weather Nerd lets you setup a daily weather forecast alert (in 15 minute intervals). You can turn the alert on/off for certain days of the week.

If you set your alert to 6pm or later, then the forecast will be for "tomorrow's" weather.


NERD WIDGETS

Some of you nerds like to use iPhone and iPad widgets. No problem. Weather Nerd comes packaged with 3 different widgets for your viewing pleasure including graphs for the "Next Hour", "Today", and "Week".


========================

Why a subscription? The average nerd checks weather 4-5 times a day. Over a year, that alone cost nearly 1 USD to our providers. That's not counting additional data used for radar tiles, alerts, widgets, multiple devices, servers, etc. It would be completely unsustainable to charge a one time fee for a lifetime of data.

Weather Nerd uses auto-renewing subscriptions. The Nerd Club subscription provides full access to weather reports, radar, widgets, and notifications. The subscription will be charged to your iTunes account at confirmation of purchase within the app. Subscriptions will automatically renew at the end of your subscription period and will be charged through your iTunes account. Subscriptions renew automatically unless canceled at least 24 hours prior to the end of the current period. You can turn off auto-renewal at any time from your iTunes account settings. Cancellation of the current active subscription period is not allowed.

Good weather app, but...

I’ve been using WN on my iPhone for years, and like it a lot. Well worth the modest annual subscription fee. However, I have a few peeves about it:
1. Although a number of reviewers mention WN as a top weather app for Apple Watch, I can’t seem to find a way to load it on my watch (Series 4, with latest watch OS). Which brings me to my second gripe:
2. I can’t seem to find any user guide available, and the FAQs are very limited. Nor is there a way to contact the developer for help, as far as I can see.
3. This is one I’ve been wondering about for as long as I’ve been using this app: the probability of rain listed at the top of a daily forecast doesn’t generally match the graph of rain probabilities throughout the day. Does the overall number mean probability that it will rain at SOME point during the day, and the graph indicate the probability at a given time? Or am I misinterpreting this?
Anyway, even with these shortcomings, I still give it a solid 4 stars as an iPhone app.
