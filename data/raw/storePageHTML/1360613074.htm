Description

Quick Peek is the easy organizing, storage, and inventory app that helps you find what you want, when you want it. A home organizing tool for the whole family. Perfect for spring-cleaning, allowing you to easily organize everything.

Now featuring Dropbox Sync to make this app the ideal visual inventory tool for teams as well.

This smart storage and iOS organizing app is paired with our smart labels to help you create an organized visual inventory of all your stuff, so you can find the items you need without tearing your house apart.

Simply snap a photo of the contents going in the box, pack them up, apply the smart label, and scan. You’ve just made a virtual box in the app that matches your real life box.

The Quick Peek app lets you sort by location, a complete list of boxes, or browse a Pinterest-style gallery of all your cataloged items. Lastly, Quick Peek has a scanner to scan the labels in real time to see exactly what is in each box. These smart labels are similar to scanning QR codes, but show results even faster!

An easy-to-use app to get organized and eliminate clutter!

Let’s say you’ve recently moved to your new place and need to find your birth certificate so you can go get your new drivers license, only you have no idea which box you placed it in. With Quick Peek, you can easily find the box where you packed all your important documents within seconds with a simple scan, a quick search, or by browsing.

You will never have to stand in front of a bunch of boxes again and say, “Oh no, I wonder where that is”. Just start scanning to take a peek inside.

Quick Peek uses iCloud backup by default but Dropbox can also be selected so you can use it on multiple devices and never have to worry about losing all your data. The ultimate iOS smart storage system.

Highly recommended for moving, spring-cleaning, home organizing, inventory for small businesses, storage, collections, tax documents, and more!


Features:

- Buy and print your own labels or buy pre-printed self-adhesive labels

Requests a creepy level of access to dropbox

You have to share your entire dropbox contents to sync. I recommend setting up a separate dropbox account for this app or sticking to the iCloud sync if you don’t need to share with multiple people (which anyone actually using this app probably needs to do).

They could also support other services for multi-user sync.

Developer Response ,

Thank you for your review! As you mentioned, Dropbox is not the only option for syncing your data - you can also sync to iCloud with the app. Using the iCloud method will allow devices linked to the same Apple ID to access the same Quick Peek data. We take the privacy of our users seriously and can assure you that the app doesn't access or write anything in your Dropbox aside from the Quick Peek folder that is used to store the data files associated with the app. We appreciate your feedback about multi-user sync options and will consider it for future development.

kosimov1 , 07/09/2019

Does what it needs to do.

QuickPeek stores images of what is in a container and gives a list of the images for later retrieval. A label is attached to the container, and a picture is taken of the label. Later, when you need to know what is in the container, you simply scan the label. A list of pictures of what is in the container (for most of us, a box!), is then available for you to decide whether to get the boX down from its place on the highest shelf, or try another box.

This is a vary basic program. It does not have options for setting up a data base system or anything to sort or search the data you have created with your phone and it’s built-in camera. That said, it follows that it may be used as a very low level “inventory” program for a small company, although I don’t think it would have been adequate for any of the small companies I started. However, it works very well for the boxes in my basement or garage!

This program is a good answer to the question “where is that old antique watermelon peeler we bought a few years back when we were on Vacation?”, or “what in the world is in all those boxes in the garage”. It may even be used to catalog a lot of photographs, though I don’t know if it would be practical for that application. But for smaller, more personal applications, it would be good.

Developer Response ,

Thank you so much for your review. In addition to scanning a box's label to see what's in the box, there is also an "all items" feature in the app which allows you to search for items by the names you originally assigned them and also view all items visually across all of your boxes. Quick Peek was designed to be simple and easy-to-use - for moving, and also organizing items in your attic, basement, garage, etc., as you've mentioned. We hope you enjoying using the app!
