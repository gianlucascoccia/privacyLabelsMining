Spencer Comer , 03/29/2020

Has lots of potential

I have to say I really like the concept of the game. However it does need some modifications to be able to compete with its competitors. I wish there were more cars in the game. This is okay because the game was released only a few months ago. I do think that more cars can and will be added overtime. I recently received the Pontiac trans am from a free reward. I think you could add more Pontiacs in the game. For example I think the G8 gxp would be a great car for the game. I also think the G5 GT or the Saturn ion redline would be great and enjoyable cars for the game. I also want to see more Paganis
like a Zonda Cinque or a Zonda C12. I think a Mitsubishi 3000GT VR4 or more lancer evos would be nice. I also think more variations of the Mercedes Benz SLS would be nice. I think Suzuki could make an appearance in the game with the Swift and the cappuccino. My next point is that there shouldn’t pp requirements for races. EA does this in their racing games and it’s really irritating and is time consuming. Another suggestion I have is that there shouldn’t be a limit in your garage for cars. Although this is similar to real life it just becomes annoying overtime. I also think you should not have to pay to change the color of your car. My final suggestion is that you should be able to race in online multiplayer groups. These are my thoughts on the game I hope you can consider them. Overall this game is much better than csr2 in my opinion.

Developer Response ,

Hi there Spencer, thank you for your review. As the game is fairly new there is still a lot of content awaiting to be released per update. We recently released Offroad in the game which includes new tracks and cars. Developing cars takes time and also depends on licensing so it isn't a quick fix.
I will add your recommended cars to our list :) we have a Discord group where other players also recommend their desired cars to be seen in game!
I will pass on your feedback regarding the PP and colour changes to cars as well as online multiplayer. Thank you for your time.
We hope you continue to play and enjoy the upcoming updates! :) - LA

filter27e , 08/23/2020

I gave it the most time possible

I gave an initial 3 star review, but kept playing to give the game a chance to impress me. It never did, instead the nasty reality came about full force.

I have played a number of games that has the model of paying for unique items and upgrades. I’ve never been a big game spender, but i realize that there was a time where I’d spend $50 on a new release that kept my attention for less time than some of these games, and I’ll invest a little here and there. This game did not call for me to do that. It ends at pretty graphics.

Frustrating physics, annoying AI, short races, no real competitions. That sums this game up a bit better. I would give props to the devs for making a game with decent controls and graphics, but the whole game reeks of a big company who bought the game, got rid of the original devs, and hired some yes-men programmers to add random content every once in a while and ways to charge for everything. Haven’t you guys considered the idea that the free players bring money in by stimulating competition? Instead I feel like if I paid the $5 or whatever to enter one of the competitions, I’d be the only sap in there.
