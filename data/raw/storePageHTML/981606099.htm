Too much tracking

I dislike how this app appears to track my every move. It appears that to remain eligible after entering a raffle you have to keep the app open and let it collect your data. This seems invasive for a person just trying to buy something limited.

A second area that needs improvement is the handing out of Ls. The app seems very slow to confirm a person did not win. I derived this from following the app during a release, and also staying in contact with the store.

This app could be also awesome, but needs more work. I would also like to better understand where my location data is going, and if my information is being sold.

Kingsalvi24 , 07/24/2020

Avoid at all cost

This app is by far the biggest scam, and money grab I have ever experienced. Yes, it allows for you to enter raffles that utilize it’s platform. You then get offered different perks like “Front of the line” which allows you to be pre-registered for drops. That’s cool. But here‘s where there is a lack of transparency and in all honesty how this developer likely makes the most money out of its users. They offer you to “Boost” your entries ranging from $3 to $5, most I’ve spent was $10 on boosting. But it’s not promising what so ever, and no guarantee that what you “Boost” for is even allowing for you to actually receive those “Perks”. It’s a “take our word for it” scam. To date, I’ve entered raffles since February 2020, and have spent roughly around $135-$150 to not have won a single pair of shoes. I try for the love of sneakers, and collecting but this is a big part of the reason why I quit and left the scene for several years before attempting to re-enter the scene.
