Description

Manage your money on the move and around the clock with this secure Personal Banking app from HSBC for iPhone, iPad and iPod touch. With more updates and features on the way you can:

• Log on around the world
• View your account balances and transaction history
• Move money

Exact features depend on the HSBC services available in your country.

To log on to this app you must be an HSBC Personal Internet Banking customer. If you are not yet registered, please visit your local HSBC website.

• HSBC helps protect your information by using 128-bit SSL encryption technology when you are logged on.
• Data charges from your mobile service provider may apply.

To find out more about the app including details of permissions requested please see https://m.mobile15.hsbc.com/pages/mobile-banking.html

Hong Kong account functions have moved into the new HSBC HK Mobile Banking app.

If you are a customer of HSBC Mexico, please search for and download HSBC Móvil to access your accounts.

brendenbeu , 07/09/2018

Unbelievably Bad

HSBC advertises their Global Transfer feature as the thing that separates them from the competition. It took me three frustrating months to get my US and French HSBC accounts set up. This Friday I initiated a Global Transfer via this app to get money into my French account to pay my rent. It appeared to work and I got a receipt in the app. On Saturday I contacted HSBC because there was no evidence of the pending transfer. They told me that the feature is in the App but it doesn’t actually work. You can only make Global Transfers via the web site. So.... why do you have features in your app that don’t work? That’s worse than not having the feature at all. It’s misleading. Reading the reviews here I just noticed a review in February that says the same thing. HSBC has known about this problem for at least six months and the feature is still there not working and misleading people?!?! I’m closing my US account. I’ll just use TransferWise to move money from my US Chase account to my French HSBC account. HSBC seems to mark up the exchange rate anyway, so using TransferWise costs about the same.
