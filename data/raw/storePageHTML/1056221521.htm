Description

••• The 'unofficial official' app for netatmo weather stations •••

myatmo is an alternative app for your netatmo weather station, with detailed charts and a user-friendly design, including Apple Watch App, Today and Home-Screen Widgets. No other app for the netatmo weather station offers as much as myatmo, the 'Swiss army knife' for your netatmo weather station.

••• Now supporting all new Apple Silicon Macs such as the new MacBook Air, the new MacBook Pro and the new Mac mini •••

Upgrade your netatmo weather station with myatmo and discover the variety and informational content of a plethora of metrics in beautiful, feature-rich and interactive charts. Not without reason many users call myatmo the 'unofficial official' app for netatmo weather stations!

myatmo includes:

• Detailed charts for all data types
• Thermal Imaging diagrams
• Comparison charts for months and whole years
• Min / Max diagrams
• Wind direction diagrams
• Wind frequency diagrams
• Cumulative precipitation diagram
• Grassland temperature sum diagram*
• Cold sum diagram*
• Overview diagram of the most important data types together
• Charts for the last 1-48 hours
• Diagrams for any period of time
• Compare different data types in a chart
• Weather forecast bar and 'Global Weather Maps'
• CSV data export via iCloud or email
• Configurable and customizable in almost all aspects
• Free color choice for better recognition of various data types
• Share chart screenshots via iMessage, Whatsapp and more
• Apple Watch App, Apple Watch Complications
• 'Home Screen' Widgets
• Today Widget

Please note: some features require a myatmo 'Premium' purchase. To use myatmo you need a netatmo weather station, for some features of myatmo you also need a netatmo wind gauge and a netatmo rain gauge. *Some diagrams are only available for weather stations in Europe!

Follow us on Twitter: @AppMyatmo

What’s New

2021年3月5日

Version 4.3.0

And once again we have a completely new function for you: with this update, all myatmo 'Premium' users receive '3-year statistics' with information on how often certain temperature ranges and other meteorologically or climatologically significant daily values ​​in the last three years were achieved.

• New: 3-year statistics

Can be called up via the main menu on the side of the overview screen.

Here you can compare how often the following daily values ​​have been achieved over the last three years: summer days, hot days, desert days, tropical nights, heating days, vegetation days, frost days, ice days, rainy days and storm days. How these different days are defined is explained in the new '3-year statistics'.

Mentioned values ​​can be displayed and compared in a special diagram for the last 3 years. myatmo offers both a bar chart and a radial chart for this purpose. As always, individual values ​​can be deactivated by tapping the diagram legend, for example to compare only the number of summer days within a three-year period.

myatmo users without 'premium status' can only use the new function for the last three years, all other users can scroll back through the years and thus view the last three years of any year.

In addition, this update also contains the usual minor bug fixes, updated components and a few performance improvements

The App Netatmo should have made

I’ve been using Netatmo products for about 5 years now and love them very much. I’ve two indoor temp/humidity sensors, one outside, and both a wind and rain sensors. The amount of data the gather is great and fun to watch. But, in all of Netatmo’s wisdom they forgot to write equally useful App for iPads and iPhones. Their App is clunky to get around in and impossible to view all of their products sensor information at once.
This is where Myatmo comes into play. I his is the App Netatmo should have built. As soon as you open up the App and login to your Netatmo account you’re shown all of your sensor data on one very thought out screen. The screen is very colorful and easy to look at with current sensor data n the left and graphs on the right. You can simply scroll down the list of your devices to view their data, not pull each own down from a pop up menu like Netatmo makes you do. The weather forecast for your area is always displayed at the bottom of the screen making it easy to view at a glance.
Very nice App indeed and a big thank you to the makers of!
