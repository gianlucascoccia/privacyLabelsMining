Description

For our 2020 Advent Calendar, we're transporting you to the wintry world of the Nordic countries! A beautiful main scene featuring a fictional Nordic village conceals a new animated story or other activity for every day of December up to Christmas Day, along with dozens of games, puzzles and hidden surprises which gradually unfold over the festive period. Enjoy a Christmas Tree decorator, Klondike Solitaire, Tri-peaks Solitaire, Match-3, 10x10, Puzzles and more! And for those who simply can’t wait for the action to unfold from December 1st, lots of games and activities are available right now!

Not Quite the Same

I agree with many of the comments here. It is a beautiful calendar - the artwork is so lovely and it definitely made me long to be somewhere cold and snowy but it was very “one note.” I really love doing the activities - and while there are the standard games and information like puzzles, card games, and a book about Nordic holiday lore - it isn’t nearly as interactive as it has been with past calendars and it was a bit of a let down. I know it takes a lot of work to do the calendar every year and with all that is going on, it may not have been possible to do more. As with so many other things, let us hope that next year is better.

CRapple52 , 12/22/2020

Missing the former versions

If you are completely new to the JL Advent Calendar, this is a smooth, interactive calendar, and you may be quite pleased. Having been a long-standing user of the one from the website, I am disappointed in several ways. First, I can no longer give these as gifts to my distant family & friends with any assurance that they will be able to actually use them on whatever kind of system they have. Second there is no “bulk” discount unless you buy from the site. Problem there is that it only works on iMacs & PCs. No phones or tablets. And if you have an old operating system on your computer, it will not download & be operable.
The setting is as beautiful as in past incarnations, but far less interactive. No fun little surprises, like squirrels popping out of chimneys. And few options for fun make-it-yourself activities as the days go by. Mostly just what’s available before the days actually begin. The smashing balls game is more regimented, and I’ve yet to earn a really decent score in the building-with-blocks game. (Of course, I just may be getting too old. 🙄😆)
So while this has been a happy staple on my gift-giving list in the past, I am very sorry that it isn’t this year. I do hope that improvements are planned for next year, or I may not even purchase it for the few on my Family Share. I have been priced and technologied out of the market.

Jake Iowa , 12/06/2020

Bad Tidings

I have loved these advent calendars for years, but this year’s version gets only two stars because of disappointing content and changes in marketing.
I long made it a practice to purchase at least 10 advent calendars from the JL website to send to friends and family. But I did not do that this year because it was clear that the version from the website would run only on computers and not on mobile devices. The iPad app required a separate $4.99 purchase from the App Store. Well, o.k., I figured, even though $4.99 is expensive for an app with a known life expectancy of 25 days. I bit the bullet and bought one for my iPad. I purchased only a couple on the JL site for friends because I had no idea what devices most of them might want to use, and I didn’t want to send a gift that then might require an out-of-pocket additional purchase.
Nevertheless, I eagerly awaited Dec. 1 to begin opening the calendar, anticipating its usual trove of gorgeous art, interactive games and activities, and interesting explanations of Nordic Christmas traditions. And then the Grinch really rode in. As of Dec. 6, I had found one out of three: Plenty of gorgeous art, but a dearth of interactive activities and no explanations, for the most part, of Nordic holiday traditions. This year’s calendar is a shadow of its former self. Score another one for 2020, I suppose.
