Description

Now with 122 knots! Pro-Knot is the most trusted printed knot reference in the world. These are the same rope knot illustrations we supply to the US Department of Defense for training, US Border Patrol, Fire Departments, Utilities, Arborists and countless Scout troops in our best selling printed format! These are the same fishing knot illustrations from the best selling printed fishing knot reference in the world!

- 4 weeks as a "WHATS HOT" iTunes App!
- Previous #1 Sports App in USA!
- #1 Sports App in 41 Countries!

Free updates with more knots are provided regularly (currently on our 11th update). We are committed to keeping this the best knot app available in the iTunes store.

- 64 essential Rope Knots
- 58 best Fishing Knots (most fishing knots of any App in the store!)

All explained in clear, easy to follow swipe through step-by-step instructions and animations. The knots, their uses and background information are described and the tying of each knot is broken down into easily understood steps, with easy to follow graphics for each step. Optional animations of all of the knots are available as a single in-app purchase.

abeness , 10/15/2019

Awesome offline reference for knots of all kinds

I’ve been a happy user of ProKnot for many years, ever since I bought a new tent and needed to learn how to tie my own adjustable guy lines without needing extra doo-dads. Enter the bowline and the tautline hitch. When the app stopped working with iOS 13, I reached out and got a quick response from John that he’d look into it, and lo and behold, the latest update released today resolves the issue. My guy lines have held up to strong winds, thanks to ProKnot’s clear transmission of knot wisdom. Fishing knots have proven useful, as have a number of others. I find myself swiping through new knots, thinking up ways to apply them in my daily life, or imagining new activities in which I might be able to use them. And nothing beats having an offline reference when cell service is spotty and you find that you need a knot you didn’t know you needed. Thanks, John.
