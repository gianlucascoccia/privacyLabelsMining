Description

Tear up the Asphalt and take on the world’s most fearless racers to become the next racing legend in the most thrilling arcade racing game available on iPhone and iPad.

Take on the world’s most fearless drivers and become the next Asphalt Legend – from the creators of Asphalt 8: Airborne.

Asphalt 9: Legends features an extensive roster of real hypercars from renowned manufacturers such as Ferrari, Porsche, Lamborghini and W Motors. Pick your dream ride and race across spectacular locations around the world. Leave your limits in the dust and become a “Legend of the Track!”

-- GAME FEATURES --

CUSTOMIZE YOUR RIDE
Collect over 60 of the world’s most amazing speed machines. Each vehicle is meticulously modelled to recreate the real thing from the inside out. Then, use the new car editor to define the exact color and materials for your car. You can also pick the color of the rims and brake calipers, and even add carbon parts to fully customize your rides.

BREATHTAKING LOCATIONS
Feel the thrill of gravity-defying races across 80 tracks in the most amazing real-world locations. Race around giant tornados in the American wilderness, avoid landslides in the Himalayas or ride across the sandy beaches of the Caribbean. Each track offers endless gameplay opportunities to turn every race into a real blockbuster movie.

SINGLE / MULTIPLAYER
Start your journey in the single-player Career mode and complete over 900 events to grow your collection of supercars. Become a real Asphalt legend by racing against up to 7 rival players from all over the world in the online multiplayer mode's different series.

SMART / MANUAL CONTROLS
Master the innovative TouchDrive™, a new control scheme that streamlines steering to let players focus on the fun of racing. You can also switch to Tap-to-Steer controls and customize the position of the buttons on-screen, or use Tilt-to-Steer to learn how to drive like a pro Asphalt racer.

CLUB RACE
For the first time in the Asphalt series, you can create your own community of like-minded racers with the Club feature. Collaborate with your Club members to unlock Milestone rewards and rise up the ranks of the Club leaderboard. You can also create your own private races to challenge your Club friends on any of the racetracks.

REGULAR EVENTS
Join in the fun with the Events hub. Compete in solo events daily to rack up extra resources and blueprints for your rides. Play ranked events and compete asynchronously against the world to rock the leaderboard and earn rare rewards. Keep an eye out for special events to unlock the rarest and most desirable cars.

IMPORTANT NOTES:
This app requires an iPad mini 2 (or newer) or an iPhone 5s (or newer). A network connection is required to play.

This app no longer supports devices running on iOS 9.0. Please make sure to update your OS to the next version, if available.

Pay to win game

I have been play Asphalt games since the year Asphalt 8 dropped and have enjoyed playing GameLoft games for a while now. But in Asphalt 9, everywhere you go the game advertising deals or packs to get your money for a decent amount of credits in game. When you start off in the game and get used to racing, you unlock a fair amount of cars, sure. Are they any good? Can’t say so myself. For every class of cars, there are about five of them, there is maybe 15 cars in each and only 2 or 3 of them will do you any good. I have maxed out multiple cards on my account and have compared cars side by side. Most players defiantly favor cars and will not play with half of the cars that there is to choose from. You can also purchase car packs in the shop that DO NOT guarantee the car blueprints that is actually on the packet, but rather car blueprints which are included in the same class as the car blueprints you are looking for.( When you collect 30-60 blueprint for a car, you unlock and can use it in races). They like to scramble it up in the packs and base what you get in the car packs off of “chance”. Typically leaving without the car blueprints you were hoping for, trying to get you to buy more packs for a better chance of unlocking the cars you want. There are also MANY bugs and glitches in the game that time and time again they will not fix after updating the game. The game is all around great and fun but unfair and annoying.

I love it but needs balance

Legendary game no arguments. Amazing graphics. The driving itself is over the top but that’s the asphalt experience and it’s really fun. And if I still want to progress but play casually I can with the touch drive feature that was totally genius. I hesitated to play asphalt 8 sometimes because it really required you to be focused on the game. But now I can still progress if don’t have both hands free. But I must say I would never take away the traditional driving controls completely because I feel like that’s the only way to enjoy a true asphalt experience. Anyways, I miss the ability to just practice on tracks without it taking away from the cars gas tanks because I want to master the tracks or play an unlimited amount even if it means not getting rewarded for it. Another thing the game needs some balance in single player. My cars rank could be 300 above the recommended rank and I lose with or without touchdrive on. One thing that could help this I noticed is when I get a knockdown the npc will spawn in front of me yet when I crash I automatically am moved to last place. I also wish you could just restart a race when something like that happens rather then finish or quit just to wait all that time to boot up the same race again. Well I hope you guys at game loft read this because I saw there were 600,000 reviews and this will probably be buried anyway haha.

Eragon3650 , 03/08/2020

Needs work

I personally love Asphalt 9. I find that the game has great potential, and with its stunning graphics, exciting gameplay, and tolerance of different styles of playing, what’s not to love? The problem is though, this game is designed to try to get people to pay money. I personally have not payed a single cent on this game. Instead, I’ve dealt with the add’s, knowing that they were helping support the game. That changed though. The game went from having casual players come across adds occasionally to having to pay $20-$100 to complete special events. On the topic of special events, why is it that you have requirements for people to use specific cars that most players have worked hard to get, then just put a price tag of $20 on the hours that the player has spent to reach this point? It’s not right!!! One more problem is on the topic of ads. I use ads whenever I can. I’m all for the free stuff. My problem is that the game will go and claim that there is a poor internet connection and it takes me another 10-15 seconds to get the ad to run, even though I have a great internet connection. And while 10-15 seconds doesn’t seem like a lot, after 10-20 ads, the time compounds on itself. Another problem is that it will only allow you to watch so many ads before claiming “no ads are available”. There definitely aren’t any ads available, one thinks to themselves while sitting through a random ad that popped up after they went to the home tab.
