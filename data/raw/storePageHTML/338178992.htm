Description

Download CFR, USC, rules, and state law to your mobile device. Join thousands and try LawStack for FREE today.

- US Constitution
- Federal Rules of Civil Procedure (FRCP)
- Federal Rules of Criminal Procedure (FRCrP)
- Federal Rules of Appellate Procedure (FRAP)
- Federal Rules of Evidence (FRE)
- Federal Rules of Bankruptcy Procedure (FRBP)
- Code of Federal Regulations (CFR)
- United States Code (USC)

Looking for more? Go pro and subscribe to a pro in-app module. Examples:

- Code of Federal Regulations (latest versions)
- United States Code (latest versions)
- California Code (CA)
- Florida Code (FL)
- Texas Code (TX)
- Delaware Code (DE)
- New York Code (NY)
- Washington Code (WA)
- Many more...

Can't find what you're looking for? Make a request. Then download more legal titles in the future versions of LawStack. We'll try to add the most requested titles first.

Features:

- Complete offline access for downloaded titles.
- Killer full-text search.
- Bookmarks.
- Header only search option.
- Search highlighting.
- Saved search term history.
- Email sections.
- Context-sensitive search.

Updated: App subscription is misleading

Update: I have been contacted by the developers. What I thought was misleading turns out to have been a bug. They were very quick to contact me and remedy the situation. I appreciate their assistance and courtesy!

Original post: The app works well but only if you maintain your subscription. Unlike buying a physical copy of your state’s laws, which contain exactly the same material, once your subscription expires you can’t view anything; including old versions. Even though the expiration notice that pops up gives you a link to view old versions, if you click on the old versions that were included in your paid subscription, it simply pops up the same notice saying your subscription is expired.

Hi Jacob, I'm so sorry that you are having this issue. You should be able to access the free version whether you have a subscription or not.
