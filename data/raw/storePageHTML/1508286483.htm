Tinylilalli15 , 06/03/2020

Good writing, choices matter, beautiful art!

I always download choose-your-story apps when they come out, since they’re my favorite kinds of games. But none of them have really caught my attention, and I’ve deleted all of them (apps like Chapters, Tabou - they’re okay, but not that great to me.) I downloaded this app because of the positive reviews, and I wasn’t disappointed! The writing is really well done, and the whole layout of the app is smooth and polished. Plus, the animations on the characters aren’t funny-looking, they’re so well-done! I wish you could pick a face for the main character, but I do understand that there’s illustrations so I get it. My only compliant would be diamonds - you only get one per finished chapter, and the choices seem very expensive, and you only start with 65 or something. Maybe up the amount the player receives per chapter to 5, perhaps, or reduce diamond choice prices? If that happened, this would be a perfect app for me. Sorry for the extensive review - to sum it up, I love it! I’ll be keeping this one for sure.

daannnii , 07/25/2020

Overall, an okay game

First, the layout of the game and the stories is amazing. Easy to use and attractive to the eye. The characters, their animation and drawing, are amazing as well- how they stand out not so much. The first two stories I tried, had to stop reading them after three chapters because the characters were just so plain. I didn’t find any of the protagonists (love interests) interesting at all and decided not to waste my time to “fight” for them. But soon, I found a story I was interested in! Really good story, writing is good (some spelling errors, rarely) and it’s cohesive what most stories don’t have. I finished this story quite quickly, got the most hearts posible without spending any diamonds to find out that you can’t get a decent (and I mean decent, doesn’t have to be happy) without spending diamonds. Extremely disappointed not going to lie, I wouldn’t have wasted my time reading the story if I knew what terrible ending I was going to get. The ending, without spending diamonds, are rushed and it just makes the protagonist go oh okay I just went through all of this for nothing and I’m perfectly fine with that. I should have noticed with how many times the app asks you to spend diamonds for situations that really shouldn’t have to. I get it, you what to make profit but the story isn’t even enjoyable if the protagonist has to reject every invitation she receives from the guy she’s trying to court. You see what’s wrong with that?

Pukyuuuu , 06/16/2020

Needs a lot of work to sustain

The stories, graphics, and flow of the chapters are great, but the need for diamonds and tickets really cuts down on the initial excitement of playing the game. At first, everything was cool because there were three tickets and there was about 150 diamonds. However, every good choice pushes for diamonds, ranging in price from 13 to 68 diamonds, including clothes and choices that will drive the story. The only way to get free diamonds is 2 at the end of every chapter, if an ad is watched as well, and watching ads, which gives a diamond per ad. But there’s only 5 ads to watch every 20 hours. It’s either pay up or get a mediocre ending. And the tickets could have a shorter waiting time; 4 hours for a ticket refill??? Just one ticket might I add, and the only way to get more tickets is through paying up, again. The concept is great, but having to deal with a game system that only seems to want your money is very off putting and takes away the experience and magic of these stories. Leaving the game like this will most likely cause customers to try it out and delete it if they get tired of having to constantly buy in order to succeed in the game. As some have suggested, allowing the player to buy a whole route would be nice as they wouldn’t have to worry about buying more and just enjoy the story.
