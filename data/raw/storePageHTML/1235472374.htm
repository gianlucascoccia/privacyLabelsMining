Description

Got a singing mood? Don’t wait a single moment! Karaoke is right here to help you sing your heart out!

Karaoke by Gismart is a handy tool that will let you express yourself singing. You hold a recording studio in your hands where you can create individual versions of international chart-topping hits!

It’s so easy and fun! All you need is your device and some passion for singing. Try it out now! Sing today!

KEY FEATURES

ENJOY TOP HITS: choose from a massive catalog of 1000+ songs and discover the greatest hits in your songbook regularly.

USERS’ FAVORITES:
** Sia - Chandelier **
** One Republic - Counting Stars **
** Capital Cities - Safe and Sound **
** Nelly Furtado - Turn Off The Light **
..and many more!

CHOOSE FROM DOZENS OF GENRES: sing songs from popular genres - Pop, Rock, Hip-hop, Rap, RnB, Country - we’ve got it all! The songbook is regularly updated with new tracks!

SOUND LIKE A PRO: use pitch correction and auto voice tuning to polish your vocal performance with great audio effects.

RECORD AND SAVE YOUR PERFORMANCE: get your own video clip in a tap!

Sing your heart out and rock like a real artist! Karaoke app is suitable for everyone who has a flair for music and wants to experience being on stage!

Express yourself singing!

Bri🏀 , 11/06/2020

Not POC-friendly

I am a person of color who wanted to try out the app after seeing an ad for it on instagram. Luckily, there was a 3-day free trial or else I would have instantly undownloaded the app because $9.99/week is a rip off for almost any “entertaining” app in my opinion LOL. So, I signed up for the 3-day trial and instantly was upset. Literally, they had barely any artists that I listen to. The only black artists that I found on this app with songs my peers and I would listen to are Beyonce, Rihanna, Alicia Keys, Khalid, and Lil Wayne. Even those artists have like 1-5 seconds apiece that you can sing, and it’s not even their best ones... just their most “white-liked” songs if I’m being honest. Aside from the artists I listen to, there were easily 100+ country, rock and roll, pop, alternative, etc. artists you could choose from. Lastly, of the three songs I tried, their beats were not that close to the original songs which threw off the karaoke experience. Great idea and concept, poor execution. If anything, this is just not a POC-friendly app.
