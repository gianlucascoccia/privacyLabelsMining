Think Snapchat, but for screen recording.

Low-key offers a new way to express yourself. Share your life with friends through your iPhone screen.

better than yo

crazy how fast this app has grown. i first heard about it from my friends at UCLA and then came home and people in San Francisco are using it too. i know there used to be an app called Yo that was popular years ago, but lowkey has a list of over 200 words and the Yo app only had one word. Low-key also allows you to easily turn words into questions by clicking the "?" button before selecting a word. the app is similiar to snapchat in the sense that nothing is stored inside the app, but low-key doesnt show send receipts and friends cant see if you opened the words. i think most kids prefer their content to disappear these days instead of having friends keep them forever, so low-key basically built on snapchats ephemeral idea, but took it to a whole other level by not showing any activity history.

all of the kids at my college have a love affair with lowkey. i can see why now. telling friends what you're doing/how you're feeling with a single click is awesome and its kinda a new way to have conversations with friends. It's a little hard to explain, but you'll understand when you download and start using it. its not the same as messaging on snapchat, because snapchat shows which friends you've recently messaged/if friends have opened your message. lowkey doesnt save anything or let friends know if you've seen their message. pretty cool new concept of messaging. it takes the pressure off in terms of feeling like you have to continue the conversation. if you dont want to respond to a word, you simply do nothing lol. its a stress free and fun way to talk to friends.
