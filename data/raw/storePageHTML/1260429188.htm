Description

Revere is a people-focused notes, todo, and reminders app that helps you build better relationships.

**The free version of Revere is free forever. The paid version supercharges it with features like Groups, Birthday Pre-reminders, and Touch ID and Face ID protection**

If you have trouble remembering names, conversations, or forget to message people back, Revere can help.

It's your single, trusted place for the important details about people in your life. Store things like people's family members’ names, how you met them, your last conversation, their work details, and more. Also add freeform notes about anything else – from gift ideas, to someone's favorite wine, to an important project or goal they’re working on.

Revere also includes reminders (single and recurring) so you never forget to call back or stay in touch. Set them to call someone back, check in on a special day, or just to say hello. With Revere you'll have the peace of mind that a reminder will arrive when you need it. Never forget to reach out at an important moment again.

Revere is great for birthdays and holidays too. Make notes with gift ideas so when the birthday arrives, you’ll have the perfect one. And you can set *Birthday Pre-Reminders* for 1 day, 1 week, or 1 month early, so you don’t have to scramble or pay express shipping when buying a gift.

Some other benefits of Revere.

SUBSCRIPTION DETAILS

There is only one paid version of Revere called Revere Premium. It's available on a monthly or yearly subscription. When you subscribe, the payment goes through the credit card attached to your Apple account.

When the subscription automatically renews, your account is charged in the 24 hours prior to the end of the current period (month or year). To cancel your subscription, do it at least 24 hours before the end of the current period.

You can manage or cancel subscriptions right from the settings page in Revere.

Terms of use – https://www.revereapp.com/terms-of-use

Perfect in-between a CRM & Contacts app

I’ve been struggling for years with needing something between a full CRM (or even a light weight CRM), LinkedIn, and the Contacts app on my phone. I meet so many people and just needed a place to jot down where/how/when I met them and notes about our conversations so I could keep track of some details of the relationship. I’m not trying to sell them anything so I don’t need all the CRM features which made using a lightweight CRM for personal/professional reasons a really weird and gross experience. I also can’t remember all the details of who I connect with on LinkedIn and why, especially in short term networking situations. Revere hits the mark of what I need beautifully. Can’t wait for more Premium features, please don’t go in the CRM direction! Keep it fun and clean to use so it’s more B2C than B2B. Thank you!

Thank you! It’s great to hear revere it’s that sweet spot for you. And yes, the plan is to keep it B2C and keep building both Premium and Free features.

Micah4Arsenal , 09/10/2019

Great App... Not great price

This app deserves a lot more recognition than it is getting. It is definitely a diamond in the rough for the App Store. I am surprised more apps haven’t come out using a similar idea, especially with the amount of people we meet on a daily basis, and may have problems remembering stuff about, etc. The interface is clean, and it is easy to use.

However, when I rate it 5-stars, it is for the free version. When you pay the subscription, it is not worth it. If the price was cut in half it may seem a bit more reasonable. You are essentially paying $6 to make folders, and get birthday updates (which most people get from Facebook anyways). Especially in light of all the other subscriptions you can get out there, the price is to high for the actual value and features added.

Hi Micah, thanks for those compliments – it's really rewarding to hear. I agree that the current Premium subscription is light on features, with Groups being the primary one. The plan is to continue a steady drum beat of new features that make Premium more and more valuable, until it's so useful that subscribing is a no-brainer. Watch for updates and more new features coming soon.

All the best,
Mark
