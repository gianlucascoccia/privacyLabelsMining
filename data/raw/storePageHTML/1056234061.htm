Description

Get rewarded using Freebird with cash-back and points for all the rides that you already are taking with Uber and Lyft.

Whether you’re on your way to a friend's house, the office, or even the airport, with Freebird you can put cashback in your pocket or collect points whenever you book a ride. To get started with Freebird, connect your Uber or Lyft account, enter your destination, and simply take a ride.

3 simple steps that let you earn cashback with Freebird

1. Choose your destination - Visit a participating location when you request your ride through Freebird.
2. Link your card - When you make a purchase (no minimum) with a credit or debit card that’s linked to your Freebird account, you can start getting cashback.
3. Pocket points or real cashback - Check your account balance and watch your savings or reward points build-up.

If you know where you want to go, simply search location or cuisine on Freebird and we’ll provide you with some fun options to choose from. Or if you’re open for something new, tap on the neighborhood option from the home screen and explore exclusive offers in other areas.

Freebird is committed to ending drunk driving. We’re partnering with prominent brands like Guinness to offer discounted rides home. Use Freebird at the end of the night to get cashback and peace of mind.

You can use Freebird to earn points taking rides anywhere across the country. In many cities, you will see selected bars and restaurants that offer additional cashback incentives. We are working to build relationships with more locations, so check the app daily as we're constantly adding new, exciting offers!

Members also get access to special promotions exclusive to Freebird, so download today for free to unlock a world of exciting deals that put money back in your pocket! Cash from your Freebird account can be transferred to your bank anytime, at no cost with no minimum amount.

Refreshing. Excellent. Always improving.

In a world where it seems every app, offer, coupon or reward comes with “fine print,” hidden costs, and misleading promises, or just flat-out doesn’t do what it purports—Freebird stands apart as an excellent, always improving, customer responsive, and well worth-your-while, cost-saving app, that is neither excessively intrusive nor exceedingly cumbersome to use.

It only adds about 5 seconds longer than normal to calling a Lyft or Uber, shows additional offers without ads or unnecessary pop-ups, and clearly shows you exactly what you’ve earned, and how many points you need to receive your next reward.

I call a moderate amount of vehicles—I’d say slightly more than an average person, but certainly not every day; and I make about $20 (or a little over) each month! That’s roughly $120 a year for using this app.

When you consider it: that’s 2-4 rides per month, I get for FREE, just by using this app. Over the course of a year, that’s 24 to 48 rides Firebird will pay for. That makes a difference.

I find the whole experience in a word: refreshing.

It’s hard not to be cynical in today’s world, where so many companies don’t tell the truth and don’t have the customers best interest at heart. Firebird gives me hope that more and more apps, business and user interfaces will choose to follow their example.
