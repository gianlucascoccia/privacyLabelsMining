Description

POPSHOP LIVE
Tune in to a whole new way to sell and shop:
- Sellers can host their own pop-up, live-streaming show and sell directly to an audience.
- Shoppers can find and follow a range of boutique stores, tune in to watch live shows, meet the sellers, and shop for unique products.
SHOP
- Find stores that go live weekly, featuring clothing for men and women, vintage and cutting-edge fashion, streetwear, shoes, accessories, jewelry, gadgets, home goods, wellness, beauty, athleisure, art, books, collectibles, toys, beauty, music, and so much more.
- Enjoy seamless and secure purchases live, an easy two-click payment, and swift shipping.
- Follow stores by creators, influencers, and sellers from all around the world.
- Add upcoming shows to your watchlist and tune in to fun live-streaming shopping shows – new sellers and shows are added on a daily basis.
- Discover awesome products, see them in action before you buy, ask questions with hosts and chat with other viewers live.
- Leave reviews and read other customers'.
SELL & STREAM
- Create your store in an easy-to-use setup, curate your product inventory, and start selling fast!
- Schedule a live-streaming show in advance, and host one anytime, anywhere, using your phone.
- Showcase products and have a conversation with customers live: answer questions, host giveaways, or invite a guest to co-host with you.
- Add new product listings on the fly, without stopping your stream.
- We take care of the payment and shipping details.
- Shopify Integration.

ericdbright , 01/20/2021

My New Virtual Hangout!

I signed up for PSL for DesignerCon 2020 and was so impressed with the service that I decided to stick around for several of the vendors. There’s really something for just about everyone! The app is remarkably easy to use, probably too easy based on my purchase history. It gets a little competitive, and I’d imagine that’s part of the attraction to this app. The other part is the sense of community that has come together. I’ve become friends with some of these people that I’d have otherwise never met or interacted with, and that’s the coolest part of PSL to me.

As for shopping, I stick to the art/toys shows mostly, like 3DRetro (Ben and the gang have AMAZING stuff!), Depressed Monsters (what can I say about Ryan and the work he does for mental health awareness!?), Scott Tolleson (my favorite designer), Eric with Giant Robot, and so many others; but I also check in on some of the other shows like Elevated Weirdo. It’s an eclectic group of personalities, it overall has been a great experience as a social platform and as a purchasing platform. Although I wish my wifi was a little faster sometime haha.

kara1234585 , 01/25/2021

Wasted my time

The team directly reached out to me to sell my products on the app. They mentioned all of the benefits for sellers and I was super excited to host my first live show. I did a video call with them and spent time emailing them and asking questions. I also spent a lot of time making the commercial and posted it on Popshop Live as well as my Instagram business page. I was confused why my commercial wasn’t getting any engagement on the app. Eventually the product manager emails me and says “great! You have a few new people following your store on PopShop Live! You’ll need to get 30 people to join the app before it can go live on the Popshop Live.” This floored me. I spent hours talking to them, preparing a commercial, and promoting this ridiculous app for them to tell me that in order for me to even reach buyers on the app, I have to convince 30 of my own followers to download it and join first. Seriously? I wouldn’t have been as disappointed if they were transparent about this from the beginning. But they told be just days before my show? Absolutely stupid. I deleted the app after that and decided this wasn’t for me. I’d rather host live shows on Instagram and not bombard my customers with commercials and pressure them to download this app in order for me to even be displayed on their main page. I want all of my wasted time and energy back. Be more transparent with your sellers next time. Bye.
