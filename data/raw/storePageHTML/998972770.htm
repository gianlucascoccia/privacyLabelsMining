Description

Jamzone is backing tracks, elevated. Browse thousands of studio multitracks, loop over built-in sections, change the volume of each instrument... And much more!

Jamzone transforms your Apple device into a live rehearsal studio! Choose your favorite song to jam along to from hundreds of the most popular.

** Multitrack mixer **

Change the volume of each instrument or play in Solo Mode to hear only the instrument you want to learn.

** One Tap Looping **

Instantly loop on one or many sections of your choice. Just want to work on the solo at the end? Or the chorus lick? Easy! In just one tap, turn on the loop mode on the section of your choice and start playing!

** Customized Pitch & Tempo **

Nothing is more frustrating than a track that goes too quickly or is in a difficult key. Make it easier with a track that adapts to YOU: thanks to customized pitch and metronome, say goodbye to G#add4!

** Live Chords & Lyrics **

Jamzone is reinventing the campfire songbook: the music scrolls in sync with the chords and lyrics, perfect for musicians, singers, or both at once!

** Hundreds of tracks **

Explore the Store and add to your collection! Hundreds of multitrack songs, recorded in studio, and ready to use.

** Inter-App Audio Ready **

Jamzone works beautifully with Inter-App Audio, allowing you to use any digital pedalboard or audio app along Jamzone.

Zinc7 , 04/18/2020

Some good & some bad

The good: tracks are pretty high quality, and some have different styled versions, which is good. Also, you only have to pay for tracks you want, and you can preview portions first.

The bad: jazz coverage is weak. A few classics, like “All Of Me” we’re represented multiple times but a lot of the standards were missing. Also browsing for just the Jazz items was difficult, there is a Big Band playlist, but that had a lot of rock stuff in it. And Big Band is not the whole of Jazz by any means. Other than searching by name I couldn’t see any other way to find stuff, as the Jazz tunes seem to be buried, what’s there anyway, making it needle in a haystack time.

But it has a few gems, “In The Mood” Andrews Sisters style allows you to mute each of the three vocalists individually, and most of the instrumentals are pretty nice sounding, seems a cut above the Midi-player style of backing. For me it’s probably useful for a couple of tunes, and that’s about it for now. Maybe they’ll add more later.
